UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1899
1 A JOINT RESOLUTION designating the Stumbo Family Memorial Highway in
2 Floyd County.
3 WHEREAS, the Stumbo Family has a long history of service and dedication to the
4 people of Floyd County and Eastern Kentucky; and
5 WHEREAS, the legacy of public service for the Stumbo Family began as far back
6 as the early twentieth century, when William M. "Buck" Stumbo, Jr., served as Floyd
7 County sheriff from 1905 to 1910; and
8 WHEREAS, several members of the Stumbo Family went on to hold the position of
9 Floyd County Sheriff, including Taylor Stumbo, from 1934 until 1938; Dr. W.L. "Walk"
10 Stumbo, who was elected in 1942 and the held the position until his death that same year;
11 and his wife, Annie Elliot Stumbo, who took over the office in 1942, becoming the first-
12 ever female sheriff in the Commonwealth; and
13 WHEREAS, the Stumbo Family has served in various positions in the Floyd
14 County court system, including Dr. W.L. "Walk" Stumbo as the Floyd County Judge,
15 Henry Stumbo as a magistrate and Floyd County Judge for over 30 years, John M.
16 Stumbo as the Floyd County Judge/Executive, Larry Foster Stumbo as a magistrate, and
17 Harold Stumbo as the Commonwealth's Attorney, Floyd County Attorney, and District
18 and Circuit Court Judge for over 30 years; and
19 WHEREAS, the Stumbo Family has a history of dedicated politicians faithfully
20 serving the people of Floyd County, including John Milton Stumbo, who served in the
21 House of Representatives in the Kentucky General Assembly starting in 1948; and
22 WHEREAS, various members of the Stumbo Family have dedicated their lives and
23 careers to the citizens of Floyd County and Eastern Kentucky, and their steadfast
24 commitment should be memorialized in some tangible way;
25 NOW, THEREFORE,
26 Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
