UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1203
1 AN ACT relating to animal testing.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 258 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 As used in Sections 1 to 3 of this Act:
6 (1) "Animal" means any warm-blooded living creature except a human being;
7 (2) "Animal shelter" has the same meaning as in KRS 258.095;
8 (3) "Animal testing facility" means a privately or publicly owned, leased, or operated
9 partnership, corporation, association, school, institution, or organization in
10 Kentucky that uses animals for experimentation or research or that tests chemical
11 substances, ingredients, drugs, medical devices, vaccines, products, or product
12 formulations on animals; and
13 (4) "Retired dog or cat" means a dog or cat:
14 (a) Confined at an animal testing facility;
15 (b) No longer required for testing, experimentation, or research purposes; and
16 (c) Determined by the animal testing facility's attending veterinarian to be
17 suitable for adoption.
18 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 258 IS CREATED TO
19 READ AS FOLLOWS:
20 (1) An animal testing facility, unless euthanizing the retired dog or cat is required for
21 health or safety reasons:
22 (a) Shall offer a retired dog or cat to an animal shelter to facilitate the transfer
23 of ownership and custody of the retired dog or cat to a person for placement
24 into the person's permanent home; or
25 (b) Shall transfer ownership and custody of the retired dog or cat to a person
26 for permanent placement in the person's home according to an
27 arrangement agreed upon by the animal testing facility and the person

11 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 258 IS CREATED TO
12 READ AS FOLLOWS:
13 (1) An animal testing facility:
14 (a) Shall not use state funds to perform research or testing on animals that
15 results in pain and distress that is classified as Category D or E by the
16 United States Department of Agriculture under the federal Animal Welfare
17 Act, 7 U.S.C. sec. 2131 et seq., and 9 C.F.R. sec. 230 et seq., as amended;
18 and
19 (b) May use state funds for:
20 1. The construction or maintenance of a research facility;
21 2. The purchase or maintenance of general-use equipment;
22 3. Overhead costs;
23 4. Capital improvements;
24 5. Faculty or employee salaries;
25 6. Administering a form of recognized veterinary treatment, including
26 services such as spaying and neutering, the purposes of which are to
27 improve the health, welfare, or safety of the animal; or

6 (2) On or before January 31, 2027, and each January 31 thereafter, each animal
7 testing facility that receives state funds shall post on its publicly accessible
8 internet website a written report for the preceding calendar year. The report shall
9 be posted in a conspicuous and easily accessible manner so that the public can
10 access a copy of the report electronically. The written report shall include the
11 following:
12 (a) The total number of animals, by species, that were held or used for research
13 or testing by the animal facility;
14 (b) A list of active animal research or testing projects, including project titles,
15 the stated purpose of each project, the species, and number of animals used
16 by each project;
17 (c) The total amount of funding spent on animal research or testing projects,
18 including federal, state, private, and other funding;
19 (d) A description of any programs used to transfer the ownership and custody
20 of retired dogs or cats following the end of their use in research, including
21 the total number of retired dogs or cats, by breed, released for adoption; and
22 (e) A detailed explanation of specific efforts by the institution to refine, reduce,
23 and replace the use of animals in research and testing during the preceding
24 calendar year.
25 (3) This section shall not apply to research or testing on livestock or poultry as
26 defined in KRS 246.010.
