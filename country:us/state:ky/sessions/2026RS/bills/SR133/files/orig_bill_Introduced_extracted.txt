1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of Charles
2 "Charlie" Ray Edmonds.
3 WHEREAS, Charles "Charlie" Ray Edmonds was born on February 11, 1938, in
4 Burna, Kentucky, to the late Ottie and Raylene Edmonds; and
5 WHEREAS, Charlie Ray Edmonds centered his life on family, hard work, faith,
6 and service to his community; and
7 WHEREAS, Charlie Ray Edmonds began his career as a bread deliveryman, where
8 he met the love of life, Wilma Edmonds, sharing 56 years of marriage filled with love,
9 laughter, and family; and
10 WHEREAS, through his dedication and entrepreneurial spirit, Charlie Ray
11 Edmonds opened and operated the local restaurant many remember as Burger Queen,
12 which later became Druther's and then Dairy Queen. In addition, Charlie Ray Edmonds
13 partnered in other business ventures, including Calvert City Shell convenience store and
14 Willow Pond in Eddyville; and
15 WHEREAS, Charlie Ray Edmonds faithfully served on the Benton City Council for
16 over 25 years, helping guide and support the growth of the city; and
17 WHEREAS, in addition to his public service, Charlie Ray Edmonds was a longtime
18 member of Christian Fellowship Church, enjoying his time serving as a greeter. In his
19 spare time, he enjoyed woodworking and cheering on Marshall County sports, the St.
20 Louis Cardinals, and the Kentucky Wildcats; and
21 WHEREAS, on February 11, 2026, Charlie Ray Edmonds passed away at home
22 surrounded by loved ones. He is survived by his beloved wife, Wilma Edmonds;
23 daughters, Alison Clark and Jennifer (Tommy) Jezik; son, Carter (Brenda) Edmonds;
24 grandchildren, Garrett Clark, Presley (Tyler Powell) Jezik, Neyland Jezik, Cole (Shelby)
25 Edmonds, and Cortny Edmonds; great-grandchildren, Chesney Edmonds and Cohen
26 Edmonds; and several nieces and nephews; and
27 WHEREAS, Charlie Ray Edmonds will be remembered for his strong work ethic,
