Section 1:
CHAPTER 58 1
CHAPTER 58
( HB 781 )
WHEREAS, high workforce participation is directly tied to a healthy and nourished populace; and
WHEREAS, maximizing the value of Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits spent on
healthy and nourishing food options makes the preferred healthy choice also the most economical choice and
ultimately increases the overall health of Kentuckians; and
WHEREAS, SNAP Employment and Training programs are available to eligible SNAP beneficiaries in all
120 Kentucky counties; and
WHEREAS, the federal government provides 100 percent matching grants for qualified private partner-funded
SNAP Employment and Training programs; and
WHEREAS, the General Assembly recognizes an opportunity, through the Commonwealth's SNAP
Employment and Training state plan, for public-private partnerships across the Commonwealth to provide both
employment training and nutrition incentives; and
WHEREAS, the General Assembly is committed to bringing potential partners to the table through a working
group of public and private entities across the Commonwealth to explore all options for increasing workforce
engagement as well as healthy incentive programs under the SNAP Employment and Training state plan;
NOW, THEREFORE,
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
Section 1. KRS 205.2003 is amended to read as follows:
(1) The secretary shall promulgate[by] administrative regulations in accordance with[subject to the provisions of]
KRS Chapter 13A to develop a work program for recipients of public assistance under Title IV-A of the
Federal Social Security Act to provide for immediate employment or preparation for employment.
(2) The self-sufficiency of participants shall be the primary goal of the work program.[ ] Participants shall bear the
ultimate responsibility in pursuit of this goal.
(3) In partnership with participants, the cabinet shall assess their strengths and needs and provide supportive
services to assist in the pursuit of work and self-sufficiency.
(4) The cabinet shall:
(a) Develop and implement initiatives under Kentucky's Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program
Employment and Training state plan, established in accordance with 7 C.F.R. sec. 273.7, to promote
improved employment outcomes and workforce participation. The initiatives shall:

Section 2:
1. Be related to healthy workforce habits among eligible individuals;

Section 3:
2. Include establishing partnerships with public and private entities, including but not limited to
workforce boards, educational institutions, employers, and community organizations;

Section 4:
3. Include partner-funded programs supporting household food security and improved nutrition,
including but not limited to an incentive program for the purpose of increasing the purchase
and consumption of nutritious foods both at work and at home;

Section 5:
4. Include job readiness, occupational training, and work-based learning opportunities;

Section 6:
5. Implement employment assessment and individualized employment planning; and

Section 7:
6. Include case management and employment navigation services;
(b) To the extent allowable by federal law, utilize federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program
matching grant funds as provided in 7 C.F.R. sec. 273.7(d);
Legislative Research Commission PDF Version
2 ACTS OF THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY
(c) On or before December 1, 2026, pursue any federal approvals necessary to implement the initiatives,
including a submission of a state plan amendment to Kentucky's federally approved Supplemental
Nutrition Assistance Program Employment and Training state plan; and
(d) Submit a report to the Legislative Research Commission for referral to the Interim Joint Committee
on Health Services and appear before the Interim Joint Committee on Health Services annually to
update the members of the committee regarding program participation levels, employment outcomes,
healthy workforce outcomes, federal fund draw downs, and program impacts.
Signed by Governor April 8, 2026.

AN ACT relating to the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program Employment and Training state plan.
WHEREAS, high workforce participation is directly tied to a healthy and nourished populace; and
