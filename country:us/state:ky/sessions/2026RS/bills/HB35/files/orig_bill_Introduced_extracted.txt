UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1489
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 164.786 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) For purposes of this section:
5 (a) "Academic term" means the fall or spring academic semester;
6 (b) "Academic year" means July 1 through June 30 of each year;
7 (c) "Approved dual credit course" means a dual credit course developed in
8 accordance with KRS 164.098 and shall include:
9 1. General education courses that meet the criteria outlined in the statewide
10 general education core approved by the Council on Postsecondary
11 Education; and
12 2. Career and technical education courses within a career pathway
13 approved by the Kentucky Department of Education that leads to an
14 industry-recognized credential;
15 (d) "Authority" means the Kentucky Higher Education Assistance Authority;
16 (e) "Dual credit" has the same meaning as in KRS 158.007;
17 (f) "Dual credit tuition rate ceiling" means one-third (1/3) of the per credit hour
18 tuition amount, rounded down to the nearest whole dollar, charged by the
19 Kentucky Community and Technical College System for in-state students;
20 (g) "Eligible high school student" means a student who:
21 1. Is a Kentucky resident;
22 2. Is enrolled in a Kentucky high school;
23 3. Has completed a thirty (30) minute college success counseling session;
24 and
25 4. Is enrolled, or accepted for enrollment, in an approved dual credit course
26 at a participating institution;
27 (h) "Participating institution" means a postsecondary institution that:

1 1. Has an agreement with the authority for the administration of the Dual
2 Credit Scholarship Program;
3 2. Charges no more than the dual credit tuition rate ceiling per credit hour,
4 including any additional fees, for any approved dual credit course it
5 offers to any Kentucky public or nonpublic high school student;
6 3. Does not charge any tuition or fees to an eligible high school student for
7 an approved dual credit course beyond what is paid by the Dual Credit
8 Scholarship Program when the course is not successfully completed; and
9 4. Is a:
10 a. Kentucky Community and Technical College System institution;
11 b. Four (4) year Kentucky public college or university; [or]
12 c. Four (4) year private college or university that is accredited by the
13 Southern Association of Colleges and Schools and whose main
14 campus is located in Kentucky; or
15 d. Proprietary school as defined in KRS 164.7884 that offers an
16 eligible program of study as defined in Section 2 of this Act; and
17 (i) "Successfully completed" means a student receiving both secondary and
18 postsecondary credit upon completion of an approved dual credit course.
19 (2) To promote dual credit coursework opportunities at no cost to eligible Kentucky
20 high school students, the General Assembly hereby establishes the Dual Credit
21 Scholarship Program.
22 (3) In consultation with the Council on Postsecondary Education and the Kentucky
23 Department of Education, the authority shall administer the Dual Credit Scholarship
24 Program and shall promulgate administrative regulations in accordance with KRS
25 Chapter 13A as may be needed for the administration of the program.
26 (4) (a) Each high school shall apply to the authority for dual credit scholarship funds
27 for each eligible high school student.

2 student for an academic term on a first-come, first-served basis by application
3 date, to the extent funds are available for that purpose.
4 (c) An eligible high school student may receive a dual credit scholarship for a
5 maximum of:
6 1. Two (2) career and technical education dual credit courses per academic
7 year; and
8 2. Two (2) general education dual credit courses over the junior and senior
9 year.

22 Dual Credit Scholarship Program; and
23 (b) The number of general education and career and technical education dual
24 credits earned by students by high school and in total.

26 the authority, shall publish data on the Dual Credit Scholarship Program's academic

3 fund in the State Treasury to be administered by the Kentucky Higher
4 Education Assistance Authority for the purpose of providing scholarships
5 described in this section.
6 (b) The trust fund shall consist of state general fund appropriations, gifts and
7 grants from public and private sources, and federal funds.

13 Section 2. KRS 164.787 is amended to read as follows:
14 (1) The General Assembly hereby establishes the Work Ready Kentucky Scholarship
15 Program to ensure that all Kentuckians who have not yet earned a postsecondary
16 degree have affordable access to an industry-recognized certificate, diploma, or
17 associate of applied science degree and, for students with intellectual disabilities
18 enrolled in comprehensive transition and postsecondary programs, affordable
19 access to meaningful credentials to prepare for competitive integrated employment.
20 (2) For purposes of this section:
21 (a) "Academic term" means a fall, spring, or summer academic term or other time
22 period specified in an administrative regulation promulgated by the authority;
23 (b) "Academic year" means July 1 through June 30 of each year;
24 (c) "Eligible institution" means:
25 1. An institution as defined in KRS 164.001 that:
26 a.[1.] Actively participates in the federal Pell Grant program;
27 b.[2.] Executes a contract with the authority on terms the authority

5 institution;

7 iii.[c.] College, university, or vocational-technical school that
8 is accredited by a recognized regional or national accrediting
9 body and licensed to operate at a site in Kentucky;
10 or
11 2. A proprietary school as defined in KRS 164.7884 that offers an
12 eligible program of study;
13 (d) "Eligible program of study" means a program approved by the authority that
14 leads to an industry-recognized certificate, diploma, or associate of applied
15 science degree in one (1) of Kentucky's top five (5) high-demand workforce
16 sectors identified by the Kentucky Workforce Innovation Board and the
17 Education and Labor Cabinet or a program of study in a comprehensive
18 transition and postsecondary program that leads to a credential, certificate,
19 diploma, or degree;
20 (e) "Fees" means mandatory fees charged by an eligible institution for enrollment
21 in a course, including but not limited to online course fees, lab fees, and
22 administrative fees. "Fees" does not include tools, books, or other
23 instructional materials that may be required for a course; and
24 (f) "Tuition" means the in-state tuition charged to all students as a condition of
25 enrollment in an eligible institution.

21 authority;
22 (g) Enroll in an eligible program of study at an eligible institution;
23 (h) Not be enrolled in an ineligible degree program, such as a bachelor or
24 unapproved associate program, at any postsecondary institution;
25 (i) Following the first academic term scholarship funds are received, achieve and
26 maintain satisfactory academic progress as determined by the eligible
