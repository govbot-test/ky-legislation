1 A RESOLUTION recognizing January 2026 as Kentucky Mentorship Month.
2 WHEREAS, the goals of Kentucky mentorship month are to raise awareness of and
3 celebrate the powerful impact of mentoring relationships, recruit new mentors, and
4 encourage institutions to integrate quality mentoring into their policies, practices, and
5 programs; and
6 WHEREAS, quality mentoring fosters positive life and social skills, promotes self-
7 esteem, bolsters academic achievement and college access, supports career exploration,
8 and nurtures youth leadership development; and
9 WHEREAS, mentoring happens in many settings, including community-based
10 programs, elementary and secondary schools, colleges, government agencies, religious
11 institutions, and the workplace; and
12 WHEREAS, mentoring happens in various ways, including formal mentoring
13 matches and informal relationships with teachers, coaches, neighbors, faith leaders, and
14 others; and
15 WHEREAS, mentoring encourages positive youth development and smart daily
16 behaviors; and
17 WHEREAS, mentors help young people set career goals and can help connect
18 mentees to industry professionals to train for and find jobs; and
19 WHEREAS, research has found that young people who had a mentor were, when
20 compared with their peers, less likely to start using drugs; and
21 WHEREAS, mentoring relationships can have a positive impact on mental health,
22 educational peer relationships, employment, and housing stability; and
23 WHEREAS, mentoring is both a prevention and intervention strategy that can
24 support young people in all aspects of their lives; and
25 WHEREAS, the benefits of mentors serve to link youth to economic and social
26 opportunity while also strengthening communities in the Commonwealth; and
27 WHEREAS, despite the benefits of mentoring, many young people grow up
