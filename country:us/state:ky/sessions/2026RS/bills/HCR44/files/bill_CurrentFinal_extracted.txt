1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION urging Congress to create an accreditation
2 pathway through the United States Department of Veterans Affairs for private companies
3 assisting veterans with their benefits.
4 WHEREAS, the men and women who have served our great Commonwealth and
5 nation deserve the benefits they have painstakingly earned through their time in uniform;
6 and
7 WHEREAS, the veterans of this Commonwealth have earned the right to make
8 their own decisions, especially when it comes to navigating the complex disability claims
9 process through the United States Department of Veterans Affairs; and
10 WHEREAS, this nation's veterans have routinely not received their full benefits
11 because the system designed to support them falls short; the process is often lengthy,
12 cumbersome, and inconsistent; and
13 WHEREAS, veterans often turn to private companies whose mission is to assist
14 veterans with their benefits claims by tackling the red tape and procuring medical
15 documentation and other necessary paperwork on behalf of the veterans using these
16 private companies; and
17 WHEREAS, these companies are often prohibited from fully helping veterans
18 because of the lack of clear guidance from Congress and the patchwork of states' laws
19 meant to deter the bad actors from harming veterans instead often hamstrings the good
20 actors; and
21 WHEREAS, currently there is not a commonsense pathway to accreditation through
22 the United States Department of Veterans Affairs for private companies to receive
23 accreditation; and
24 WHEREAS, Congress should act swiftly to pass the CHOICE for Veterans Act
25 (H.R. 3132) which creates a pathway for accreditation for private companies that aid
26 veterans in procuring their earned benefits;
27 NOW, THEREFORE,
