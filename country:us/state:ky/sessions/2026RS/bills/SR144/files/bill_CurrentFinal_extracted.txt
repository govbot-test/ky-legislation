1 A RESOLUTION designating March 31, 2026, as Kentucky Translational Science
2 Day in the Commonwealth.
3 WHEREAS, part of the mission of the University of Kentucky is to advance the
4 health of the state it serves, fulfilling a unique mandate to improve quality of life for all
5 Kentuckians; and
6 WHEREAS, the Commonwealth’s flagship university is home to a wealth of
7 expertise, anchored by 16 colleges on one contiguous campus, allowing the university to
8 develop multidisciplinary solutions to address the most pressing and complex health
9 challenges in the state; and
10 WHEREAS, the University of Kentucky demonstrates a strong commitment to
11 translational science, a field of study that aims to convert scientific discoveries in
12 laboratories into practical interventions that improve the health of communities
13 worldwide; and
14 WHEREAS, research conducted at the University of Kentucky attracts hundreds of
15 millions of dollars in federal and industry funding annually to address some of the
16 nation's most critical health crises, including Alzheimer's disease and related dementias,
17 cancer, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and the unique health challenges facing Central
18 Appalachia; and
19 WHEREAS, the University of Kentucky Center for Clinical and Translational
20 Science (CCTS) serves as the essential engine that ensures that research evidence is
21 shared and implemented to benefit the public in clinical practice, prevention initiatives,
22 and community health systems; and
23 WHEREAS, by pioneering innovative methods, such as artificial intelligence-
24 supported trend prediction and universal biospecimen banking, the University of
25 Kentucky CCTS promotes efficiency in research and removes operational barriers to
26 discovery; and
27 WHEREAS, the University of Kentucky CCTS invests in the people behind the
