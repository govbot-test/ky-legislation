2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 39A IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) The position of chief resiliency officer is hereby established to lead efforts to
6 enhance the Commonwealth's ability to prepare for natural disasters and
7 emergencies. The chief resiliency officer shall be appointed by and serve at the
8 pleasure of the Governor and operate in consultation with the director of the
9 Division of Emergency Management.
10 (2) The chief resiliency officer shall:
11 (a) Serve as a central coordinator for preparedness, resilience, and mitigation
12 efforts across the Commonwealth;
13 (b) Develop and maintain, in conjunction with the Division of Emergency
14 Management, a statewide resiliency plan to reduce long-term disaster risks,
15 strengthen infrastructure, and promote cost-effective emergency
16 preparedness investments;
17 (c) Coordinate across executive branch agencies and with local governments,
18 regional entities, the private sector, and academic and nonprofit partners;
19 (d) Strengthen Kentucky's emergency threat preparedness and resilience,
20 including but not limited to tornadoes, floods, earthquakes, ice storms,
21 wildfires, biohazard and hazardous materials incidents, radioactive
22 contamination, public health emergencies and disease outbreaks, power-
23 grid failures, and cyberattacks;
24 (e) Support disaster preparedness and response grant funding efforts and
25 applications for local government and other disaster preparedness and
26 response organizations;
27 (f) Research, review, and adopt emerging best practices in disaster prevention,

1 mitigation, and resilience; and
2 (g) Employ staff as necessary to fulfill the responsibilities and duties set forth
3 in this section.

23 Operations Plan, and[ to] review and give concurrence to local emergency
