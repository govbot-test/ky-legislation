UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1758
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 164.5161 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) As used in this section:
5 (a) "Education[Educational] institution" means an in-state public or private
6 postsecondary education[educational] institution; and
7 (b) "Spouse" means an individual who is married to a member of the Kentucky
8 National Guard and verified in the Defense Enrollment Eligibility
9 Reporting System database.
10 (2) (a) The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky establishes the
11 National Guard Tuition Scholarship[Award] Program to provide members of
12 the Kentucky National Guard and their spouses the opportunity to attend an
13 in-state education[educational] institution.
14 (b) The amount of the scholarship awarded[tuition paid] shall be up to or equal
15 to the in-state full or part-time tuition rate and mandatory student fees[rate]
16 of the education institution the member or his or her spouse attends.
17 (c) The amount of the scholarship awarded[except that tuition paid] in support
18 of a member or his or her spouse attending a private postsecondary
19 educational institution shall be up to or equal to the average in-state full or
20 part-time tuition rate and fees of a public university or community college.
21 Until the appropriation for the tuition scholarship[assistance] program of the
22 Department of Military Affairs is first fully funded by the General Assembly,
23 a scholarship[tuition for a member] to attend a private institution under this
24 section shall not be awarded[paid].
25 (d) The tuition scholarship[award] program shall be established in the Kentucky
26 Higher Education Assistance Authority, and moneys shall be available for all
27 education institution programs that lead to a postsecondary credential,

3 (3) (a) Each member of the Kentucky National Guard and the member's spouse
4 shall collectively be entitled to a scholarship award for not more than one
5 hundred thirty (130) hours of tuition under the tuition scholarship program.
6 (b) The total number of credit hours available for the member and the
7 member's spouse may be transferred between the member and his or her
8 spouse and used concurrently until depleted.

10 not use the National Guard Scholarship Program concurrently with the
11 Work Ready Kentucky Scholarship Program established in KRS 164.787.

14 described in subsection (3) of this section at any postsecondary institution
15 for an area of study that will have material benefits to the operations of the
16 National Guard.
17 (b) The additional hours awarded under this subsection shall not be
18 transferable.

21 management of the tuition scholarship[award] program.

2 Section 2. KRS 164.5165 is amended to read as follows:
3 Any active member of the Kentucky National Guard who is not a Kentucky resident shall
4 qualify for the tuition scholarship program[award] benefits provided by KRS 164.516 to
5 164.5169 subject to the following conditions:
6 (1) The member enrolls in an education[educational] institution within this state; and
7 (2) The tuition scholarship program[award] benefits provided to the out-of-state
8 member shall not exceed an amount equivalent to the in-state tuition amount the
9 member would have received if the member were a Kentucky resident.
10 Section 3. KRS 164.5169 is amended to read as follows:
11 Upon certification by the Department of Military Affairs that the member is eligible, the
12 Kentucky Higher Education Assistance Authority shall, to the extent that funds have been
13 appropriated and are available to the National Guard Tuition Scholarship[Award]
14 Program, pay to the education[educational] institution an amount up to or equal to the in-
15 state tuition costs of full-time or part-time study.
