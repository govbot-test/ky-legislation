1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of Levi
2 Peterson, Jr.
3 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., was born on December 13, 1933, in Birmingham,
4 Alabama, to the late Levi Peterson, Sr., and Lelia Bell McCurdy Peterson; and
5 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., professed his faith in God at a young age and was
6 later baptized into the Catholic faith; and
7 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., was mentored by a priest he highly respected,
8 Father Michael Caswell, who provided wisdom and guidance during a very turbulent
9 period in his life; and
10 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., served as an educator in various positions and
11 schools for 34 years until he finally retired in 1994; and
12 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., had a strong impact on his community, being
13 described as "a trailblazer, public servant leader in education, opening doors for future
14 generations of students and educators"; and
15 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., passed peacefully from this life on February 21,
16 2026; and
17 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., was preceded in death by his devoted wife
18 Margaret; eldest son Levi III; and sisters Sheila Mahand and Sister Mary of the Trinity,
19 OP; and
20 WHEREAS, Levi Peterson, Jr., is survived by his son, Herschel Peterson;
21 granddaughters, Margaret Peterson and Savannah (Mantayvius) Peterson-Richards; great-
22 grandson, Jamari Richards; sister, Geraldine Weathers; nieces, Carmen (Kevin)
23 Weathers-Washington, Charmein Weathers, Camille Woods, and Dr. Charita Weathers;
24 nephew, Thalmus (Joi) Mahand; surrogate sons, Eric (Joan) Coleman, Mark Leavell,
25 Lynn Radford, Garland Collins, and Johnny Burse; surrogate niece, Francene Gilmer; and
26 a host of other cherished family and dear friends;
27 NOW, THEREFORE,
