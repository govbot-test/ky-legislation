2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 157.420 is amended to read as follows:
4 Public school funds made available to the credit of each district during any year shall be
5 received, held, and expended by the district board, subject to the provisions of law and
6 administrative regulations of the Kentucky Board of Education. The following
7 restrictions shall govern the expenditure of funds from the public school fund:
8 (1) The salary paid any rank of teachers shall be at least equivalent to the amount set
9 forth in the biennial budget schedule for each rank and experience for a term of one
10 hundred eighty-five (185) days for full-time service during the regular school year.
11 (2) (a) Beginning with the 2026-2028[2004-2006] biennium, the Kentucky Board of
12 Education shall not approve any working budget or salary schedule for local
13 boards of education for any school year unless:
14 1. The one hundred eighty-five (185) day salary schedule for certified staff
15 has been adjusted over the previous year's salary schedule to
16 incorporate the percent increase in the nonseasonally adjusted annual
17 average Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U),
18 U.S. City Average, All Items, between the two (2)_ most recent
19 calendar years available, as published by the United States Bureau of
20 Labor Statistics[by a percentage increase at least equal to the cost-of-
21 living adjustment that is provided state government workers under the
22 biennial budget]; and
23 2. The compensation schedule for classified staff has been adjusted over
24 the previous year's compensation schedule to incorporate the percent
25 increase in the nonseasonally adjusted annual average Consumer
26 Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U), U.S. City Average, All
27 Items, between the two (2) most recent calendar years available, as

3 (8) In surveying the schools, the Department of Education shall designate each school
4 facility as a permanent, functional, or transitional center.
5 (a) "Permanent center" means a center which meets the program standards
6 approved by the Kentucky Board of Education, is located so that students are
7 not subjected to an excessive amount of time being transported to the site, and
8 has established an attendance area which will maintain enrollment at capacity
9 but will also avoid overcrowding.
10 (b) "Functional center" means a center which does not meet all the criteria
11 established for a permanent facility, but is adequate to meet accreditation
12 program standards to insure no substantial academic or building deficiency.
13 The facility plan shall include additions and renovations necessary to meet
14 current accreditation standards for which federal, state, and local funds may
15 be used.
16 (c) "Transitional center" means a center which the local board of education has
17 determined shall no longer be designated permanent or functional. The center
18 shall be destined to be closed and shall not be eligible for new construction,
19 additions, or major renovation. However, the board of education shall
20 maintain any operating transitional center to provide a safe and healthy
21 environment for students.
22 (9) Beginning in fiscal year 2011-2012, the Kentucky Department of Education shall
23 standardize the process for evaluating the overall quality and condition of all school
24 buildings across the state. The evaluation process shall:
