1 AN ACT relating to welcome centers and rest areas.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 177 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section:
6 (a) "Acknowledgment sign" means an official sign that:
7 1. Meets all design and placement guidelines for acknowledgment signs
8 as established in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices for
9 Streets and Highways as published by the United States Department of
10 Transportation;
11 2. Meets all sign design rules covered in the Standard Highway Signs
12 publication adopted by the United States Department of
13 Transportation;
14 3. Is located in the right-of-way; and
15 4. Is intended to inform the traveling public that a highway-related
16 service, product, or monetary contribution has been sponsored by a
17 private entity; and
18 (b) "Sponsorship agreement" means an agreement or contract between the
19 cabinet and a person or private entity, which is acknowledged for a
20 highway-related service, product, or monetary contribution provided.
21 (2) The cabinet may establish a sponsorship agreement that allows a person or
22 private entity to sponsor a welcome center or rest area. Any service, product, or
23 monetary contribution received by the cabinet shall be used for the maintenance
24 and upkeep of the welcome center or rest area.
25 (3) A person or private entity that enters into a sponsorship agreement with the
26 cabinet shall not be permitted to offer or make available commercial services or
27 products to the public at welcome centers or rest areas that they sponsor and

2 (a) Interstate highway system; or

4 (4) A sponsorship agreement shall allow the installation of acknowledgment signs

10 sponsorship agreements to install acknowledgment signs at welcome centers and
11 rest areas.

13 centers and rest areas shall:
14 (a) Be subject to approval by the Federal Highway Administration;
15 (b) Include requirements that eligible sponsors comply with state and federal
16 law prohibiting discrimination based on race, color, national origin, age,
17 sex, religion, disability, or any other federally protected class;
18 (c) Include language requiring the cabinet to terminate a sponsorship
19 agreement if it determines the sponsorship agreement or acknowledgment
20 sign:
21 1. Presents a safety concern;
22 2. Interferes with the free and safe flow of traffic; or
23 3. Is not in the public interest; and
24 (d) Describe the types of sponsors and sponsorship agreements that are
25 acceptable and consistent with applicable state and federal law.
26 (7) A person or private entity that enters into a sponsorship agreement with the
