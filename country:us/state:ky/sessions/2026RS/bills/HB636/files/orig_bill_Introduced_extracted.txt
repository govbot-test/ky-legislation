1 AN ACT relating to civil immunity for animal rescue.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 411.245 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) A person who enters a vehicle, as defined in KRS 503.010, for the purpose of
5 removing a minor or domestic animal shall be immune from civil liability for any
6 resulting damage to the vehicle if the person:
7 (a) Has a reasonable, good faith belief, based upon the circumstances known to
8 the person at the time, that entry into the vehicle is necessary because the
9 minor or domestic animal is in imminent danger of physical injury if not
10 immediately removed from the vehicle;
11 (b) Has contacted local law enforcement, the local fire department, or a 911
12 emergency telephone service prior to entering the vehicle;
13 (c) Uses no more force than is reasonably necessary under the circumstances to
14 enter the vehicle and remove the minor or domestic animal[ than is
15 reasonably necessary under the circumstances]; and
16 (d) 1. Remains with the minor or domestic animal in a safe location, out of the
17 elements but reasonably close to the vehicle, until law enforcement,
18 firefighters, or other emergency responders arrive; or
19 2. Reasonably determines that emergency conditions require leaving the
20 scene with the minor or domestic animal, and places written notice on
21 the vehicle containing:
22 a. The person's contact information;
23 b. The reason for entry into the vehicle[ was made];
24 c. The[ minor's] location of the minor or domestic animal; and
25 d. Notice that authorities have been contacted.
