2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 214 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) The Department for Public Health shall make available to the public on its
6 website educational material on the recognition and warning signs of infantile
7 seizure disorder.
8 (2) The department may solicit and accept the assistance of relevant medical
9 associations or community resources to develop, promote, and distribute the
10 educational material.
11 (3) A health care provider who regularly provides care for infants shall offer to and
12 discuss with the families and caregivers of infants the information available
13 under subsection (1) of this section.
14 Section 2. KRS 311.601 is amended to read as follows:
15 (1) The board may adopt reasonable rules and regulations to effectuate and implement
16 the provisions of KRS 311.550 to 311.620, including but not limited to regulations
17 designed to ensure the continuing professional competency of present and future
18 licensees. As an adjunct to the power conferred upon the board by this section, the
19 board may require licensees to submit to interrogation as to the nature and extent of
20 their postgraduate medical education and to require licensees found to be deficient
21 in their efforts to keep abreast of new methods and technology, to obtain additional
22 instruction and training therein.
23 (2) As part of the continuing medical education which the board adopts to ensure
24 continuing professional competency of present and future licensees, the board shall
25 ensure that:
26 (a) Current practicing pediatricians, including those certified in medicine and
27 pediatrics, radiologists, family practitioners, and those physicians practicing

6 (b) Future practicing pediatricians, including those certified in medicine and

19 medicine and pediatrics, and family practitioners, demonstrate completion
20 of a one (1) time course of at least one (1) hour of continuing medical
21 education approved by the board and covering the recognition and warning
22 signs of infantile seizure disorder.
