1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of Joseph
2 Hardin McFarland.
3 WHEREAS, Joseph Hardin McFarland was born on January 30, 1931, in Bowling
4 Green, Kentucky, and traversed these earthly bounds to join his Father in Heaven on
5 January 14, 2026, at the age of 94 years; and
6 WHEREAS, Joseph Hardin McFarland was a 1949 graduate of Bowling Green
7 High School. He received his Bachelor of Business Administration from the University
8 of Nebraska Omaha and his Master of Hospital Administration from Baylor University;
9 and
10 WHEREAS, Joseph Hardin McFarland was married to his wife, Alicia Ruth
11 Rawlins McFarland, for 65 years and they had three daughters; and
12 WHEREAS, Joseph Hardin McFarland was in the United States Army for 28 years
13 where he served in Korea, Fort Benning, Fort Sam Houston, Vietnam, Germany, and the
14 Presidio of San Francisco; and
15 WHEREAS, Joseph Hardin McFarland retired from the 6th Army Medical
16 Command in 1976 as a Lieutenant Colonel and returned to Bowling Green; and
17 WHEREAS, upon his retirement from service, Joseph Hardin McFarland returned
18 to his beloved Presbyterian Church where he served as an elder and deacon as a devoted
19 follower of Jesus Christ. He joined the Noon Rotary Club where he was an active
20 member for 40 years. His mission of service continued outside of the church as he served
21 on the Salvation Army board of directors and was active in numerous organizations,
22 supporting the arts, Western Kentucky University, and the community's most vulnerable.
23 Joseph Hardin McFarland started the shoe and sock donation drive for Warren
24 Elementary School and enjoyed reading to the kindergarten and first grade classes
25 regularly; and
26 WHEREAS, Joseph Hardin McFarland was preceded in death by his dear wife. He
27 is survived by his three daughters, Jane McFarland Coverdale, Susan McFarland (Greg)
