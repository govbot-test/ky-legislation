1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor of Kentucky's rural electric
2 cooperative utility workers who provided vital support during the deadly tornado that
3 devastated Kentucky on May 16, 2025.
4 WHEREAS, on May 16, 2025, a deadly severe weather breakout spawned
5 devastating tornadoes causing significant damage in Kentucky, Missouri, Illinois, and
6 Arkansas, with Kentucky experiencing peak winds estimated at 179 miles per hour; and
7 WHEREAS, on that evening, an EF-4 tornado nearly a mile wide ripped through
8 parts of south central and eastern Kentucky along its 55.6-mile path, which severely
9 impacted Laurel, Pulaski, and Russell Counties; and
10 WHEREAS, this devastating storm left 19 Kentuckians dead, many others
11 hospitalized, and tens of thousands of homes and businesses without vital power due to
12 extensive damage to electric distribution and transmission infrastructure, including
13 downed poles, broken conductors, damaged substations, and destroyed transmission
14 structures; and
15 WHEREAS, this was the deadliest tornado to ever be reported in the region; and
16 WHEREAS, the storm directly hit South Kentucky Rural Electric Cooperative's
17 headquarters in Somerset, rendering its supervisory control and data acquisition system
18 and outage management system inoperable and heavily damaging its distribution system;
19 and
20 WHEREAS, in total, dozens of distribution substations and electric circuits were
21 severely impacted at the peak of the storm, while hundreds of power poles were broken
22 and many miles of power line were knocked down; and
23 WHEREAS, crews from electric cooperatives and mutual-aid partners immediately
24 mobilized, deploying skilled line and substation personnel throughout the region, and
25 organized into multiple crews who tirelessly worked around the clock, often in darkness,
26 severe weather, and debris-filled environments, putting public safety and community
27 recovery ahead of their own comfort and rest; and
