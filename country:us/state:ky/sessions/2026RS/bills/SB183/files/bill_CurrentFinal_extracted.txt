3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 367 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 As used in Sections 1 to 3 of this Act:
6 (1) "Company" means a publicly traded for-profit corporation, limited liability

9 (2) "Company proposal" means a proposal made by a company that is included in

11 nominations or elections, executive compensation, corporate transactions and

14 guidelines designed to assist with voting decisions on any company proposal or

17 shareholder-sponsored proposal, which shall include, at a minimum:

20 shareholder-sponsored proposal and the stated reasons for the opposition;
21 (c) Whether the shareholder-sponsored proposal is consistent with the
22 investment policy of the shareholder receiving the advice;
23 (d) The economic benefits and costs of implementing the shareholder-
24 sponsored proposal, as written, in the long and short term;
25 (e) The quantifiable impact of the shareholder-sponsored proposal, as written,
26 on the investment returns of the shareholder receiving the advice; and
27 (f) An explanation of the modeling, procedures, and processes used to complete

21 shareholders to other objectives, including sacrificing investment returns or
22 undertaking additional investment risk to promote or further nonpecuniary
23 interests;
24 (b) Involves providing a voting recommendation with respect to a shareholder-
25 sponsored proposal that:

13 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 367 IS CREATED TO
14 READ AS FOLLOWS:
15 (1) Failure to comply with the notification and disclosure requirements established
16 for proxy advisors in Sections 1 and 2 of this Act shall be deemed to be an unfair,
17 false, misleading, or deceptive act or practice in the conduct of trade or commerce
18 in violation of KRS 367.170.
19 (2) The recipient of proxy advisory services provided by a proxy advisor, the company
20 that is the subject of the proxy advisory services, or any shareholder of the
21 company that is the subject of the proxy advisory services may bring a cause of
22 action seeking declaratory or injunctive relief in the Circuit Court of the county
23 where the plaintiff resides or where the violation occurred.
24 (3) (a) In any proceeding under subsection (2) of this section, the plaintiff shall
25 serve the Attorney General with a copy of the petition within seven (7) days
26 of filing. The Attorney General may intervene in the action.
27 (b) All of the remedies, powers, and duties provided to the Attorney General
