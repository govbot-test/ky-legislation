1 AN ACT relating to natural resources.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 350.600 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) The General Assembly finds that[ there has been a] recent interest in[ the
5 Commonwealth in the] mining and processing of oil shale in the Commonwealth is
6 due to the critical need for fossil fuel[ which has developed in this country]. The
7 General Assembly further finds that the development of this fossil fuel could be of
8 significant long-range benefit to the Commonwealth and to the United States of
9 America; however,[ but that] the unregulated extraction and processing of oil shale
10 within the Commonwealth could cause soil erosion, water pollution, and the
11 destruction of aesthetic and agricultural values. The General Assembly further finds
12 that, in order to effectively regulate the extraction and processing of oil shale, the
13 Energy and Environment Cabinet shall conduct extensive research concerning the
14 process, waste production, and potentially adverse environmental impacts of such
15 recovery operations.
16 (2) It is the intent of the General Assembly to provide for the[ such] regulation and
17 control of[ the] mining activity and the processing of oil shale[ as] to minimize and
18 prevent its adverse effects on the citizens and the environment of the
19 Commonwealth. The General Assembly further finds that the Energy and
20 Environment Cabinet is the most competent agency to regulate the extraction and
21 processing of oil shale due to its expertise in the handling of the surface mining of
22 other minerals and in the regulation of other sources of air and water pollution.
23 (3) The Energy and Environment Cabinet shall file with the administrative regulations
24 compiler reasonable rules and administrative regulations in accordance with KRS
25 Chapter 13A pertaining to oil shale mining operations, which shall include strip
26 mining as defined by KRS 350.010, the surface effects of underground mining of
27 oil shale, and the in situ mining of oil shale by June 30, 1981. No person shall
