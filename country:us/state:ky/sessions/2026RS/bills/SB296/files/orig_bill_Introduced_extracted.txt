3 SECTION 1. KRS CHAPTER 426A IS ESTABLISHED AND A NEW
4 SECTION THEREOF IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 Sections 1 to 11 of this Act may be cited as the Uniform Registration of Canadian
6 Money Judgments Act.
7 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 426A IS CREATED TO
8 READ AS FOLLOWS:
9 As used in Sections 1 to 11 of this Act:
10 (1) "Canada" means the sovereign nation of Canada and its provinces and
11 territories. "Canadian" has a corresponding meaning; and
12 (2) "Canadian judgment" means a judgment of a court of Canada, other than a
13 judgment that recognized the judgment of another foreign country.
14 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 426A IS CREATED TO
15 READ AS FOLLOWS:
16 (1) Sections 1 to 11 of this Act shall apply to a Canadian judgment to the extent the
17 judgment is within the scope of Section 14 of this Act, if recognition of the
18 judgment is sought to enforce the judgment.
19 (2) A Canadian judgment that grants both recovery of a sum of money and other
20 relief may be registered under Sections 1 to 11 of this Act, but only to the extent
21 of the grant of recovery of a sum of money.
22 (3) A Canadian judgment regarding subject matter both within and not within the
23 scope of Sections 1 to 11 of this Act may be registered under Sections 1 to 11 of
24 this Act, but only to the extent the judgment is with regard to subject matter
25 within the scope of Sections 1 to 11 of this Act.

10 TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
11 (1) Except as provided in subsection (2) of this section, Sections 12 to 23 of this Act

15 and enforceable.
16 (2) Sections 12 to 23 of this Act do not apply to a foreign-country judgment, even if
17 the judgment grants or denies recovery of a sum of money, to the extent that the
18 judgment is:
19 (a) A judgment for taxes;
20 (b) A fine or other penalty; or
21 (c) A judgment for divorce, support, or maintenance, or other judgment
22 rendered in connection with domestic relations.
23 (3) A party seeking recognition of a foreign-country judgment has the burden of
24 establishing that Sections 12 to 23 of this Act apply to the foreign-country
25 judgment.

1 (1) Except as otherwise provided in subsections (2) and (3) of this section, a court of
2 this state shall recognize a foreign-country judgment to which Sections 12 to 23
3 of this Act apply.
4 (2) A court of this state may not recognize a foreign-country judgment if:
5 (a) The judgment was rendered under a judicial system that does not provide
6 impartial tribunals or procedures compatible with the requirements of due
7 process of law;
8 (b) The foreign court did not have personal jurisdiction over the defendant; or
9 (c) The foreign court did not have jurisdiction over the subject matter.
10 (3) A court of this state shall not be required to recognize a foreign-country judgment
11 if:
12 (a) The defendant in the proceeding in the foreign court did not receive notice
13 of the proceeding in sufficient time to enable the defendant to defend;
14 (b) The judgment was obtained by fraud that deprived the losing party of an
15 adequate opportunity to present its case;
16 (c) The judgment or the cause of action on which the judgment is based is
17 repugnant to the public policy of this state or of the United States;
18 (d) The judgment conflicts with another final and conclusive judgment;
19 (e) The proceeding in the foreign court was contrary to an agreement between
20 the parties under which the dispute in question was to be determined other
21 than by proceedings in that foreign country;
22 (f) In the case of jurisdiction based only on personal service, the foreign court
23 was a seriously inconvenient forum for the trial of the action;
24 (g) The judgment was rendered in circumstances that raise substantial doubt
25 about the integrity of the rendering court with respect to the judgment; or
26 (h) The specific proceeding in the foreign court leading to the judgment was
27 not compatible with the requirements of due process of law.

4 SECTION 16. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 426A IS CREATED
5 TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
6 (1) A foreign-country judgment shall not be refused recognition for lack of personal
7 jurisdiction if the defendant:
8 (a) Was served with process personally in the foreign country;
9 (b) Voluntarily appeared in the proceeding, other than for the purpose of
10 protecting property seized or threatened with seizure in the proceeding or of
11 contesting the jurisdiction of the court over the defendant;
12 (c) Before the commencement of the proceeding, had agreed to submit to the
13 jurisdiction of the foreign court with respect to the subject matter involved;
14 (d) Was domiciled in the foreign country when the proceeding was instituted or
15 was a corporation or other form of business organization that had its
16 principal place of business in, or was organized under the laws of, the
17 foreign country;
18 (e) Had a business office in the foreign country and the proceeding in the
19 foreign court involved a cause of action arising out of business done by the
20 defendant through that office in the foreign country; or
21 (f) Operated a motor vehicle or airplane in the foreign country and the
22 proceeding involved a cause of action arising out of that operation.
23 (2) The list of bases for personal jurisdiction in subsection (1) of this section shall
24 not be exclusive. The courts of this state may recognize bases of personal
25 jurisdiction other than those listed in subsection (1) of this section as sufficient to
26 support a foreign-country judgment.

7 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 426A IS CREATED TO
8 READ AS FOLLOWS:
9 As used in Sections 1 to 11 of this Act:
10 (1) "Canada" means the sovereign nation of Canada and its provinces and
11 territories. "Canadian" has a corresponding meaning; and
12 (2) "Canadian judgment" means a judgment of a court of Canada, other than a
13 judgment that recognized the judgment of another foreign country.
14 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 426A IS CREATED TO
15 READ AS FOLLOWS:
16 (1) Sections 1 to 11 of this Act shall apply to a Canadian judgment to the extent the
17 judgment is within the scope of Section 14 of this Act, if recognition of the
18 judgment is sought to enforce the judgment.
19 (2) A Canadian judgment that grants both recovery of a sum of money and other
20 relief may be registered under Sections 1 to 11 of this Act, but only to the extent
21 of the grant of recovery of a sum of money.
22 (3) A Canadian judgment regarding subject matter both within and not within the
23 scope of Sections 1 to 11 of this Act may be registered under Sections 1 to 11 of
24 this Act, but only to the extent the judgment is with regard to subject matter
25 within the scope of Sections 1 to 11 of this Act.

4 SECTION 16. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 426A IS CREATED
5 TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
6 (1) A foreign-country judgment shall not be refused recognition for lack of personal
7 jurisdiction if the defendant:
8 (a) Was served with process personally in the foreign country;
9 (b) Voluntarily appeared in the proceeding, other than for the purpose of
10 protecting property seized or threatened with seizure in the proceeding or of
11 contesting the jurisdiction of the court over the defendant;
12 (c) Before the commencement of the proceeding, had agreed to submit to the
13 jurisdiction of the foreign court with respect to the subject matter involved;
14 (d) Was domiciled in the foreign country when the proceeding was instituted or
15 was a corporation or other form of business organization that had its
16 principal place of business in, or was organized under the laws of, the
17 foreign country;
18 (e) Had a business office in the foreign country and the proceeding in the
19 foreign court involved a cause of action arising out of business done by the
20 defendant through that office in the foreign country; or
21 (f) Operated a motor vehicle or airplane in the foreign country and the
22 proceeding involved a cause of action arising out of that operation.
23 (2) The list of bases for personal jurisdiction in subsection (1) of this section shall
24 not be exclusive. The courts of this state may recognize bases of personal
25 jurisdiction other than those listed in subsection (1) of this section as sufficient to
26 support a foreign-country judgment.
