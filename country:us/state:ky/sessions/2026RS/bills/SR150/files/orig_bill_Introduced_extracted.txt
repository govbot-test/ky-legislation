1 A RESOLUTION honoring Senator Cassie Chambers Armstrong on her
2 achievement in becoming the first woman in Kentucky history to hold a Senate
3 Democratic leadership position.
4 WHEREAS, Senator Cassie Chambers Armstrong was sworn into office in March
5 2023, and has dutifully served Kentucky's 19th Senatorial District, which encompasses
6 parts of Jefferson County; and
7 WHEREAS, born and raised in Berea, Kentucky, Senator Cassie Chambers
8 Armstrong is a graduate of Yale University, the London School of Economics, and
9 Harvard Law School; and
10 WHEREAS, a devoted public servant, Senator Cassie Chambers Armstrong began
11 her legal career as a Skadden Fellow, providing free legal representation to low-income
12 survivors of domestic violence. She later joined the faculty at the University of Louisville
13 Brandeis School of Law, where her scholarship focused on rural access to justice and
14 strengthening public institutions; and
15 WHEREAS, prior to serving in the Kentucky Senate, Senator Cassie Chambers
16 Armstrong served on the Louisville Metro Council, where she championed affordable
17 childcare, stronger housing protections, and policies to support working families; and
18 WHEREAS, since entering the General Assembly, Senator Cassie Chambers
19 Armstrong has focused on expanding opportunities for Kentuckians, strengthening
20 families, improving economic stability, and ensuring that government works
21 transparently and effectively for all Kentuckians. Her legislative work emphasizes
22 practical solutions that modernize state systems, support children and families, and
23 promote fairness across communities; and
24 WHEREAS, prior to the 2026 Regular Session, Senator Cassie Chambers
25 Armstrong was elected by her colleagues to serve as the Senate Democratic Caucus
26 Whip, becoming the first woman in Kentucky history to hold a Senate Democratic
27 leadership position, a historic milestone for the Democratic caucus and the
