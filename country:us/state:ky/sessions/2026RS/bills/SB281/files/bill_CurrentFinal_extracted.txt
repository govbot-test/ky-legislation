2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 405.021 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) (a) The Circuit Court may grant reasonable visitation rights to either the paternal
5 or maternal grandparents of a child and issue any necessary orders to enforce
6 the decree if:
7 1. The court finds by clear and convincing evidence that:
8 a. There are compelling factors to overcome the presumption that a
9 fit parent acts in the best interests of the child; and
10 b. Visitation with the grandparent[ it Determines that it] is in the
11 best interests[interest] of the child; or[ to do so]
12 2. Someone other than a parent is the grandchild's custodian and the
13 court finds by a preponderance of the evidence that visitation with the
14 grandparent is in the best interests of the child.
15 (b) Once a grandparent has been granted visitation rights under this subsection,
16 those rights shall not be adversely affected by the termination of parental
17 rights belonging to the grandparent's son or daughter, who is the father or
18 mother of the child visited by the grandparent, unless the Circuit Court
19 determines that it is in the best interests[interest] of the child to do so.
20 (2) In determining whether grandparent visitation is in the best interests of the child,
21 the court may consider the:
22 (a) Nature and stability of the relationship between the child and the
23 grandparent seeking visitation, including the petitioner's capacity to give
24 the child love, affection, emotional support, and guidance;
25 (b) Amount of time the grandparent and child spent together;
26 (c) Potential detriments and benefits to the child from granting visitation,
27 including whether the loss of the relationship between the petitioner and

1 child is likely to harm the child or cause emotional distress to the child;
2 (d) Effect granting visitation would have on the child's relationship with the
3 parents or custodian;
4 (e) Physical and emotional health of all the adults involved, custodian, parents,
5 and grandparents alike;
6 (f) Stability of the child's living and schooling arrangements;
7 (g) Wishes and preferences of the child;
8 (h) Motivation of the adults participating in the grandparent visitation
9 proceedings; and
10 (i) Special circumstance when the parent of the child who is the son or
11 daughter of the grandparent is deceased or the marriage of the parent has
12 been dissolved[
13 (b) If the parent of the child who is the son or daughter of the grandparent is
14 deceased, there shall be a rebuttable presumption that visitation with the
15 grandparent is in the best interest of the child if the grandparent can
16 prove a pre-existing significant and viable relationship with the child.
17 (c) In order to prove a significant and viable relationship under paragraph
18 (b) of this subsection, the grandparent shall prove by a preponderance of
19 the evidence that:
20 1. The child resided with the grandparent for at least six (6) consecutive
21 months with or without the current custodian present;
22 2. The grandparent was the caregiver of the child on a regular basis for at
23 least six (6) consecutive months;
24 3. The grandparent had frequent or regular contact with the child for at
25 least twelve (12) consecutive months; or
26 4. There exist any other facts that establish that the loss of the relationship
27 between the grandparent and the child is likely to harm the child].

10 Section 2. KRS 620.090 is amended to read as follows:
11 (1) If, after completion of the temporary removal hearing, the court finds there are
12 reasonable grounds to believe the child is dependent, neglected or abused, the court
13 shall:
14 (a) Issue an order for temporary removal and shall grant temporary custody to the
15 cabinet or other appropriate person or agency. Preference shall be given to
16 available and qualified relatives of the child considering the wishes of the
17 parent or other person exercising custodial control or supervision, if known.
18 The order shall state the specific reasons for removal and show that alternative
19 less restrictive placements and services have been considered. The court may
20 recommend a placement for the child;
21 (b) Inquire as to an existing child support order; and
22 (c) If there is no existing order, or if the order is to be amended, the court shall:
23 1. Make specific findings, either written or on the record, as to:
24 a. The child support obligation in the best interest of the child;
25 b. The action to be taken by the payee, payor, or any other party by
26 making an application for services to the child support office who
27 shall take all appropriate action; or

1 c. Setting a hearing as soon as practicable; and
2 2. Require proper service before establishing a new child support order.
3 (2) In placing a child under an order of temporary custody, the cabinet or its designee
4 shall use the least restrictive appropriate placement available. Preference shall be
5 given to available and qualified relatives of the child considering the wishes of the
6 parent or other person exercising custodial control or supervision, if known. The
7 child may also be placed in a facility or program operated or approved by the
8 cabinet, including a foster home, or any other appropriate available placement.
9 However, under no circumstance shall the child be placed in a home, facility, or
10 other shelter with a child who has been committed to the Department of Juvenile
11 Justice for commission of a sex crime as that term is defined in KRS 17.500, unless
12 the child committed for the commission of a sex crime is kept segregated from
13 other children in the home, facility, or other shelter that have not been committed
14 for the commission of a sex crime.
15 (3) If the court finds there are not reasonable grounds to believe the child is dependent,
16 neglected or abused, or if no action is taken within seventy-two (72) hours, the
17 emergency custody order shall be dissolved automatically and the cabinet or its
18 designee shall return the child to the parent or other person exercising custodial
19 control or supervision. A request for a continuance of the hearing by the parent or
20 other person exercising custodial control or supervision shall constitute action
21 precluding automatic dissolution of the emergency custody order.
22 (4) When the court issues a temporary order for the custody of a child, the court may
23 order that, within two (2) weeks, arrangements be made for the child to receive a
24 thorough medical, visual, and dental examination by a professional authorized by
25 the Kentucky Revised Statutes to conduct such examinations. The costs of the
26 examination shall be paid by the cabinet.
27 (5) The child shall remain in temporary custody with the cabinet for a period of time

4 time after making written findings establishing the need for the extension and after

7 shall consider granting reasonable visitation rights to any other grandparent of the
