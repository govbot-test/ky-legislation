1 WHEREAS, the Ohio River basin provides critical natural infrastructure that
2 reduces flooding, supports fish and wildlife habitat, and strengthens the long-term
3 resilience of Kentucky communities facing increasing economic and environmental
4 challenges; and
5 WHEREAS, despite its importance, the Ohio River basin remains the largest
6 watershed in the United States without a dedicated federal restoration program, even as
7 similar initiatives in the Great Lakes and Chesapeake Bay have delivered substantial
8 economic, ecological, and public health benefits; and
9 WHEREAS, the federal Ohio River Restoration Program Act would establish the
10 Ohio River Basin Restoration Program to improve water quality, restore fish and wildlife
11 habitat, reduce flood risk, address invasive species and toxic contamination, and expand
12 recreation, workforce development, and community revitalization across the basin; and
13 WHEREAS, the program would operate as a collaborative, nonregulatory
14 partnership, providing federal resources to support state, local, nonprofit, and
15 community-led restoration projects without creating new permitting requirements or
16 regulatory authorities; and
17 WHEREAS, Kentucky stands to benefit from coordinated restoration investments
18 through improved drinking water quality, lower water treatment costs, revitalized
19 riverfront communities, enhanced fisheries and wildlife habitat, expanded outdoor
20 recreation opportunities, and strengthened long-term economic development and
21 community stability;
22 NOW, THEREFORE,
23 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the General Assembly of the
24 Commonwealth of Kentucky:
25 Section 1. The House of Representatives hereby expresses its support for House
26 Resolution 5966 and Senate Bill 3796, the Ohio River Restoration Program Act, and
27 recognizes the importance of restoring and protecting the Ohio River Basin to safeguard
