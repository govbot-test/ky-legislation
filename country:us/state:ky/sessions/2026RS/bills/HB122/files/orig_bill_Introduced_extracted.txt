UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 204
1 AN ACT relating to historical instruction.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, any world history or world civilization
6 course offered by a public middle or high school that is not an advanced
7 placement course shall include instruction on African history relevant to the time
8 period covered by the course, including but not limited to the following
9 civilizations:
10 (a) The Kingdoms of Kush, Kongo, Punt, Benin, Zulu, Berber, Mutapa, and
11 Zimbabwe; and
12 (b) The Mali, Songhai, Carthage, Ghana, Asante, Ethiopian, Aksumite, and
13 Egyptian Empires.
14 (2) (a) Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, any United States history course
15 offered by a public middle or high school that is not an advanced placement
16 course and includes instruction on the founding of the United States shall
17 also include instruction on Native American culture and history prior to
18 and after the founding of the United States.
19 (b) The Native American instruction required by this subsection shall include
20 but not be limited to instruction on the history and culture of the indigenous
21 peoples of the Eastern Woodlands, Great Basin, Plains, Southwest, Mission
22 Indians, Northwest Coast, Alaska, and Pacific Islands.
23 (3) The department shall collaborate with:
24 (a) The Kentucky Center for African-American Heritage to develop an elective
25 high school course offering in African history; and
26 (b) The Kentucky Native American Heritage Commission to develop an elective
27 high school course offering in Native American history.
