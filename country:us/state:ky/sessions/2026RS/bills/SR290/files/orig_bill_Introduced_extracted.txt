1 WHEREAS, the Kentucky Senate has consistently prioritized combating sex
2 trafficking and crimes against children through legislation, by partnering with the
3 Attorney General to fund anti-trafficking initiatives in the Commonwealth, and by
4 recognizing January as Human Trafficking Prevention Month to increase public
5 awareness of the realities of human trafficking; and
6 WHEREAS, the people of Kentucky, including the constituents of Representative
7 Thomas Massie, have a vested interest in ensuring federal law enforcement upholds the
8 rule of law, protects vulnerable individuals, pursues justice without fear or favor, and
9 builds on the transparency achieved through the Epstein Files Transparency Act; and
10 WHEREAS, the Senate commends Representative Thomas Massie's persistent
11 efforts to expose the Jeffrey Epstein files and believes that any credible evidence of
12 criminal wrongdoing uncovered therein warrants thorough investigation and appropriate
13 prosecution;

16 Kentucky:
17 Section 1. The Senate urges the United States Attorney General to direct the
18 Department of Justice to conduct a comprehensive review of all materials released
19 pursuant to the Epstein Files Transparency Act, legislation spearheaded by Kentucky's
20 Representative Thomas Massie, and to pursue criminal charges against any individual for
21 whom there is sufficient evidence of violations of federal law, including but not limited
22 to sex trafficking, exploitation of minors, conspiracy, or related offenses.
23 Section 2. The Senate expresses appreciation for Representative Thomas
24 Massie's leadership in making the Jeffrey Epstein files public, thereby advancing justice
25 for victims and transparency for the American people.
