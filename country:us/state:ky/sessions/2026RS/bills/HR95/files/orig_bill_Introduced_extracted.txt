1 A RESOLUTION honoring the accomplishments of the Hindu community in
2 Kentucky.
3 WHEREAS, Hinduism is the third-largest religion in the world, with the belief in
4 the concept that a person's actions and thoughts determine the course of life; and
5 WHEREAS, approximately one percent of the Kentucky's population identify as
6 Hindu, with 200 to 250 Hindu families living in Bowling Green and the Hindu Temple of
7 Kentucky serving near 1,500 Hindu families of Bhutanese, Nepalese, and Indian descent;
8 and
9 WHEREAS, there are a growing number of Hindu temples opening throughout the
10 state, with temples in Lexington, Louisville, Bowling Green, Owensboro, and Richmond;
11 and
12 WHEREAS, both Hindus and Kentuckians have a strong sense of community, with
13 a shared belief in the value of community service. The Hindu Temple of Kentucky hosts
14 frequent fundraising events for local health initiatives, statewide health services, and
15 assistance with government programs and national relief funds, members of the Hindu
16 community in Louisville gathered to repaint the Swaminarayan Temple and raised
17 approximately $3,500 in donations for the Coalition for the Homeless in Louisville; and
18 WHEREAS, like Kentuckians, Hindus want strong values in their children. The
19 Hindu Temple of Kentucky offers Hindu religion classes for children and teenagers, hosts
20 community activities, and provides resources for the Hindus in Kentucky, with the
21 objectives to "build character and self-esteem" and to "empower children and youth...to
22 become positive contributors to society." Similarly, the recent construction of a Hindu
23 temple in Richmond was built in large part to instill "good morals and character" in
24 youth; and
25 WHEREAS, Hindus and Kentuckians are strongly committed to family, hospitality,
26 and fostering a welcoming environment. The inspiration for Tyler Childers's song "Tirtha
27 Yatra" comes from his experiences and interactions with Hindus and their religious
