4 Section 1. KRS 164.769 is amended to read as follows:
5 (1) It is the intent of the General Assembly to establish a teacher scholarship program

9 have the following meanings]:
10 (a) "Critical shortage area" means an understaffing of teachers in particular
11 subject matters at the secondary level, in grade levels, or in geographic
12 locations at the elementary and secondary level, as determined by the
13 commissioner of education in consultation with the authority. The
14 commissioner and the authority may use any source considered reliable,
15 including but not limited to local education agencies, to identify the critical
16 shortage areas;
17 (b) "Dual credit" has the same meaning as in KRS 158.007;
18 (c) "Eligible program of study" means an undergraduate or graduate program of
19 study which is preparatory to teacher certification;
20 (d) "Participating institution" means an institution of higher education located in
21 Kentucky which offers an eligible program of study and has in force an
22 agreement with the authority providing for administration of this program;
23 (e) "Qualified teaching service" means teaching the major portion of each school
24 day for at least seventy (70) days each semester in a public school of the
25 Commonwealth or a private school certified pursuant to KRS 156.160(2),
26 except that an individual having a disability as defined by Title II of the
27 Americans with Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. sec. 12131 et seq., or serious and

18 enrollment in an eligible program of study at a participating institution shall be
19 eligible to apply for and be awarded teacher scholarships. Teacher scholarships

21 requirements established by the Education Professional Standards Board pursuant

24 awarding teacher scholarships to students who meet the preceding criteria, then
25 awards shall be made to any otherwise eligible students.

27 scholarship to be awarded for each semester and summer term under this section.
