1 A RESOLUTION honoring police officer Nick Wilt on the occasion of his
2 retirement from Louisville Metro Police Department.
3 WHEREAS, Nick Wilt's service to the Louisville Metro Police Department began
4 on March 21, 2023; and
5 WHEREAS, Nick Wilt was honored for his retirement in January 2026 after a brief
6 but exceptional career with the Louisville Metro Police Department; and
7 WHEREAS, Nick Wilt, is best remembered for his bravery by being one of the first
8 officers to respond to the Old National Bank shooting on April 10, 2023; which left 6
9 people dead and eight injured; and
10 WHEREAS, only 10 days out of the training academy, Nick Wilt was severely
11 wounded during that incident and was forced to embark on an extended recovery and
12 rehabilitation journey; and
13 WHEREAS, Nick Wilt's heroic actions during the Old National Bank shooting,
14 saved many lives and left a lasting impression of what it means to be a hero; and
15 WHEREAS, in presenting Nick Wilt with an award for his service, Louisville
16 Metro Police Chief Paul Humphrey stated, "You represent the best of what we are. I
17 don’t know what kind of cop you were going to be, but I know what kind of man you
18 are"; and
19 WHEREAS, in giving Nick Wilt a key to the city, Mayor Craig Greenberg said,
20 "That day here in Louisville, Kentucky, one of our newest officers answered that call
21 without hesitation and with incredible bravery, putting himself in harm’s way, . . . [and]
22 prioritizing other people’s safety over his own"; and
23 WHEREAS, Attorney General Russell Coleman echoed the sentiments of others
24 when he said "So many people that you didn’t even know were praying for you, giving
25 thanks for your survival, and praying that God would come alongside your recovery";
26 and
27 WHEREAS, Assistant Chief Bryan Edelen lauded Nick Wilt’s example since that
