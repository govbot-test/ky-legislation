5 WHEREAS, Denver Wells joined the United States Marine Corps near the close of
6 World War II, and was assigned the title of Private First Class; and
7 WHEREAS, PFC Denver Wells served in the Pacific Theater on the USS Curtiss,
8 the first seaplane tender constructed for the United States Navy, which was just returning
9 to full service after it was attacked during the Japanese assault on Pearl Harbor; and
10 WHEREAS, PFC Denver Wells was aboard the USS Curtiss when it sailed towards
11 the island of Okinawa to join forces at the Battle of Okinawa, one of the largest battles of
12 the Pacific War; and
13 WHEREAS, PFC Denver Wells arrived offshore to join the battle on May 22, 1945,
14 where the USS Curtiss was to serve as the flagship for Commander, Fleet Air Wing 1;
15 and
16 WHEREAS, on June 21, 1945, just one day before the close of the Battle of
17 Okinawa, a kamikaze and its bomb ripped two holes into the hull of the USS Curtiss,
18 killing 35 servicemen, including PFC Denver Wells; and
19 WHEREAS, at the time of his death, PFC Denver Wells had been aboard the USS
20 Curtiss for less than two months and was just eight days shy of his 20th birthday; and
21 WHEREAS, PFC Denver Wells represents the values of service and sacrifice that
22 continue to define our Commonwealth and this great Nation;
23 NOW, THEREFORE,
