1 A RESOLUTION honoring Senator Jimmy Higdon upon his retirement from the
2 Senate.
3 "Everyone will sit under their own vine and fig tree,
4 and no one will make them afraid."
5 Micah 4:4
6 WHEREAS, Jimmy Higdon was born July 15, 1953, to Cecil and Alice Higdon of
7 Lebanon, Kentucky; and
8 WHEREAS, the oldest of seven children, Jimmy graduated from Marion County
9 High School in 1971, and then earned a B.S. in Industrial Arts Technology with a minor
10 in business administration from Morehead State University in 1975; and
11 WHEREAS, upon graduation, Jimmy Higdon was commissioned as a 2nd
12 Lieutenant in the United States Army; and
13 WHEREAS, Jimmy Higdon returned to Lebanon, Kentucky, in 1976 and became a
14 partner in Key Market, which later was renamed Higdon’s Foodtown, which he operated
15 with his wife, Jane, from 1986 to 2011; and
16 WHEREAS, Higdon's Foodtown was a local institution that gained notoriety in
17 2010, when video of Senator Higdon tackling a shoplifter went viral. The video also had
18 the added benefit of bolstering the Senator's stance of being tough on crime; and
19 WHEREAS, Jimmy Higdon was elected in 2002 as the State Representative for the
20 24th District, which included Marion, Casey, and a portion of Pulaski Counties; and
21 WHEREAS, for his work in education while serving in the House of
22 Representatives, Jimmy Higdon was awarded the Joe Kelly Education Award by the
23 Department of Education and was named a "Friend of Education" by the Kentucky
24 School Board Association; and
25 WHEREAS, in December 2009, Jimmy Higdon won a special election to represent
26 the 14th State Senatorial District, which currently encompasses LaRue, Marion, Nelson,
27 Spencer, and Washington Counties; and

1 WHEREAS, highlights of Senator Jimmy Higdon's service include serving as Chair
2 of the Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee, serving as Senate
3 Majority Whip from 2014 to 2018 and Senate President Pro Tem from 2018 to 2019, and
4 serving as Senate Transportation Committee Chair since 2020; and
5 WHEREAS, as Transportation Committee Chair, Senator Jimmy Higdon oversaw
6 record funding for state road projects, including $500 million for major state highway
7 projects, such as the Brent Spence Bridge, the Mountain Parkway completion, and
8 construction of the I-69 Bridge. Senator Higdon also spearheaded $450 million in
9 funding for projects in high growth counties and for economic development projects
10 through the state; and
11 WHEREAS, Senator Jimmy Higdon also assisted with legislation transitioning to
12 the KAVIS system, which modernized Kentucky’s vehicle title and registration systems,
13 and strongly advocated for expansion of driver licensing and testing centers; and
14 WHEREAS, equally as meaningful was Senator Jimmy Higdon's work on the
15 Public Pension Oversight Board, which he has chaired since 2019, becoming the General
16 Assembly’s expert on public pension matters, improving the public’s knowledge of
17 pension issues facing the Commonwealth, and shepherding the systems towards
18 improved financial stability that will benefit future generations in this great
19 Commonwealth; and
20 WHEREAS, in 2017, after being approached by a constituent, Senator Jimmy
21 Higdon established Military Kids Day as a recognition and celebration of the children of
22 military families at the Capitol. He has put his heart and soul into this annual event,
23 which has grown from fewer than 40 attendees in 2017 to approximately 250 participants
24 in 2025, has received national recognition, and serves as a model for improving civic
25 engagement in other states; and
26 WHEREAS, Senator Jimmy Higdon is known for working across the aisle and
27 across state government with the executive branch, as well as fulfilling the unofficial role

20 Section 1. The members of the Senate do hereby honor the extraordinary
21 service of Senator Jimmy Higdon to the General Assembly and the Commonwealth of

24 extraordinary service of Senator Jimmy Higdon to the Commonwealth of Kentucky and
25 the General Assembly.
