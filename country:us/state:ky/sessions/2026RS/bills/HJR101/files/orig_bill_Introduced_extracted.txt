UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1185
1 A JOINT RESOLUTION designating the S/SGT David W. Harris Memorial Bridge
2 in Carter County.
3 WHEREAS, David W. Harris, one of seven children of Roy and Pearl Everman
4 Harris, was born on November 26, 1918, in Carter County, Kentucky; and
5 WHEREAS, David W. Harris served in the United States Army Air Forces during
6 World War II, enlisting as a private in the 358th Bomb Squadron, 303rd Bomber Group,
7 on May 11, 1942, and achieving the rank of Staff Sergeant; and
8 WHEREAS, S/SGT David W. Harris was trained as an airplane mechanic at the
9 Army Air Force Technical School, Amarillo Army Air Field, Amarillo, Texas; and
10 WHEREAS, following training, S/SGT David W. Harris was sent to England to
11 serve as a flight engineer/top turret gunner on a B-17 bomber; and
12 WHEREAS, S/SGT David W. Harris was tragically killed on February 22, 1944,
13 when his bomber collided in mid-air with another B-17 bomber over England while
14 forming up for a mission to bomb the aircraft industry at Aschersleben, Germany; and
15 WHEREAS, S/SGT David W. Harris was awarded the Air Medal, the Purple Heart,
16 the World War II Victory Medal, and the American Campaign Medal and is interred in
17 the Cambridge American Cemetery, in Cambridge, England; and
18 WHEREAS, Staff Sergeant David W. Harris served with honor and valor during
19 World War II and this bridge designation will serve as a reminder of his life, sacrifice,
20 and dedication to our nation;
21 NOW, THEREFORE,
22 Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
23 Section 1. The Transportation Cabinet shall designate the bridge on Kentucky
24 Route 9 in Carter County at mile point 11.01 (Bridge Number 022B00149N), as the
25 "S/SGT David W. Harris Memorial Bridge" and shall, within 30 days of the effective
26 date of this Resolution, erect appropriate signage denoting this designation.
