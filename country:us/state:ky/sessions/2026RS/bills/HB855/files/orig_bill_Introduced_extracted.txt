1 AN ACT relating to persons entitled to vote.
2 WHEREAS, school boards exercise significant authority over policies that shape
3 the daily lives and academic futures of public school students; and
4 WHEREAS, permitting residents who are 16 and 17 years of age to vote in school
5 board elections, when approved by a local school board, encourages informed civic
6 participation while preserving local control;
7 NOW, THEREFORE,
8 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
9 Section 1. KRS 116.025 is amended to read as follows:
10 (1) Every person who is a citizen of the United States, a resident of this state, and a
11 resident of the precinct in which he or she offers to vote on or before the day
12 preceding the closing of the registration books for any primary, regular[general], or
13 special election, who possesses on the day of any election the qualifications set
14 forth in Section 145 of the Constitution, exclusive of the durational residency
15 requirements, who is not disqualified under that section or under any other statute,
16 and who is registered as provided in this chapter, may vote for all officers to be
17 elected by the people and on all public questions submitted for determination at that
18 election, in the precinct in which he or she is qualified to vote. Any person who
19 shall have been convicted of any election law offense which is a felony shall not be
20 permitted to vote until his or her civil rights have been restored by executive
21 pardon.
22 (2) Any person charged with or indicted for a crime, whether or not in custody for
23 same, who has not yet been convicted of the offense and who is not otherwise
24 ineligible to vote, may vote for all offices to be elected by the people and on all
25 public questions submitted for determination at that election, in the precinct in
26 which he or she is qualified to vote.

12 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 116 IS CREATED TO
13 READ AS FOLLOWS:
14 (1) County clerks shall administer the registration and voting of persons sixteen (16)
15 and seventeen (17) years of age who are eligible under Section 1 of this Act using
16 existing polling locations, but may use separate election procedures and
17 registration systems.

19 limited to school board contests.

21 accordance with KRS Chapter 13A as necessary for the administration of this
22 section. These regulations are not limited to, but shall include:
23 (a) A registration form and a process to register persons sixteen (16) and
24 seventeen (17) years of age to vote only in school board elections, which
25 shall conform as nearly as possible to the equivalent form and process used
26 for all other eligible voters;
27 (b) A method to verify the identity of registered voters who are sixteen (16) and
