2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 17 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section:
6 (a) "Criminal history records information":
7 1. Means information collected by criminal justice agencies on
8 individuals consisting of identifiable descriptions and notations of
9 arrests, indictments, information, or other formal criminal charges,
10 and any disposition arising therefrom, including sentencing,
11 correction supervision, and release;
12 2. Includes information that is sealed pursuant to court order or other
13 law if the records are accessible by criminal justice agencies for the
14 purpose of conducting background checks; and
15 3. Does not include identification information such as fingerprint
16 records to the extent that the information does not indicate
17 involvement of the individual in the criminal justice system;
18 (b) "Criminal justice agency" means:
19 1. The Kentucky Court of Justice;
20 2. The Department of Kentucky State Police;
21 3. Sheriffs;
22 4. State and local correctional facilities;
23 5. City, county, urban-county government, charter county government,
24 consolidated local government, and unified local government police
25 departments;
26 6. School resource officer departments;
27 7. Airport safety and security departments;

7 Section 2. KRS 610.340 is amended to read as follows:
8 (1) (a) Unless a specific provision of KRS Chapters 600 to 645 specifies otherwise,
9 all juvenile court records of any nature generated pursuant to KRS Chapters
10 600 to 645 by any agency or instrumentality, public or private, shall be
11 deemed to be confidential and shall not be disclosed except to the child,
12 parent, victims, or other persons authorized to attend a juvenile court hearing
13 pursuant to KRS 610.070 unless ordered by the court for good cause.
14 (b) Juvenile court records which contain information pertaining to arrests,
15 petitions, adjudications, and dispositions of a child may be disclosed to
16 victims or other persons authorized to attend a juvenile court hearing pursuant
17 to KRS 610.070.
18 (c) Release of the child's treatment, medical, mental, or psychological records is
19 prohibited unless presented as evidence in Circuit Court. Any records
20 resulting from the child's prior abuse and neglect under Title IV-E or Title IV-
21 B of the Federal Social Security Act shall not be disclosed to victims or other
22 persons authorized to attend a juvenile court hearing pursuant to KRS
23 610.070.
24 (d) Victim access under this subsection to juvenile court records shall include
25 access to records of adjudications that occurred prior to July 15, 1998.
26 (2) The provisions of this section shall not apply to public officers or employees
27 engaged in the investigation of and in the prosecution of cases under KRS Chapters
