1 AN ACT relating to teacher scholarships.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 164 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) The General Assembly hereby establishes the Promise Program to assist
6 individuals to become certified Kentucky teachers and render teaching service in
7 Kentucky schools.
8 (2) For purposes of this section, the terms listed below shall have the following
9 meanings:
10 (a) "Authority" means the Kentucky Higher Education Assistance Authority;
11 (b) "Eligible program of study" means an undergraduate or graduate program
12 of study which is preparatory to teacher certification;
13 (c) "Eligible student" means a student who:
14 1. Is a citizen or permanent resident of the United States;
15 2. Is a Kentucky resident, as determined by the participating institution
16 in accordance with criteria established by the Council on
17 Postsecondary Education for the purposes of admission and tuition
18 assessment;
19 3. Is enrolled at a participating institution;
20 4. Scored in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of all Kentucky students
21 on the college admissions examination administered in the spring of
22 grade eleven (11) as required by KRS 158.6453; and
23 5. Meets any other criteria established in administrative regulation
24 promulgated by the authority;
25 (d) "Participating institution" means an institution of higher education located
26 in Kentucky which offers an eligible program of study and has in force an
27 agreement with the authority providing for administration of this program;

2 (e) "Qualified teaching service" means teaching the major portion of each
3 school day for at least eighty (80) days each semester in a public school of
4 the Commonwealth while satisfying a one (1) year contract, except that an
5 individual having a disability as defined in Title II of the Americans with
6 Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. sec. 12131 et seq., or serious and extended
7 illness, whose disability or illness, certified by a licensed physician, prevents
8 that individual from teaching a major portion of each school day, shall be
9 deemed to perform qualified teaching service by teaching the maximum
10 time permitted by their attending physician.
11 (3) The authority may, to the extent of appropriations and other funds available to it
12 pursuant to subsection (8) of this section, award promise awards to eligible
13 students pursuant to subsection (4) of this section, who initially demonstrate
14 financial need in accordance with standards and criteria established by the
15 authority. Each promise award shall be evidenced by a promissory note that
16 requires repayment or cancellation pursuant to subsection (5) of this section.
17 (4) Promise awards shall be provided to eligible students accepted into an eligible
18 program of study. To the extent funds are available, the authority shall provide
19 eligible students a maximum award of up to the highest tuition charged by a
20 Kentucky public university for full-time enrollment in an undergraduate
21 program, not to exceed the applicant's total cost of attendance minus other
22 financial assistance received or expected to be received by the applicant during
23 the academic period.
24 (5) (a) The authority shall disburse promise awards to eligible students who agree
25 to render qualified teaching service as certified teachers and are
26 unconditionally admitted and enrolled in an eligible program of study.
27 (b) A promise award shall not be provided or a promissory note cancellation

6 (c) Recipients shall render five (5) years of qualified teaching service with no
7 accrual of interest or payment due on the amount of promise awards. At the
8 completion of each year of qualified teaching service, twenty percent (20%)
9 of the initial promise award shall be cancelled.
10 (d) If the recipient of a promise award fails to complete an eligible program of
11 study at a participating institution or fails to render qualified teaching
12 service, unless the failure is temporarily waived for cause by the authority,
13 the recipient shall immediately become liable to the authority for repayment
14 of the sum of all outstanding promissory notes and interest accrued.
15 Persons liable for repayment of awards under this paragraph shall be liable
16 for interest accruing from the date on which the recipient ceases to be
17 enrolled in an eligible program of study and ceases qualified teaching
18 service.
19 (e) The authority shall establish, by administrative regulation, the terms and
20 conditions for the award, cancellation, and repayment of promise
21 scholarships, including but not limited to the selection criteria, eligibility for
22 renewal awards, amount of scholarship payments, deferments, the rate of
23 repayment, and the interest rate thereon.
24 (f) Notwithstanding any other statute to the contrary, the maximum interest
25 rate applicable to repayment of a promissory note under this section shall be
26 eight percent (8%) per annum, except that if a judgment is rendered to
27 recover payment, the judgment shall bear interest at the rate of five percent
