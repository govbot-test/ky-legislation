UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 2308
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 160.020 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) All school districts embracing designated cities together with the territory within
5 their limits, including any territory added for school purposes outside of the city
6 limits, and all independent graded common school districts having a school census
7 enumeration of two hundred (200) or more children, constitute independent school
8 districts, except those which have merged with a county school district since June
9 14, 1934. No independent district [other than a designated city ]shall continue to
10 operate when its average daily enrollment[school census enumeration of children]
11 falls below minimum enrollment requirements[two hundred (200) pupils] unless it
12 appears to the Kentucky Board of Education that the district can maintain a more
13 efficient program of school service by operating as an independent district as
14 demonstrated through evidence that includes but is not limited to the:
15 (a) District's student achievement, academic growth, school climate, and
16 successful student outcomes;
17 (b) Lack of waste, duplication, or mismanagement in the operation of the
18 district;
19 (c) Number of nonresident pupils who enroll into the district; and
20 (d) Projected impact from implementing a merger with a contiguous district in
21 providing an efficient program of school service and an adequate education
22 for the district's students.
23 (2) The minimum enrollment requirements shall be:
24 (a) Three hundred (300) in-person students for school districts providing no
25 service in any grade nine (9) through twelve (12); and
26 (b) Four hundred fifty (450) in-person students for school districts providing
27 service in any grade nine (9) through twelve (12).

1 (3) As used in this section, "designated city" means a city classified as a city of the
2 first, second, third, fourth, or fifth class as of January 1, 2014, under the city
3 classification system in effect prior to January 1, 2015. The Department of
4 Education shall, on or before January 1, 2015, create an official registry listing the
5 cities that qualify as a "designated city" under this section and shall publish that
6 registry on its website[Web site].
7 Section 2. KRS 160.041 is amended to read as follows:
8 (1) When a board of education of a[an independent] school district desires to have its
9 district merge with[become] a contiguous[part of the county] school district, it shall
10 by motion so record its desire in the minutes of the board. The board, or its
11 executive officer, shall convey this request to the[ county] board of education of the
12 contiguous school district. At its next regular meeting, or at a special meeting held
13 prior thereto, the[ county] board of education of the contiguous school district shall
14 consider the[pass upon this] request.
15 (2) (a) If the[ county] board of education receiving the request refuses, or the two (2)
16 boards of education cannot agree upon such a proposition of merger[ of the
17 independent district with the county district], the question of merger shall be
18 submitted to the qualified voters of the two (2) districts at the next regular
19 election if the question is filed with the county clerk not later than the second
20 Tuesday in August preceding the regular election.
21 (b)[(a)] If a majority of those voting on the question from each respective
22 school district favor merger, the school boards of the two (2) school districts
23 shall jointly develop a plan for adoption of the merger.
24 (c)[(b)] If the two (2) school boards cannot agree to the terms of merger within
25 sixty (60) days following the date of the regular election, the chief state school
26 officer shall develop the terms of the adoption of merger.
