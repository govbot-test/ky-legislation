1 A RESOLUTION recognizing and honoring Representative Al Gentry for his
2 leadership in the creation and success of state, national, and international golfer
3 associations for golfers with disabilities.
4 WHEREAS, Alan Joseph Gentry was born and raised in southwest Jefferson
5 County and learned a strong work ethic at an early age; and
6 WHEREAS, Al Gentry graduated from Bishop David High School in 1982 and
7 earned a bachelor of science in geology from the University of Louisville in 1988; and
8 WHEREAS, in 1993 at the age of 28, Al Gentry was pursuing post-graduate work
9 at the University of Kentucky, and while working as a hydrogeologist he was involved in
10 a tragic workplace drilling accident that caused the loss of his dominant right arm above
11 his elbow; and
12 WHEREAS, while still in his hospital bed recovering from the accident, Al Gentry
13 was introduced to amputee golf by the local one-armed golfing legend, Don Fightmaster;
14 and
15 WHEREAS, reliant on the foundational work ethic and his motto of "Never Quit,"
16 Al Gentry returned to the workforce and began competing in golf events for amputee
17 athletes; and
18 WHEREAS, Al Gentry founded the Kentucky Amputee Golf Association in 1994,
19 which later began operating as the Amputee Golf Charities, and continues to host annual
20 golf tournaments to promote adaptive golf for individuals with physical, visual, or
21 intellectual disabilities; and
22 WHEREAS, in 2000, alongside Don Fightmaster, Scott Luck, and Bill Frey, Al
23 Gentry founded the North American One-Armed Golfer Association (NAOAGA). He
24 served as the president of the association from 2001 to 2008 and continues to serve as a
25 lifetime board member; and
26 WHEREAS, in 2008, the NAOAGA, along with the Society of One-Armed Golfers
27 in Europe, established the Fightmaster Cup, a Ryder Cup style competition between

1 North America and Europe; and
2 WHEREAS, in 2014, Al Gentry was the driving force to create the United States
3 Adaptive Golf Alliance (USAGA) to unite the variety of local, state, and national
4 organizations working with golfers with disabilities to form an alliance to generate one,
5 large, collective, and consistent voice; and
6 WHEREAS, in 2019, Al Gentry co-authored the USAGA's Ranking and
7 Competition Standards Manual providing the country's first published standards for
8 golfers with disabilities. Al Gentry was the sole creator of the United States Adaptive
9 Golf Rankings which is the current system for classifying athletes with disabilities based
10 on their disability impairment using 15 different sport classes and providing a net ranking
11 system incorporating the USAGA standards; and
12 WHEREAS, in 2020, Al Gentry and the USAGA Competition Ranking Committee
13 created a course conditions adjustment for rounds of golf played in difficult conditions
14 for the adaptive ranking system. Gentry's methodology was recognized by the United
15 States Golf Association and integrated into their own handicap system used by all golfers
16 in the United States; and
17 WHEREAS, in 2022, Al Gentry was inducted into the Eastern Amputee Golf
18 Association Hall of Fame in recognition of his leadership and dedication to promoting
19 amputee golf, supporting disabled golfers, and his individual skill in golf; and
20 WHEREAS, Al Gentry has made an impact in the world of golf for all golfers and
21 especially for golfers with disabilities where he has implemented his "never quit" motto
22 to provide an accessible, fair, and competitive sport for all individuals;
23 NOW, THEREFORE,
24 Be it resolved by the Senate of the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of
25 Kentucky:
