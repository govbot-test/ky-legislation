UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 2603
1 A RESOLUTION recognizing and celebrating the patriotic service and community
2 contributions of veterans and the importance of protecting the earned benefits of our
3 veterans from retaliatory and punitive measures.
4 WHEREAS, the people of Kentucky recognize and celebrate the patriotic service
5 and community contributions of the more than 370,000 Kentucky veterans and their
6 families, totaling over one million residents, who call our great Commonwealth their
7 home; and
8 WHEREAS, the people of the Commonwealth and this nation have a First
9 Amendment right to free speech, and the veterans of the United States Armed Forces
10 have sacrificed for the protection of that exercise; and
11 WHEREAS, in recognition of veterans' vital contributions to our state economy and
12 local communities, we voice full support for the preservation and protection of their
13 earned benefits from any punitive elimination or reduction of their retiree pay, veteran
14 healthcare, or disability compensation for any exercise of their First Amendment right of
15 free speech; and
16 WHEREAS, the right to free speech was a central impetus for the American
17 Revolution, and America's veterans, from the camps of Valley Forge to the deserts of
18 Afghanistan, have stood time and time again to protect this uniquely American idea of
19 free speech; and
20 WHEREAS, Kentucky veterans have faithfully served the nation, and we
21 steadfastly honor their service by protecting their earned benefits and right to free speech.
22 There would be no right to free speech were it not for America's veterans; and
23 WHEREAS, any punitive risk that unjustly threatens Kentucky veterans' benefits
24 would severely impact our 370,000 veterans and their families; and
25 WHEREAS, volumes of First Amendment case law have been promulgated
26 because of the speech of veterans advocating for their hard-earned rights; and
27 WHEREAS, to disenfranchise one veteran can disenfranchise all veterans;
