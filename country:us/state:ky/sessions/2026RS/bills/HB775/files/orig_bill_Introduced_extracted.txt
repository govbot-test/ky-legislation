6 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 6 IS CREATED TO
7 READ AS FOLLOWS:
8 (1) Service as a delegate or alternate delegate by a member of the General Assembly
9 is an additional duty of the member's office.
10 (2) A delegate or alternate delegate shall not be entitled to compensation for service
11 as a delegate or alternate delegate.
12 (3) A delegate or alternate delegate shall be entitled to reimbursement for necessary
13 expenses incurred in performance of official duties, subject to any applicable
14 limitation on reimbursement provided by state law.
15 (4) A delegate or alternate delegate shall not accept a gift, loan, food or beverages,
16 entertainment, lodging, transportation, or other benefit from any lobbyist or
17 legislative agent as defined in KRS 6.611.

19 following oath before voting or taking an action as a delegate or alternate
20 delegate of this Commonwealth: "I do solemnly swear (or affirm) that to the
21 best of my abilities, I will, as a delegate (or alternate delegate) to the Article
22 V convention, act according to the limits of the authority granted to me by
23 Kentucky law, not consider or vote to approve any amendment to the United
24 States Constitution not authorized by the General Assembly in its
25 application to Congress to call this convention or any amendment outside
26 the scope of this convention if the General Assembly did not make an
27 application, faithfully abide by and execute the instructions to delegates or

9 SECTION 4. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 6 IS CREATED TO
10 READ AS FOLLOWS:
11 (1) The General Assembly by joint resolution shall adopt instructions to the delegates
12 and alternate delegates to govern the actions of those officers at an Article V
13 convention.
14 (2) The General Assembly shall not adopt instructions for an Article V convention
15 called following an application by the General Assembly to the United States
16 Congress for the convention that would authorize a delegate or alternate delegate
17 to consider or vote to approve an amendment to the United States Constitution
18 that is not authorized by the General Assembly in its application for the
19 convention.
20 (3) The General Assembly may amend instructions for an Article V convention at
21 any time by joint resolution, except as provided in subsection (2) of this section.
22 (4) A delegate or alternate delegate shall not cast an unauthorized vote.
23 (5) Except as provided in Section 5 of this Act, the determination that a vote is an
24 unauthorized vote may only be made by the chamber of the General Assembly
25 that appointed the delegate or alternate delegate who cast the vote in question.
26 (6) A vote determined to be an unauthorized vote shall be considered invalid.
27 (7) A delegate or alternate delegate who casts a vote determined to be an
