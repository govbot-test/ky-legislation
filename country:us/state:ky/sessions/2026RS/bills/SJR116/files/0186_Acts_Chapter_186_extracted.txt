Kentucky University to coordinate a search for actionable solutions to physician shortages and to explore and expand
health care opportunities in medically underserved areas in collaboration with community, state agency, professional
associations, and other stakeholders.
WHEREAS, over 75 percent of physicians practice in urban areas in Kentucky, leaving many counties with a
shortage of physicians; and
WHEREAS, the United States Department of Health and Human Services has designated 40 counties as health
professional shortage areas for primary care providers; and
WHEREAS, without local access to physicians, individuals in medically underserved areas are often
compelled to travel long distances to other counties for health care, which may involve transportation and financial
barriers and result in individuals going without care; and
WHEREAS, regular primary care physician visits serve as the first line in diagnosing illness and have been
associated with a reduced number of hospitalizations and emergency room department visits; and
WHEREAS, the Association of American Medical Colleges reports that 57 percent of medical residents
continued practicing medicine in their training state; and
WHEREAS, 38 percent of physicians practice medicine in the same state where they completed undergraduate
medical education, and the rate is higher for public undergraduate medical school graduates, 46 percent; and
WHEREAS, according to the American Medical Association, the highest state retention rates are for family
medicine, 64.6 percent, psychiatry, 64.5 percent, and pediatrics, 60 percent; and
WHEREAS, while some efforts have been made to increase medical residency placements and admissions into
medical school in Kentucky, these efforts have not been nearly enough to meet the need; and
WHEREAS, greater efforts could be taken to encourage medical school graduates to complete their
residencies in medically underserved areas so they might be compelled to continue practicing medicine and living
there; and
WHEREAS, opportunities to expand health care access in medically underserved areas may include health
care workforce diversification, innovative technology-enabled solutions and support, and attention to non-clinical
health care needs and community-based care;
NOW, THEREFORE,
Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
Section 1. (1) The General Assembly directs the University of Kentucky, the University of Louisville,
and Eastern Kentucky University to coordinate a search for actionable solutions to the physician shortages and to
explore and expand health care opportunities in collaboration with community, state agency, professional
associations, and other stakeholders. The search to find actionable solutions and explore opportunities to expand
health care access shall focus on the:
(a) Shortage of primary and specialty care physicians in medically underserved areas;
(b) Chronic lack of access to physician care by medically underserved populations;
(c) Retaining of physicians in medically underserved areas; and
(d) Workforce, technological, and community-based opportunities to increase access to care.
(2) The search and exploration shall include an examination of:
(a) Current actions being taken;
(b) Possibilities for building on current actions; and
(c) Innovative actions that may be taken, including but not limited to:

shortage of physicians and other clinicians, resulting in insufficient workforce capacity and impacting access to
health care across the state, and strategic action is necessary to expand access to care. Training models at universities

communities in which they trained to establish their practice, specifically in communities where primary academic
training activities will occur and will evolve into a collaborative partnership with local and regional public and
private higher education institutions and health care providers to deliver education and clinical training.
(2) The General Assembly authorizes the University of Kentucky to develop a feasibility study to explore
the persistent and growing shortage of physicians and other clinicians in collaboration with all health care workforce
partners in and around the Owensboro region to take advantage of HealthForce Kentucky.
(3) The feasibility study may be provided to the General Assembly by December 1, 2026. Based upon the
feasibility study, the General Assembly shall consider funding of up to $10,000,000 in the 2027 Regular Session to
address the findings of the feasibility study.
Signed by Governor April 17, 2026.
