1 AN ACT relating to the transportation of forestry products.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 189 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section, "primary forest product" means lumber and other
6 material, including but not limited to sawdust, wood chips, bark, slabs, and logs,
7 originating from their points of origin to first market.
8 (2) Notwithstanding KRS 189.269, and subject to the limitations in subsection (3) of
9 this section, trucks transporting primary forest products on a state-maintained
10 highway within one hundred (100) miles of their point of origin may operate at
11 the following weights:
12 (a) Ninety thousand (90,000) pounds for a truck with two (2) rear axles in
13 tandem arrangement that are spaced forty-two (42) inches or more apart
14 and less than ninety-six (96) inches apart; and
15 (b) One hundred thousand (100,000) pounds for a truck with three (3) rear
16 axles that are spaced forty-two (42) inches or more apart and less than one
17 hundred twenty (120) inches apart.
18 (3) This section shall not apply to:
19 (a) The interstate highway system; or
20 (b) Any bridge with a posted weight limit less than the adjoining highway.
