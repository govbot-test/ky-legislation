1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of Jeremiah
2 Parsons.
3 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons, of Prestonsburg, Kentucky, was born on July 1,
4 1984, to Rudy Parsons, Jr., and Alice Jacobs Parsons; and
5 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons was the epitome of kindness; and
6 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons left behind a legacy in both tourism and the arts in
7 his community; and
8 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons was the executive director of Paintsville Tourism,
9 and his love for Paintsville and unwavering dedication to his community will never be
10 forgotten; and
11 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons was the director of Main Street Players, where he
12 was dedicated to nurturing young performers and building a theatre community rooted in
13 creativity, confidence, and kindness; and
14 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons encouraged young performers to find their voices,
15 and instill in them courage and love for the arts that will stay with them for a lifetime;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons poured his heart into serving Paintsville and
18 uplifting the people and places he cared so deeply about; and
19 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons passed away on January 15, 2026, and he will be
20 missed by all who knew and loved him; and
21 WHEREAS, Jeremiah Parsons is survived by his brothers, Mickey Rudolph (Sarah)
22 Parsons, Andrew Parsons, Matthew (Glenda) Parsons, Joshua (Jessica) Parsons, Thomas
23 Parsons, Nathaniel Lee Dye, and Shane Combs; Papaw and Mamaw, Eugene and Luveda
24 Jacobs; best friends, Ali Marie Meyer, Tyler Green, Rachel Crider, and Myssi Cope; and
25 a host of nieces, nephews, aunts, uncles, family, and friends who will miss his presence in
26 their lives;
27 NOW, THEREFORE,
