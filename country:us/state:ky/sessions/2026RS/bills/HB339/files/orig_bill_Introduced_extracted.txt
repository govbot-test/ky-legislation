Section 1:
UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 248
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 36 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section:
6 (a) "Employee" means any individual employed by any employer; and
7 (b) "Employer":
8 1. Means any person, either individual, corporation, partnership, agency,
9 or firm who employs an employee and includes any person, either
10 individual, corporation, partnership, agency, or firm acting directly or
11 indirectly in the interest of an employer in relation to an employee;
12 and
13 2. Includes cities, counties, urban-county governments, charter county
14 governments, unified local governments, consolidated local
15 governments, and special districts.
16 (2) Subject to subsection (4) of this section, an employer shall not discriminate
17 against, discipline, or discharge an employee because:
18 (a) The employee is a member of the Civil Air Patrol; or
19 (b) The employee is absent from work when:
20 1. The absence is for the purpose of responding as a member of the Civil
21 Air Patrol to:
22 a. An emergency declared by the Governor or the President of the
23 United States; or
24 b. A mission directed from the Air Force Rescue Coordination
25 Center;
26 2. The employee gives his or her employer as much notice as possible of
27 the dates he or she will be absent to serve with the Civil Air Patrol

1 during the emergency; and
2 3. The employee provides his or her employer with verification from the
3 Civil Air Patrol of the emergency need for his or her volunteer service.
4 (3) Any employee who is a member of the Civil Air Patrol and was absent under the
5 conditions set out in subsection (2)(b) of this section, upon his or her return from
6 emergency Civil Air Patrol service, shall be permitted to return to his or her
7 former position of employment with the seniority, status, pay, or any other rights
8 or benefits he or she would have had if he or she had not been absent.
9 (4) This section shall not prohibit an employer from:
10 (a) Treating the time the employee is absent because of emergency Civil Air
11 Patrol service as unpaid time off; and
12 (b) Complying with a collective bargaining agreement or employee benefit plan
13 entered into prior to the effective date of this Act.
14 Section 2. KRS 61.394 is amended to read as follows:
15 (1) (a) All officers and employees of this state, or of any department or agency
16 thereof who are members of the National Guard or of any reserve component
17 of the Armed Forces of the United States, or of the reserve corps of the United
18 States Public Health Service, shall be entitled to leave of absence from their
19 respective duties, without loss of time, pay, regular leave, impairment of
20 efficiency rating, or of any other rights or benefits to which they are entitled,
21 while:
22 1.[(a)] In the performance of duty or training in the service of a state or of
23 the United States under competent orders as specified in this section;
24 2.[(b)] Physically disabled as a result of an injury, illness, or disease
25 incurred or aggravated in the line of duty while performing active-duty
26 or inactive-duty training; or
27 3.[(c)] Entitled to incapacitation pay pursuant to 37 U.S.C. sec. 204.
