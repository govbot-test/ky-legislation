1 AN ACT relating to geoengineering.
2 WHEREAS, "geoengineering" means the intentional manipulation of the
3 environment, through an atmospheric polluting intervention, to effect changes to the
4 earth's atmosphere or surface, including but not limited to the practices of weather
5 modification, solar radiation modification, stratospheric aerosol injection, and cloud
6 seeding; and
7 WHEREAS, geoengineering activities endanger human health and safety and the
8 environment, threaten air, water, soil, and wildlife resources, disrupt agricultural
9 operations, and potentially interfere with aviation, state security, and the economy of the
10 Commonwealth; and
11 WHEREAS, pursuant to the Tenth Amendment of the Constitution of the United
12 States and the inherent right for Kentucky citizens to engage in thoughtful deliberation
13 and determine public policy by voting, the citizens of the Commonwealth do not consent
14 to any unconstitutional actions or efforts made by the federal government or international
15 bodies that release atmospheric contaminants into the Commonwealth's atmosphere
16 through geoengineering, solar radiation modification, cloud seeding, weather
17 modification, or any other means; and
18 WHEREAS, as recognized by the World Meteorological Organization in guidelines
19 it adopted in 2017, weather modification activities should not be undertaken without
20 considering the high levels of uncertainty involved and the potential harm that could
21 result; and
22 WHEREAS, to preserve the safe and healthful uses of the Commonwealth's
23 atmosphere for its people, environment, wildlife, and agriculture, and to improve
24 beneficial climate efforts, it is necessary to prohibit geoengineering, including but not
25 limited to solar radiation modification, and to provide for enforcement and penalties for
26 violative activities;
27 NOW, THEREFORE,

1 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
2 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 512 IS CREATED TO
3 READ AS FOLLOWS:
4 (1) As used in this section:
5 (a) "Atmospheric contaminant":
6 1. Means any type of aerosol, biological, nonbiological, or hazardous
7 agent, chaff, chemical, chemical compound, genetically modified
8 agent, metal, radioactive material, substance, vapor, electromagnetic
9 radiation or field, mechanical vibration, particulate of any size, or any
10 air pollutant regulated by the Commonwealth, and any combination
11 thereof; and
12 2. Does not include engine exhaust from an aircraft using unadulterated
13 certified aviation fuel;
14 (b) "Atmospheric polluting intervention" means any manipulation or
15 interference with earth's natural systems or processes by altering
16 atmospheric or environmental conditions by:
17 1. Stratospheric aerosol injection;
18 2. Cloud seeding;
19 3. Solar radiation modification; or
20 4. The intentional release of an atmospheric contaminant from an
21 airborne source that may have harmful consequences on human
22 health, the environment, or agriculture, excluding any release or
23 emission from a ground-level source including but not limited to an
24 industrial or commercial activity or facility, residence, vehicle, or
25 agricultural operation;
26 (c) "Chaff" means aluminum-coated silica glass fibers, typically dispersed in
27 bundles containing millions of inhalable fibers, which break apart and fall

1 (j) "Stratospheric aerosol injection" means the release of reflective sulfate or
2 other aerosol substances in the stratosphere by high-altitude planes,
3 balloons, high-altitude blimps, artillery, or any other means; and
4 (k) "Weather modification" means the changing, controlling, or interfering
5 with or attempting to change, control, or interfere with the natural
6 development of cloud forms, precipitation, barometric pressure,
7 temperature, conductivity or other electromagnetic or sonic characteristics
8 of the atmosphere.
9 (2) A person is guilty of criminal atmospheric pollution when he or she intentionally
10 or wantonly engages in any atmospheric polluting intervention.
11 (3) Criminal atmospheric pollution shall be a Class D felony. Notwithstanding KRS
12 534.030 and 534.050, and in addition to any other penalties that may apply, a
13 person guilty of criminal atmospheric pollution shall pay a civil penalty of not
14 less than five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000). Each day that a person
15 engages in criminal atmospheric pollution shall be considered a separate offense.
16 (4) All peace officers of the Commonwealth shall enforce the requirements of this
17 section, and any person may make a complaint to any peace officer or local law
18 enforcement official relating to any suspected violation of this section.
19 (5) If any criminal atmospheric pollution has been approved, explicitly or implicitly,
20 by a federal government agency or official, the arresting officer shall issue a
21 notice to the appropriate federal agency or official that the criminal atmospheric
22 pollution intervention cannot lawfully be carried out within or over the
23 Commonwealth.
