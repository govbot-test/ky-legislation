1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of Bobbie
2 Lou Rasnick.
3 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick was born on December 25, 1929, to William
4 Harrison and America Ratliff Goff in Williamson, West Virginia; and
5 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick was a 1947 graduate of Elkhorn City High
6 School and was salutatorian of her class; and
7 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick was a devoted member of Pikeville United
8 Methodist Church and served as church treasurer for decades; and
9 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick was known throughout her community for her
10 lifelong passion for learning, traveling, and being outdoors; and
11 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick worked as a bookkeeper for a number of local
12 businesses, including First National Bank and Chrisman Insurance and was an avid
13 advocate for the City of Pikeville and the state of Kentucky. Bobbie Lou Rasnick broadly
14 served community over the years; and
15 WHEREAS, following her retirement in 1994, Bobbie Lou Rasnick began traveling
16 the nation and the world seeing and experiencing different locations and cultures; and
17 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick was known for her passion towards conservative
18 causes which led her to serve as treasurer of the Pike County Republican Women's Club;
19 and
20 WHEREAS, Bobbie Lou Rasnick was preceded in death by her husband, Arthur
21 Blake "Shorty" Rasnick in 1975, and leaves behind her sister Juanita (John) Wilson; four
22 children John (Carolyn) Rasnick, Linda (Steve) Ratliff, David (Fredia) Rasnick, and
23 Peggy (Phil) Justice; eight grandchildren, Kati Rasnick, Joseph (Kathryn) Rasnick,
24 Rachael (Tommy) Benz, Isaac Ratliff, Matthew (Kate) Rasnick, Stephanie Ratliff, Will
25 Rasnick, and Blake Justice; three great-grandchildren, Ann Ashby Rasnick, Harrison
26 Rasnick, and Jackson Benz. Also, left behind to miss her daily is her neighbor, nephew,
27 and "adopted son," John Michael Johnson, and the lovely ladies that helped walk her
