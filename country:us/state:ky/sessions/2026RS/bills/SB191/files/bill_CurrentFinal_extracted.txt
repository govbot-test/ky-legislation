2 Child Care Incentive Pilot Program.
3 WHEREAS, providing performance-based incentives to diverse types of early
4 childhood education and child-care providers leads to the expansion of services across the
5 Commonwealth; and
6 WHEREAS, providing performance-based incentives to low-income parents of
7 children not receiving services from early childhood education and child-care providers,
8 Head Start, or public Pre-K is a way to encourage parents to prepare their children for
9 kindergarten; and
10 WHEREAS, providing performance-based incentives increases the number of
11 children entering school kindergarten ready, which positively impacts future educational
12 attainment; and
13 WHEREAS, providing performance-based incentives encourages early childhood
14 education and child-care providers to provide services for children with special needs;
15 and
16 WHEREAS, providing performance-based incentives would ensure positive growth
17 and improved quality of care for infant and toddler care programs;
18 NOW, THEREFORE,
19 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
20 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 199 IS CREATED TO
21 READ AS FOLLOWS:
22 (1) As used in this section:
23 (a) "Cabinet" means the Cabinet for Health and Family Services;
24 (b) "Type I child-care center" means a child-care center licensed by the cabinet
25 to regularly provide child care services for:
26 1. Four (4) or more children in a nonresidential setting; or
27 2. Thirteen (13) or more children in a designated space separate from the

2 (c) "Type II child-care center" means a child-care center that is the primary
3 residence of the licensee in which child care is regularly provided for at
4 least seven (7) but not more than twelve (12) children, including children
5 related to the licensee; and
6 (d) "University" means the University of Kentucky.
7 (2) By August 1, 2026, the university shall establish and operate the Kentucky
8 Kindergarten Readiness Performance-Based Child Care Incentive Pilot Program,
9 which shall operate from August 1, 2027, until July 31, 2029.
10 (3) The pilot program shall:
11 (a) Determine how to provide performance-based incentives to diverse types of
12 early childhood education providers, child-care providers, and low-income
13 parents in order to increase the number of children in the Commonwealth
14 who are assessed as kindergarten ready;
15 (b) Determine how to best assess applicants and distribute moneys;
16 (c) Develop a written plan and implement assessment processes to determine
17 kindergarten readiness for children in the Commonwealth. The university
18 shall convene a working group to assist in the development of the written
19 plan and assessment processes;
20 (d) Ensure that participants in the pilot program represent geographically and
21 socioeconomically diverse areas of the Commonwealth;
22 (e) Evaluate the impact of performance-based incentives implemented through
23 the pilot program using a rigorous design framework that supports causal
24 inferences;
25 (f) Design, identify, and make available resources for parents to assist them in
26 preparing children for kindergarten readiness;
27 (g) Determine how to award funds, including but not limited to direct payments

1 or refundable tax credits, to licensed Type I and Type II child-care centers;
2 cabinet-regulated early childhood education providers, certified child-care
3 homes, and registered providers; and single or married parents who qualify
4 for the child-care subsidy administered by the cabinet pursuant to KRS
5 199.8994. To be eligible for funds:
6 1. A provider or a home shall maintain services for infants and toddlers
7 at a ratio established by the Division of Child Care within the cabinet;
8 2. A provider or a home shall maintain and submit accurate and detailed
9 records of care for any child; and
10 3. A parent shall submit records to verify he or she meets the
11 requirements to receive child care subsidy funds under KRS 199.8994;
12 (h) Establish a standard for awarding a two thousand dollar ($2,000) one (1)
13 time award, including but not limited to direct payments or refundable tax
14 credits, per child once a child is designated by the assessment processes as
15 kindergarten ready as follows:
16 1. A full award to a licensed Type I or Type II child-care center or
17 cabinet-regulated early childhood education provider, certified child-
18 care home, or registered provider who meets the requirements of
19 paragraph (g) of this subsection if the kindergarten-ready child
20 received full-time services from the center or provider the two (2) years
21 prior to the assessment;
22 2. A full award to a single or married parent who meets the requirements
23 of paragraph (g) of this subsection if the kindergarten-ready child was
24 cared for in the home the two (2) years prior to the assessment,
25 regardless of the utilization of a babysitter;
26 3. A full award to a licensed Type I or Type II child-care center or
27 cabinet-regulated early childhood education provider, certified child-
