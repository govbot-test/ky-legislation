2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 314.142 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) The Kentucky Board of Nursing shall promulgate administrative regulations
5 pursuant to KRS Chapter 13A to create a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner Program.
6 These administrative regulations shall address, at a minimum:
7 (a) Educational requirements for sexual assault nurse examiners and statewide
8 standards for provision of the education;
9 (b) The application process through which registered nurses who submit
10 documentation of required education and clinical experience and who remit
11 the designated application fee may apply to the board to be credentialed as a
12 "Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner";
13 (c) Continuing education requirements for maintenance of the sexual assault
14 nurse examiner credential;
15 (d) Methods of monitoring overall program implementation; and
16 (e) Procedures for obtaining input from the Sexual Assault Response Team
17 Advisory Committee established under KRS Chapter 403 prior to proposing
18 amendments to any provision of the administrative regulations promulgated
19 under this section or KRS 314.131.
20 (2) Any person in this state who holds a credential as a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner
21 as defined in KRS 314.011(14) shall have the right to use the title "Sexual Assault
22 Nurse Examiner" and the abbreviation "SANE". No other person shall assume the
23 title or use the abbreviation or any other words, letters, signs, or figures to indicate
24 that the person using the same is a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner.
25 (3) The board shall employ a health professional as a statewide sexual assault nurse
26 examiner (SANE) coordinator to support the logistical operations of the SANE
27 Program and individual SANEs credentialed under this section.

1 (4) The board shall establish a description, essential duties, and responsibilities of
2 the SANE coordinator position that shall include but not be limited to:
3 (a) Identifying and recruiting potential SANEs across the state;
4 (b) Organizing and facilitating comprehensive SANE training programs;
5 (c) Establishing and maintaining relationships with key stakeholders such as
6 hospitals, law enforcement agencies, rape crisis advocates, and others; and
7 (d) Increasing SANE Program collaboration by creating opportunities for
8 networking and building rapport among SANE training programs.
9 (5) The board shall collaborate with the Kentucky Hospital Association, the Cabinet
10 for Health and Family Services, the Sexual Assault Response Team Advisory
11 Committee, and any other entities deemed appropriate by the board, to develop a
12 statewide strategic plan that utilizes a regional model to ensure SANE coverage
13 for all Kentucky hospitals. The strategic plan shall be submitted to the Legislative
14 Research Commission for referral to the Interim Joint Committee on Health
15 Services by December 1, 2026.
16 (6) The board shall develop a state registry of SANEs and make it accessible from the
17 board's website by registered SANEs and Kentucky hospitals. The registry shall
18 be operational by December 1, 2026.
19 (7) The board shall notify each newly registered SANE that the state registry of
20 SANEs is available on the board's website as required in subsection (6) of this
21 section.
