Section 1:
UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 889
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS 164.740 TO 164.7891 IS CREATED
4 TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) To ensure the availability of paramedics in the coal-producing counties of the
6 Commonwealth, the General Assembly hereby establishes the Coal County
7 Paramedic Scholarship Program to provide eligible Kentucky students the
8 opportunity to attend a participating postsecondary institution and to become
9 licensed paramedics in the Commonwealth, provided that the scholarship
10 recipient agrees to render qualified service as a paramedic in a coal-producing
11 county for four (4) years upon program completion in accordance with this
12 section.
13 (2) As used in this section:
14 (a) "Coal-producing county" has the same meaning as in KRS 42.4592;
15 (b) "Eligible program of study" means a paramedic program approved by the
16 authority and offered by a participating institution accredited by the
17 Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs; and
18 (c) "Qualified service" means full-time employment in a coal-producing county
19 of the Commonwealth of Kentucky as a licensed paramedic for a majority of
20 the calendar year.
21 (3) The authority may award scholarships, to the extent funds are available for that
22 purpose, to any person who:
23 (a) Is a Kentucky resident;
24 (b) Is considered a permanent resident of a coal-producing county for at least
25 one (1) year immediately preceding July 1 of the academic year in which the
26 scholarship is made;
27 (c) Is a United States citizen as determined by the participating institution in

3 (d) Is enrolled or accepted for enrollment in an eligible program of study at a
4 participating institution on a full-time basis;
5 (e) Agrees to render four (4) years of qualified service in a coal-producing
6 county of the Commonwealth upon program completion; and
7 (f) Agrees to sign a promissory note as evidence of the scholarship awarded
8 and the obligation to repay the scholarship amount or render qualified
9 service as agreed in lieu of payment.
10 (4) (a) Notwithstanding KRS 164.753(3), the amount of the scholarship awarded
11 under subsection (3) of this section shall not exceed one hundred percent
12 (100%) of the in-state tuition and fees required for participation in the
13 eligible program of study at a participating institution. The authority shall
14 establish, by administrative regulation promulgated in accordance with KRS
15 Chapter 13A, a procedure for awarding scholarships should funding be
16 insufficient to award scholarships to all eligible students. The authority may
17 also, by administrative regulation, establish scholarship amounts based on
18 demonstration of initial financial need by eligible students.
19 (b) The actual amount of the scholarship awarded to each eligible student by
20 the authority for each semester shall be based on the amount of funds
21 available and the criteria established under paragraph (a) of this
22 subsection.
23 (5) (a) The authority shall require each student receiving a scholarship under this
24 section to execute a promissory note as evidence of the obligation.
25 (b) The recipient shall render four (4) years of qualified service in a coal-
26 producing county upon completion of a paramedic program. Upon
27 completion of four (4) years of qualified service in a coal-producing county,

1 the authority shall cancel all promissory notes.
2 (c) If a recipient fails to complete an eligible program of study, or fails to
3 render qualified service as agreed in this subsection, the recipient shall be
4 liable for the total repayment of the sum of all outstanding promissory notes
5 and accrued interest.
6 (6) Any person who is in default on any obligation to the authority under any
7 program administered by the authority under KRS 164.740 to 164.785 shall not
8 be awarded a scholarship under this section, or have a promissory note canceled,
9 until all financial obligations to the authority are satisfied, except that
10 ineligibility for this reason may be waived by the authority for cause.
11 (7) A repayment obligation imposed by this section shall not be voidable by reason of
12 the age of the recipient at the time of executing the promissory note.
13 (8) Failure to meet repayment obligations imposed by this section shall be cause for
14 the revocation of the scholarship recipient's paramedic license, subject to the
15 procedures set forth in KRS Chapter 311A.
16 (9) Notwithstanding KRS 164.753(3), the authority shall establish by administrative
17 regulation procedures for the administration of this program, including but not
18 limited to the execution of appropriate contracts and promissory notes,
19 cancellation of obligations, the rate of repayment, and deferment of repayment of
20 outstanding debt.
21 (10) Notwithstanding any other statute to the contrary, the maximum interest rate
22 applicable to repayment of a promissory note under this section shall be twelve
23 percent (12%) per annum, except that if a judgment is rendered to recover
24 payment, the judgment shall bear interest at the rate of five percent (5%) greater
25 than the rate actually charged on the promissory note.
26 (11) (a) There is hereby established in the State Treasury a trust and agency
27 account to be known as the coal county paramedic scholarship fund.

1 (b) The fund shall consist of amounts transferred from coal severance tax
2 receipts as provided in paragraph (e) of this subsection, state
3 appropriations, gifts, grants, and federal funds.
4 (c) The fund shall be administered by the authority.
5 (d) Amounts deposited in the fund shall be used for providing scholarships to
6 qualifying students studying to be a paramedic in schools in the
7 Commonwealth and for no other purpose.
8 (e) 1. Beginning July 1, 2027, receipts from the coal severance tax levied
9 under KRS 143.020 shall be transferred to the fund on an annual
10 basis in an amount not to exceed the lesser of:
11 a. One percent (1%) of the total annual coal severance tax
12 revenues collected under KRS 143.020; or
13 b. The amount necessary to provide full funding for all students
14 who qualify for a scholarship under this section, considering all
15 other resources available.

17 made as follows:
18 a. On or before August 1 of each year, sixty-five percent (65%) of

20 transferred to the fund; and
21 b. The remaining thirty-five percent (35%) shall be transferred on
22 or before December 1 of each year.
23 3. a. The amount transferred shall be based upon the prevailing
24 revenue estimate for coal severance tax receipts at the time each
25 transfer is made.

27 the prevailing revenue estimate for the year, the next August 1

7 economic assistance fund established in KRS 42.450 within ninety (90) days
8 of the end of the fiscal year.
9 (g) All moneys repaid to the authority under this section shall be added to the
