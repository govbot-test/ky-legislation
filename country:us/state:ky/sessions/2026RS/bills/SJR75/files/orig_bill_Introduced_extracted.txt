UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1836
1 Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
2 Section 1. The Public Service Commission is hereby directed to open one or
3 more administrative cases to investigate and identify measures utilities can use to
4 improve the affordability of utility service for low- and fixed-income individuals.
5 Section 2. The Public Service Commission shall invite and encourage
6 participation in the administrative case or cases from electric, gas, water, and wastewater
7 utilities to become parties to the administrative case, and engage with affected
8 communities and nonprofit advocacy organizations to ensure broad and representative
9 participation in both formal and informal proceedings and to inform the development of
10 recommendations.
11 Section 3. The administrative case or cases shall:
12 (1) Examine strategies to provide consistent access to utility service for low and
13 fixed-income customers;
14 (2) Examine strategies to protect consumers from payment delinquencies, late
15 fees, and disconnections that could threaten their health, safety, and well-being; and
16 (3) Evaluate potential solutions to address affordability such as rate design,
17 financial assistance programs, and other regulatory and nonregulatory approaches and
18 innovations.
19 Section 4. The Public Service Commission shall report to the Legislative
20 Research Commission no later than September 1, 2027, summarizing the findings and
21 recommendations arising from the administrative case or cases and identifying specific
22 measures that can be implemented by the General Assembly, the Public Service
23 commission, and various utilities to address affordability and disconnection.
24 Section 5. Whereas the steady increase in electricity and anticipated reduction
25 in 2027-2028 funding for the Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)
26 threatens the health and safety of vulnerable low- and fixed-income citizens, an
27 emergency is declared to exist, and this Joint Resolution shall take effect upon its passage
