1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION relating to the establishment of a Beauty
2 Industry Law Task Force to complete a detailed review of the current laws to ensure
3 alignment throughout the relevant statutes and administrative regulations.
4 WHEREAS, Kentucky regulates over 40,000 licensed cosmetologists, barbers, and
5 licensed facilities; and
6 WHEREAS, there have been numerous changes to the beauty industry's laws and
7 administrative regulations in the past few years; and
8 WHEREAS, the current Boards of Cosmetology and Barbering do not have the
9 personnel capacity to accomplish the necessary task of removing outdated or duplicative
10 information from the statutes and laws governing their practice;
11 NOW, THEREFORE,
12 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the General Assembly of the
13 Commonwealth of Kentucky, the Senate concurring therein:
14 Section 1. The Legislative Research Commission is hereby directed to create
15 the Beauty Industry Law Task Force to conduct a comprehensive review of current
16 cosmetology and barbering statutes and administrative regulations to identify outdated
17 information, duplicative material, and discrepancies.
18 Section 2. The Beauty Industry Law Task Force shall be composed of the
19 following members, with final membership of the task force being subject to the
20 consideration and approval of the Legislative Research Commission.
21 (1) Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of
22 the House of Representatives, one of whom shall be a member of the minority party, and
23 one of whom shall be designated by the Speaker of the House of Representatives as a co-
24 chair of the task force;
25 (2) Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, one of
26 whom shall be a member of the minority party, and one of whom shall be designated by
27 the President of the Senate as a co-chair of the task force;

1 (3) One board member from the Board of Cosmetology;
2 (4) One board member from the Board of Barbering;
3 (5) Two licensed professionals, each with at least 10 years of experience in the
4 industry; and
5 (6) Two beauty or barbering school owners.
6 Section 3. The Beauty Industry Law Task Force shall meet at least four times
7 during the 2026 Interim and shall submit its findings, legislative recommendations, or a
8 memorandum to the Legislative Research Commission no later than December 1, 2026. If
9 legislative recommendations are submitted, the Legislative Research Commission may
10 refer the recommendations to the appropriate committee or committees of jurisdiction in
11 advance of the 2027 Regular Session of the Kentucky General Assembly.
12 Section 4. Provisions of this Resolution to the contrary notwithstanding, the
13 Legislative Research Commission shall have the authority to alternatively assign the
14 issues specified herein to an interim joint committee or a subcommittee thereof, and to
15 designate a study completion date.
