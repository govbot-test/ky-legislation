1 A RESOLUTION recognizing The Links, Incorporated Day at the State Capitol.
2 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated is a highly regarded international nonprofit
3 organization dedicated to unity, friendship, and service; and
4 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated was established in 1946 and has since grown
5 to a current membership of 17,000 professional women of color in 299 chapters located
6 in 41 states, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of the Bahamas, and the United
7 Kingdom; and
8 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated is one of the nation’s oldest and largest
9 volunteer service organizations, consisting of extraordinary women of color who are
10 committed to the economic and cultural enrichment of African Americans and other
11 persons of African descent; and
12 WHEREAS, the members of The Links, Incorporated are influential leaders and
13 decision makers and include many distinguished women who have made a positive
14 impact on their communities and the world, including business and civic leaders,
15 mentors, activists, and volunteers; and
16 WHEREAS, members contribute more than one million documented hours of
17 community service annually, strengthening their communities and enhancing civic life
18 across the United States; and
19 WHEREAS, notable members of The Links, Incorporated include the 49th Vice
20 President of the United States, Kamala Harris; the civil rights leader and Children’s
21 Defense Fund founder, Marian Wright Edelman; and the first African American female
22 president of historically Black Spelman College, Johnetta Betsch Cole; and
23 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated has received awards from the United Nations
24 Association of New York and the Leon H. Sullivan Foundation in recognition of its
25 premier programs; and
26 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated has promoted civic engagement in state-level
27 politics and government, engaging with people of color serving as leaders and elected
