3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 65 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) The General Assembly finds and declares the following:
6 (a) The revitalization area shall include the voluntary participation of the
7 Kentucky counties, and the cities incorporated therein, that are defined as
8 rural based upon the Rural-Urban Continuum Codes 4 to 9 in alignment
9 with the United States Department of Agriculture Economic Research
10 Service’s metro and nonmetro designations;
11 (b) The revitalization area possesses a rich and proud history of social, cultural,
12 and economic contributions to the Commonwealth from its very beginning;
13 (c) Even though the revitalization area saw a significant decline in population
14 during the period 2011 through 2019, its population continues to be the
15 home to one million eight hundred fifty thousand (1,850,000) citizens who
16 live and work in the Commonwealth and desire to build a better future for
17 themselves, their families, and the generations that follow;
18 (d) The gross domestic product growth from 2001 to 2023 of eighteen percent
19 (18%) within the revitalization area was slower than the growth of forty-
20 nine percent (49%) in the thirty-five (35) urban areas;
21 (e) The citizens within the revitalization area currently, on average, earn
22 twenty-six percent (26%) less per capita than citizens within urban counties,
23 and citizens with a bachelor’s degree earn forty percent (40%) less;
24 (f) The revitalization area has a higher rate of home ownership than urban
25 areas, although the area also has triple the vacancy rate of urban areas;
26 (g) Agriculture and coal mining, both major contributors to the economy of the
27 revitalization area, have been declining for several years without
