Section 1:
CHAPTER 96 1
CHAPTER 96
( SB 104 )
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 519 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) As used in this section:
(a) "First responder" has the same meaning as in KRS 507.070, and includes a probation or parole
officer, university police officer, and airport police officer; and
(b) "Harass" means to intentionally engage in a course of conduct directed at a first responder which
causes or is intended to cause substantial emotional distress in the first responder and serves no
legitimate purpose.
(2) A person is guilty of impeding a first responder when, after receiving a verbal warning not to approach
from another person he or she knows or reasonably should know is a first responder who is engaged in the
lawful performance of his or her duties, the person knowingly violates the verbal warning and approaches
or remains within twenty-five (25) feet of the first responder with the intent to:
(a) Impede or interfere with the first responder's ability to perform the first responder's duties;
(b) Threaten the first responder with physical harm; or
(c) Harass the first responder.
(3) Impeding a first responder is a:
(a) Class B misdemeanor for the first offense;
(b) Class A misdemeanor for a second or third offense; and
(c) Class D felony for each subsequent offense.
Section 2. KRS 95A.292 is amended to read as follows:
(1) The commission shall establish the Alan "Chip" Terry Professional Development and Wellness Program for
firefighters and rescue squad members.
(2) As used in this section, "rescue squad member" means an individual who is a member of a rescue squad as
defined in KRS 39F.010.
(3) The program shall:
(a) Use seminar-based peer support and counseling services designed to reduce negative mental and
behavioral health outcomes; and
(b) Be offered to Kentucky professional and volunteer firefighters and rescue squad members in Kentucky
at least two (2) times each calendar year.
(4)[(3)] On a limited basis, the program may be offered to professional and volunteer firefighters and rescue
squad members from states other than Kentucky upon application to and approval by the executive director.
However, no Kentucky professional and volunteer firefighters or rescue squad members may be denied
admission to the program if professional and volunteer firefighters or rescue squad members from another
state are admitted to the program.
(5)[(4)] The commission shall promulgate administrative regulations in accordance with KRS Chapter 13A to
implement this section. The administrative regulations shall address, at a minimum:
(a) The required qualifications and duties of any person used by the commission to implement or
administer the program;
(b) The curriculum, programming, seminar type, and treatment modalities used in the program;
