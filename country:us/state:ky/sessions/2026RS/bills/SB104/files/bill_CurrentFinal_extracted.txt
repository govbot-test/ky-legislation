2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 519 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section:
6 (a) "First responder" has the same meaning as in KRS 507.070, and includes a
7 probation or parole officer, university police officer, and airport police
8 officer; and
9 (b) "Harass" means to intentionally engage in a course of conduct directed at a
10 first responder which causes or is intended to cause substantial emotional
11 distress in the first responder and serves no legitimate purpose.
12 (2) A person is guilty of impeding a first responder when, after receiving a verbal
13 warning not to approach from another person he or she knows or reasonably
14 should know is a first responder who is engaged in the lawful performance of his
15 or her duties, the person knowingly violates the verbal warning and approaches
16 or remains within twenty-five (25) feet of the first responder with the intent to:
17 (a) Impede or interfere with the first responder's ability to perform the first
18 responder's duties;
19 (b) Threaten the first responder with physical harm; or
20 (c) Harass the first responder.
21 (3) Impeding a first responder is a:
22 (a) Class B misdemeanor for the first offense;
23 (b) Class A misdemeanor for a second or third offense; and
24 (c) Class D felony for each subsequent offense.
25 Section 2. KRS 95A.292 is amended to read as follows:
26 (1) The commission shall establish the Alan "Chip" Terry Professional Development
27 and Wellness Program for firefighters and rescue squad members.

12 participating firefighter or rescue squad member is the holder of the
13 privilege.

15 and educational purposes.
16 (c) Any communication making an actual threat of physical violence against a
17 clearly identified or reasonably identifiable victim or an actual threat of some
18 specific violent act may be revealed by the program in order to prevent the
19 commission of any physical violence or violent act using the protocol
20 established in KRS 202A.400.
21 (8)[(6)] (a) There is hereby established in the State Treasury a restricted fund to be
22 known as the professional and volunteer firefighters professional development
23 and wellness program fund.
24 (b) The fund shall consist of moneys received from the Firefighters Foundation
25 Program Fund established in KRS 95A.220, grants, gifts, state appropriations,
26 and federal funds.
27 (c) The fund shall be administered by the commission.

1 (d) Amounts deposited in the fund shall be used only for administration of the
2 program.
3 (e) Notwithstanding KRS 45.229, fund amounts not expended at the close of a
4 fiscal year shall not lapse but shall be carried forward to the next fiscal year.
5 (f) Any interest earnings of the fund shall become a part of the fund and shall not
6 lapse.[
7 (g) Moneys deposited in the fund are hereby appropriated for the purposes set
8 forth in this section and shall not be appropriated or transferred by the General
9 Assembly for any other purposes.]
10 (9)[(7)] (a) For the purposes of this subsection, "critical incident" means any event
11 that has a stressful impact sufficient enough to overwhelm a firefighter or
12 rescue squad member's[ firefighter's] usual coping strategies. These events
13 may include:
14 1. A fire or vehicle crash resulting in serious injury or death to a first
15 responder or citizen;
16 2. A firefighter or rescue squad member being the victim of a felonious
17 assault;
18 3. The death of a colleague or partner;
19 4. A death of, or serious injury to, a person in the medical care of the
20 firefighter or rescue squad member;
21 5. The severe injury to, or death of, a child, particularly if the firefighter or
22 rescue squad member has a child of or near the same age; or
23 6. An incident involving multiple deaths or injuries in a short amount of
24 time.
25 (b) Any firefighter or rescue squad member involved directly in a critical
26 incident may take up to forty-eight (48) hours of leave immediately following
27 a critical incident. This leave may commence upon:

1 1. The completion of that firefighter or rescue squad
2 member's[firefighter's] shift encompassing the critical incident, or when
3 all necessary administrative procedures relating to a critical incident
4 have been completed; and
5 2. The firefighter or rescue squad member informs his or her supervisor.
6 (c) For regular firefighters, this leave may be unpaid or paid leave. The pay status
7 is to be determined by the firefighter's employment contract, collective labor
8 agreement if any, or by written departmental policy. This subsection shall not
9 be construed to set aside any employment contract, labor agreement, or
10 departmental policies that grant more than forty-eight (48) hours of leave
11 following an officer involved critical incident.
12 (d) For volunteer firefighters, this leave may be unpaid or paid leave. The pay
13 status is to be determined by the firefighter's written departmental policy.
14 (e) For rescue squad members, this leave may be unpaid or paid leave. The pay
15 status is to be determined by a written policy of the Division of Emergency
16 Management.
17 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 39F IS CREATED TO
18 READ AS FOLLOWS:
19 The division shall enter into an agreement with the Kentucky Fire Commission to
20 provide rescue squad members with access to the program under Section 2 of this Act.
21 Section 4. Sections 2 to 3 of this Act may be cited as Gavin's Rights.
