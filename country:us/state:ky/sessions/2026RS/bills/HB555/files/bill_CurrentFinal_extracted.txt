2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 158.854 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) The Kentucky Board of Education shall promulgate an administrative regulation in
5 accordance with KRS Chapter 13A to specify the minimum nutritional standards
6 for all foods and beverages that are sold outside the National School Breakfast and
7 National School Lunch programs, whether in vending machines, school stores,
8 canteens, or a la carte cafeteria sales. Minimum nutritional standards shall be based
9 on the most recent edition of the United States Department of Agriculture's Dietary
10 Guidelines for Americans. The administrative regulation shall address serving size,
11 sugar, and fat content of the foods and beverages. School districts may impose more
12 stringent standards than the standards implemented under the administrative
13 regulation. A school shall follow the minimum standards specified in the
14 administrative regulation unless a waiver has been requested under KRS 156.161 by
15 the school district for the school from the Kentucky Board of Education.
16 Notwithstanding the duration of a waiver granted under KRS 156.161, any waiver
17 approved by the Kentucky Board of Education relating to this section shall be
18 reviewed on an annual basis.
19 (2) As used in this section:
20 (a) "Competitive food" means any food or beverage item sold in competition with
21 the National School Breakfast and National School Lunch programs. The term
22 does not include any food or beverage sold a la carte in the cafeteria;
23 (b) "School day" means the period of time between the arrival of the first student
24 at the school building and the end of the last instructional period;[ and]
25 (c) "School-day-approved beverage" means water, one hundred percent (100%)
26 fruit juice, lowfat milk, and any beverage that contains no more than ten (10)
27 grams of sugar per serving; and

1 (d) "Student-based enterprise" means a district-approved program operated by
2 a school district's students as part of a course designed to provide students
3 with knowledge and experience of the operation of a business.
4 (3) No school may sell competitive foods or beverages from the time of the arrival of
5 the first student at the school building until thirty (30) minutes after the last lunch
6 period.
7 (4) Only school-day-approved beverages shall be sold in elementary schools during the
8 school day in vending machines, school stores, canteens, or fundraisers that sell
9 beverages by students, teachers, or groups.
10 (5) Nothing in this section or KRS 158.850 shall be construed to limit the sale of any
11 foods or beverages by fundraisers off school property.
12 (6) Notwithstanding subsection (3) of this section, a local board of education may
13 identify a student-based enterprise to sell competitive foods throughout the school
14 day. The local board may establish policies for the operation of the student-based
15 enterprise and for the use of the proceeds from the operation of the student-based
16 enterprise. Competitive foods sold by a student-based enterprise shall comply with
17 the minimum nutritional standards established under subsection (1) of this
18 section.
