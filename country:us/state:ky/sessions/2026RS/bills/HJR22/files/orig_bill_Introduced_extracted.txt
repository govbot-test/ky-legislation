1 A JOINT RESOLUTION directing the Kentucky Transportation Cabinet to study
2 the efficacy of planting mint along highways to mitigate motor vehicle collisions with
3 wildlife.
4 WHEREAS, in 2024, there were 3,406 highway collisions in the Commonwealth
5 that involved a motor vehicle striking a deer, the highest recorded number in the last five
6 years; and
7 WHEREAS, each year, the Commonwealth continues to see increased collisions
8 with deer and other wildlife, which often result in fatalities, serious injuries, and severe
9 damage to motor vehicles; and
10 WHEREAS, the Kentucky Transportation Cabinet conducted a wildlife-vehicle
11 collision reduction pilot study in central Kentucky that was completed in 2025; and
12 WHEREAS, the initial conclusions of that study found that hunting pressures, road
13 location, vehicle noise, and adjacent roadway slope are all significant factors to consider
14 in creating an effective wildlife-vehicle collision mitigation plan; and
15 WHEREAS, Mentha, also known as mint, has been used for generations by farmers
16 and land managers to deter deer from fields and crops; and
17 WHEREAS, mint could have benefits if strategically planted along highways in the
18 Commonwealth to deter deer and other wildlife from crossing, potentially preventing
19 fatal accidents;
20 NOW, THEREFORE,
21 Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
22 Section 1. (1) The Kentucky Transportation Cabinet, in conjunction with
23 the Kentucky Department of Fish and Wildlife Resources, is directed to study, examine,
24 and evaluate if a program to plant mint along highways in the Commonwealth would:
25 (a) Adequately deter deer and other wildlife from crossing highways;
26 (b) Reduce wildlife collisions and fatalities; and
27 (c) Have a harmful or positive impact to the natural ecosystem.
