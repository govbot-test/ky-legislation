1 A RESOLUTION adopting the Rules of Procedure for the 2026 Regular Session of
2 the Senate.
3 Be it resolved by the Senate of the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of
4 Kentucky:
5 Section 1. The following Rules of Procedure are adopted to govern the 2026
6 Regular Session of the Senate.
7 Rule 1. Hours of Meeting. The Senate shall meet at the call of the members.
8 The official time shall be governed by the clock over the main entrance to the Senate
9 Chamber.
10 Rule 2. Quorum. A majority of the Senators elected to the Senate shall
11 constitute a quorum. If a quorum is not present at the time fixed for a meeting of the
12 Senate, four Senators may adjourn or recess from day to day or from time to time and
13 eight Senators may order a call of the Senate and send for absent Senators.
14 Rule 3. Call of the Senate. Upon a call of the Senate, the Clerk shall call the
15 roll, then call the absentees again. The doors of the Senate Chamber shall then be closed
16 and the absentees not excused by the Senate may be sent for and arrested by the Sergeant
17 at Arms.
18 The Senate shall determine upon what conditions they shall be discharged from
19 arrest. Senators who voluntarily appear shall be immediately admitted to the floor of the
20 Senate and their names entered upon the Journal as present, unless the Senate otherwise
21 directs.

2 Rule 7. Withdrawal of Motions. Every oral motion after it has been stated by

8 or in the Senate, when not a privileged question, shall be propounded in the order in

10 remote date shall be put first.

19 7. To limit or extend limits of debate.
20 8. To postpone to a fixed time.
21 9. To lay on the Clerk’s desk.
22 10. To refer or commit.
23 11. To amend.
24 12. To postpone indefinitely.

1 question, to limit or extend limits of debate, to postpone to a time certain, to lay on the
2 Clerk’s desk, to refer or commit or to postpone indefinitely shall not be in order on the
3 same day, upon the same question, and at the same status unless other business

6 Rule 10. Motion to Adjourn. A motion to adjourn, to take a recess, or a motion
7 to adjourn to a time certain, shall always be in order, except when a Senator is speaking,
8 while a vote is being taken, or when the Committee on Committees is reporting; subject,
9 however, to the limitations set out in Rule 9.
10 Rule 11. Motion to Table. The adoption of the motion to table, under these rules,
11 defeats the subject matter under consideration. The reconsideration of the motion to table
12 shall require approval of a majority of the members elected.
13 Rule 12. Previous Question. When the previous question has been ordered, a
14 vote shall be taken immediately upon the pending measure and any pending amendments
15 as are in order. The effect of the previous question shall be to put an end to all debate, to
16 prevent the offering of additional amendments and to bring the Senate to an immediate
17 vote upon the measure and amendments aforesaid. The previous question may be ordered
18 by a majority of the Senators elected. On the call of the roll, no Senator shall be allowed
19 to speak more than three minutes to explain a vote and shall not speak at all if the
20 question is not a debatable question.
21 Rule 13. Motion to Set the Limits of Debate. A motion to extend a time limit
22 for debate on a measure beyond that permitted under Rule 12 shall be in order unless the
23 previous question shall have been ordered on the measure. The time limit set for debate
24 under this rule shall be allotted by the President evenly between the opponents of the
25 measure and the proponents of the measure. Adoption of a motion under this rule does
26 not prevent the offering of additional amendments.
27 Rule 14. Motion to Reconsider. A motion to reconsider a vote shall not be in

12 Rule 16. Motion to Strike Out Enacting Clause. A motion to amend by striking
13 out the enacting words of a bill or resolution shall have precedence over a motion to
14 amend, and, if adopted, shall have the same effect as though the bill or resolution were
15 regularly voted upon and rejected.
16 Rule 17. Motion to Separate Part of a Measure. A motion to commit,
17 recommit, or postpone a part of a measure so as to separate that part of the measure from
18 the remainder shall not be in order.
19 Rule 18. Postponement of Measures. When a measure shall have been
20 postponed indefinitely it shall not be in order again during the session.
21 Rule 19. Reading of Pending Papers. Any pending bill, resolution, motion or
22 report shall be read by the Clerk upon the demand of any Senator, but it shall not again be
23 read on the same day unless so ordered by the Senate.
24 Rule 20. Nominations. In all elections a previous nomination shall be made.
25 MEMBERS
26 Rule 21. Attendance of Members. No Senator shall be absent from a session of
27 the Senate without leave from the Senate.

1 Rule 22. Decorum of Members. No Senator shall designate another Senator by
2 name. Members shall wear appropriate professional business attire while on the floor of
3 the Senate.
4 Rule 23. Call to Order. If any Senator, in speech or otherwise, transgresses the
5 rules of order or decorum, the Senator shall immediately be called to order by the chair
6 and shall be seated. The Clerk shall reduce the objectionable words to writing and read
7 them to the Senate. After hearing a short explanation from the Senator called to order, or
8 upon the withdrawal of the objectionable language, the President may permit the Senator
9 to proceed, or may require the Senator’s silence until the matter is disposed of. The ruling
10 of the chair shall be subject to an appeal to the Senate. A Senator offending the Senate
11 shall be liable to censure.
12 Rule 24. Debate. No Senator may speak more than once to the same subject until
13 all Senators desiring to be heard have spoken, but nothing in this rule shall do away with
14 the previous question if then in effect, nor permit debate on an undebatable motion.
15 No Senator shall speak more than fifteen minutes in the aggregate on any question
16 or measure, including debate on amendments and explanation of the member's vote. No
17 Senator shall be allowed to speak more than three minutes to explain a vote. At the end of
18 a member's allotted time, or any portion thereof, the floor shall be returned to the
19 President.
20 Rule 25. Members Shall Vote at Seats. A Senator shall vote only when at the
21 Senator’s seat or visibly approaching it.
22 OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

24 year, or at any time there is a vacancy in the office of President, immediately following
25 the adoption of the Rules, the President of the Senate shall take the chair to receive
26 nominations from the floor and to preside over the election of the President. The member
27 who receives the votes of a majority of the members elected shall be the President. If

3 Rule 37. Committee on Committees. There shall be a Committee on
4 Committees composed of the President, the President Pro Tem, the Majority Floor
