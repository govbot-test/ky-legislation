1 AN ACT relating to cremation of dead bodies.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 72.450 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) (a) A coroner who has possession of a dead body or a part thereof shall make a
5 bona fide attempt of at least thirty (30) days to notify the spouse, if any, or
6 next of kin of the decedent's death. In the event the coroner is unable to locate
7 the spouse, if any, or next of kin, or if the spouse or next of kin does not take
8 possession of the deceased, he or she may cause the body to be:
9 1. [ ]Buried or;
10 2. Cremated and interred;
11 [ ]at the expense of the fiscal court, consolidated local government, or urban-
12 county government, whichever is appropriate.
13 (b) The decision between burial or cremation and interment shall be made by
14 the fiscal court, consolidated local government, or urban-county
15 government that has responsibility for the expense pursuant to this
16 subsection.
17 (c) If a fiscal court, consolidated local government, or urban-county
18 government has responsibility for the expense pursuant to this subsection, it
19 shall consult with:
20 1. The local county coroner before it makes the decision between burial
21 or cremation and interment; and
22 2. If the decedent is known to be a member of a religious community,
23 any such religious community that has expressed, in writing, a
24 willingness to the local county coroner that it will pay for, or otherwise
25 provide, the burial expense of the community's members.
26 (2) In the event the body is buried or cremated and interred at public expense, the
27 coroner shall take possession of all money or other property found on or belonging
