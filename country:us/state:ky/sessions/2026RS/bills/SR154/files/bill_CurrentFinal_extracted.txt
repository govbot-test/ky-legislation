1 A RESOLUTION honoring University of Kentucky Athletic Director Mitch
2 Barnhart upon his retirement.
3 WHEREAS, Mitch Barnhart, a native of Kansas City, Kansas, earned a bachelor's
4 degree from Ottawa University in Kansas and a master's degree in sports administration
5 from Ohio University; and
6 WHEREAS, after serving as athletic director at Oregon State University from 1998
7 to 2002, Mitch Barnhart was named the tenth athletic director for the University of
8 Kentucky (UK) in 2002; and
9 WHEREAS, known for his motto of "putting championship rings on fingers and
10 diplomas in hands," Mitch Barnhart has had much success in both areas; and
11 WHEREAS, Mitch Barnhart's legacy at UK includes success in a wide array of
12 sports. All counted, UK has won six NCAA championship rings under Mitch Barnhart,
13 including the men's basketball team in 2012, volleyball in 2020, and rifle in 2011, 2018,
14 2021, and 2022; and
15 WHEREAS, under Mitch Barnhart's leadership, UK has also totaled 63 regular
16 season and tournament conference championships including first-time titles in baseball,
17 women's tennis, men's golf, and women's swimming and diving; and
18 WHEREAS, Mitch Barnhart announced his intention to retire in June of this year
19 after leading UK athletics for 24 years; and
20 WHEREAS, having the longest term of service among current athletic directors at
21 schools in the Power 4 college athletic conferences, Mitch Barnhart's legacy reaches not
22 just the past, present, and future of UK Athletics, but also the entirety of college sports;
23 and
24 WHEREAS, Mitch Barnhart's experience, knowledge, and synthesis of athletic and
25 academic success have made his a highly respected and sought-after presence regarding
26 the key issues of the day; and
27 WHEREAS, at the heart of everything Mitch Barnhart has accomplished at UK are
