24 (2) If a police officer, firefighter, emergency medical services personnel, front-line
25 staff member, or a Kentucky National Guard member suffers a psychological,
26 psychiatric, or stress-related change in the human organism that is not a direct
27 result of a physical injury but is the result of a work-related event or cumulative

1 work-related stress, then that psychological, psychiatric, or stress-related change
2 shall be considered an injury arising out of and in the course of employment if it
3 is demonstrated by the preponderance of the evidence that:
4 (a) The work-related event or cumulative work-related stress was extraordinary
5 and unusual in comparison to pressures and tensions experienced by the
6 average employee across all occupations; and
7 (b) The work-related event or cumulative work-related stress, and not some
8 other event, was the proximate cause of the psychological, psychiatric, or
9 stress-related change in the human organism.
10 (3) A psychological, psychiatric, or stress-related change in the human organism
11 shall not be considered an injury arising out of and in the course of employment
12 if it results from any disciplinary action, work evaluation, job transfer, layoff,
13 demotion, termination, or similar action taken in good faith by the employer.
14 (4) (a) If a police officer, firefighter, emergency medical services personnel, front-
15 line staff member, or a Kentucky National Guard member is diagnosed with
16 post-traumatic stress disorder by a qualified mental health professional
17 within three (3) years of the last active date of employment as a police
18 officer, firefighter, emergency medical services personnel, front-line staff
19 member, or Kentucky National Guard member, then there shall be a
20 rebuttable presumption that the post-traumatic stress disorder is an injury
21 covered by this chapter, and the employer with whom the employee was last
22 injuriously exposed to the harmful stress shall be exclusively liable for
23 benefits.
24 (b) A presumption of a work-related injury under paragraph (a) of this
25 subsection may be overcome by the preponderance of the evidence that the
26 post-traumatic stress disorder was caused by nonservice-connected risk
