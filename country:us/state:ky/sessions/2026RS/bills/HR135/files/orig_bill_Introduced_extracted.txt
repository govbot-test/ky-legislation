1 A RESOLUTION recognizing April 2, 2026, as World Autism Awareness Day and
2 April 2026 as Autism Acceptance Month.
3 WHEREAS, autism disorders affect persons regardless of race, religion,
4 socioeconomic status, or geography; and
5 WHEREAS, autism is the fastest-growing developmental disorder in the United
6 States, with approximately one in 35 children being diagnosed with autism spectrum
7 disorder; and
8 WHEREAS, there are over five million people in the United States with autism and,
9 as of 2019, over 80,000 Kentuckians diagnosed with autism; and
10 WHEREAS, autism disorders are lifelong conditions that require support, training,
11 assistance, and advocacy on behalf of those individuals who suffer from such disorders;
12 and
13 WHEREAS, on December 18, 2007, the United Nations General Assembly adopted
14 a resolution declaring World Autism Awareness Day by unanimous consent; and
15 WHEREAS, the Autism Society of Kentucky understands the importance of
16 fostering acceptance in order to achieve equity in education, employment, housing, health
17 care, and comprehensive long-term services and support; and
18 WHEREAS, the Autism Society of Kentucky provides education to promote
19 acceptance, inspire inclusion, and create systems-wide change for individuals with autism
20 so they may achieve full participation within society; and
21 WHEREAS, policies need to be implemented that break down barriers for
22 individuals with autism so they may succeed in their chosen communities; and
23 WHEREAS, the goals of World Autism Awareness Day are to inform the general
24 public about the global health crisis of autism disorders, stress the importance of early
25 diagnosis and early intervention, and celebrate the unique talents and qualities of
26 individuals with autism disorders; and
27 WHEREAS, April is recognized as Autism Acceptance Month to increase
