1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of Norma
2 June Evans.
3 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans was born on May 28, 1941, to Roscoe and Earsel
4 Penn in McCracken County, Kentucky; and
5 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans attended Draughon's Business College in 1959 and
6 graduated from Murray State University in 1972; and
7 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans worked as a speech therapist at McCracken County
8 Board of Education, while also instructing classes in Adult Basic Education and
9 McCracken County's Summer Head Start Program; and
10 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans was very creative and artistic and enjoyed
11 decorating, drawing, painting, and sewing; and
12 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans also loved gardening and handcrafted numerous
13 floral arrangements throughout the years for her beloved family members and friends;
14 and
15 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans had a deep appreciation for the small moments in
16 life, such as laughter and a morning cup of coffee, and she loved sharing those moments
17 with others; and
18 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans was survived by her four children, Patricia Adams,
19 Jill Reeves, David (Elizabeth) Reeves, and Priscilla (Mike) Ridenour; six grandchildren,
20 Andrew Adams, Eli (Alison) Reeves, Emily (Ben) Sirk, Jackie (Jordan) Turner, Max
21 Thompson, and Sydney (Will) Puckett; four great-grandchildren, June and Heidi Sirk and
22 Avery and Emma Turner; one brother, Clifford (Sherri) Penn and one nephew Shawn
23 (Amy) Penn; and several great nieces and nephews; and
24 WHEREAS, Norma June Evans passed peacefully from this life on January 16,
25 2026, at home surrounded by family;
