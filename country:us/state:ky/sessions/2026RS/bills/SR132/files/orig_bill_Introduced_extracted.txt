1 A RESOLUTION honoring Dr. Joel Barrow and the Choctaw Academy as the first
2 Indigenous American to become a college-educated medical doctor in the country and the
3 first federal Native American boarding school that empowered him.
4 WHEREAS, Dr. Joel Barrow was a visionary scholar who paved the way for future
5 generations of Native American medical professionals in the Commonwealth of
6 Kentucky and the nation; and
7 WHEREAS, Dr. Joel Barrow was a proud member of the Potawatomi tribe, where
8 he was known as Arcmuggue before later changing his name; and
9 WHEREAS, Arcmuggue, an orphan, was selected by his tribe to attend the
10 Choctaw Academy in Scott County, Kentucky, which was the first secular, federal,
11 Native American boarding school, built by Vice President R. M. Johnson in 1825, and,
12 uniquely, at the request of Native tribes; and
13 WHEREAS, the Choctaw Academy would become known by historians as "the
14 most important place in Antebellum America," having educated 600 Native Americans
15 from 17 different tribes; and
16 WHEREAS, young Arcmuggue overcame great distress, as his arrival coincided
17 with the Potawatomi period of removal, the "Trail of Death"; and
18 WHEREAS, Dr. Joel Barrow would later be remembered as among the most gifted
19 students ever to attend this special school; and
20 WHEREAS, Dr. Joel Barrow overcame barriers and would go on to receive a grant
21 from the United States War Department to pursue a medical degree from Transylvania
22 University in Lexington, Kentucky, which he earned in 1844, making history as the first
23 college-educated and college-trained Native American doctor in the country; and
24 WHEREAS, after completing his education, Dr. Joel Barrow went west to the
25 Potawatomi tribe, utilizing his hard-earned skills for the benefit of his Native American
26 brethren; and
27 WHEREAS, Dr. Joel Barrow was among those who recognized that education is
