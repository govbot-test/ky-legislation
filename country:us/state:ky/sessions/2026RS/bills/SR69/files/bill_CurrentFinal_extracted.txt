4 WHEREAS, in May 1936, Congress passed the Rural Electrification Act, and
5 President Franklin D. Roosevelt signed it into law as part of his New Deal, which
6 allowed the federal government to provide low-cost loans to not-for-profit cooperatives
7 formed by farmers to bring electricity to rural America; and

9 had electricity, so on July 7, 1941, thirteen owner-member cooperatives formed the not-

11 self-supplying economical electric generation and transmission to rural areas across the

15 owned utilities; and
16 WHEREAS, in 1954, EKPC began operating its first power plant, the William C.

23 two coal-fired plants, the H.L. Spurlock Station near Maysville and the John Sherman
24 Cooper Station near Somerset, and two plants powered by natural gas or fuel oil, at
25 Bluegrass Station in Lagrange and the J.K. Smith Station near Winchester; and
26 WHEREAS, EKPC also practices environmental stewardship by deploying an
27 impressive fleet of five renewable generation power-plants, making electricity from
