1 READ AS FOLLOWS:
2 (1) A person is guilty of interference with a religious service when, with intent to
3 prevent or disrupt a religious service held by a religious organization, he or she:
4 (a) Does any act tending to obstruct, or physically or audibly interfere with, the
5 religious service or any individual's access to or egress from the service;
6 (b) Intentionally damages or destroys, or attempts to damage or destroy, the
7 property of a religious organization; or
8 (c) By force or threat of force, intentionally injures, intimidates, or interferes
9 with, or attempts to injure, intimidate, or interfere with, any individual
10 attending or attempting to attend the religious service.
11 (2) Interference with a religious service is a Class A misdemeanor.
12 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 411 IS CREATED TO
13 READ AS FOLLOWS:
14 (1) A person who violates Section 2 of this Act shall be personally liable in a civil
15 cause of action brought against him or her by:
16 (a) A victim who was lawfully exercising or seeking to exercise a right of
17 religious worship; or
18 (b) The religious organization that was undertaking the religious service.
19 (2) The action may be filed in the Circuit Court for the county where the alleged
20 violation occurred or the county where the plaintiff resides.
21 (3) The court may award appropriate relief including:
22 (a) Injunctive relief;
23 (b) Compensatory damages, or at the plaintiff's election prior to the rendering
24 of final judgment, statutory damages of ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for
25 each violation of Section 2 of this Act;
26 (c) Costs and expenses, including reasonable expert witness fees; and
27 (d) Reasonable attorney's fees.
