UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 2181
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 422.305 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) (a) When a subpoena duces tecum is served upon any employee of any medical
5 provider[hospital], requiring the production of any[ such] medical charts or
6 records at any action or proceeding, it is sufficient if the employee of the
7 medical provider[hospital] charged with the responsibility of being custodian
8 of the original charts or records[thereof] promptly notifies, in writing, the
9 attorney for the party causing service of the subpoena, of the medical
10 provider's[hospital's] election to proceed under the provisions of KRS
11 422.300 to 422.330 and of the estimated actual and reasonable expenses of
12 reproducing the requested[such] charts or records.
13 (b) Upon receipt of the[such] notification, the attorney causing the service of the
14 subpoena shall notify all other attorneys of record, or other parties if they are
15 not represented by attorneys, of the medical provider's[hospital's] election.
16 (c) Following the medical provider's[such] election, the employee of the medical
17 provider[hospital] charged with the responsibility of being custodian of the
18 original charts or records specified in the subpoena shall hold the originals
19 available at the medical provider's facility[hospital], and upon payment to the
20 medical provider[hospital] of the estimated reproduction expenses by the
21 party causing service of the subpoena, or by any other party, shall promptly
22 deliver, by certified mail or by personal delivery, legible and durable copies,
23 certified by the medical provider's[said hospital] employee, of all medical
24 charts or records specified in the[such] subpoena to the person specified in the
25 subpoena.
26 (2) (a) The certification shall be signed before a notary public by the employee of the
27 medical facility[hospital] charged with[ the responsibility of] being the

2 patient's medical record number, the number of pages in the medical record,
3 and a legend substantially to the following effect: "The copies of records for
4 which this certification is made are true and complete reproductions of the
5 original or microfilmed medical records which are housed in (name of
6 medical provider[hospital]). The original records were made in the regular
7 course of business, and it was the regular course of (name of medical
8 provider[hospital]) to make the[such] records at or near the time of the matter
9 recorded. This certification is given pursuant to KRS .... by the custodian of
10 the records in lieu of his or her personal appearance."
11 (b) 1. The[Such] copies shall be separately enclosed and sealed in an inner
12 envelope or wrapper bearing the legend "Copies of Medical Records.[,]"

14 subpoena, the name of the medical provider[hospital], the full name of
15 the patient, the patient's medical record number, and the name and
16 business telephone number of the employee making the certification,
17 together with[and] the sealed envelope or wrapper and[, together with]
18 the certification, shall then be enclosed and sealed in an outer envelope
19 or wrapper, and delivered to the requesting party.

21 presented to the person receiving the records for his or her signature and shall
22 be immediately signed and returned to the person delivering the records. The
23 receipt shall contain the name of the medical provider[hospital], the full name
24 of the patient, the medical record number of the patient, the date the copies of
25 records were received, and the signature of the person receiving the records.
26 (b) When the copies of records are sent via certified mail, the receipt used by the
27 postal authorities shall be sufficient to prove delivery and receipt of the copies

2 (4) If the medical provider[hospital] has none of the charts or records specified in the
3 subpoena, or only part of the charts or records[thereof], the employee of the

5 of the original medical provider's[hospital] charts or records shall[ so] state that
6 fact in a notarized affidavit and, following notice and payment of expenses shall
7 hold available the original charts or records which are in the medical
8 provider's[hospital's] custody and specified in the subpoena, and shall deliver the
