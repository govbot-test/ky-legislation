4 Section 1. KRS 48.705 is amended to read as follows:
5 (1) A budget reserve trust fund account is hereby created in the general fund, pursuant
6 to KRS 45.305. The budget reserve trust fund account shall be funded through
7 direct appropriations, and surplus amounts as provided in subsection (2) of this
8 section. Moneys in the account shall remain unallotted unless required by the
9 provisions of this section.
10 (2) (a) Each fiscal year, except as provided in subsection (3) of this section, within
11 thirty (30) days of the end of the fiscal year, the secretary of the Finance and
12 Administration Cabinet shall cause to be deposited to the budget reserve trust
13 fund account the lesser of the following amounts:
14 1. Fifty percent (50%) of the general fund surplus; or
15 2. The amount necessary from the general fund surplus to make the
16 balance of the budget reserve trust fund account equal to five percent
17 (5%) of the actual general fund receipts collected during the fiscal year
18 just ended, as determined by the Finance and Administration Cabinet.
19 (b) Any amounts to be deposited to the budget reserve trust fund account from the
20 general fund surplus shall be determined after the surplus has been reduced by
21 the amount necessary to implement the provisions of any surplus expenditure
22 plan authorized by KRS 48.140 and enacted as a part of a branch budget bill.
23 (3) If, at the close of any fiscal year, the budget reserve trust fund account has a balance
24 equal to or greater than five percent (5%) of the actual general fund receipts
25 collected during the fiscal year just ended, as determined by the Finance and
26 Administration Cabinet, the deposits required under subsection (2) of this section
27 shall be suspended for that year.

1 (4) (a) Moneys in the budget reserve trust fund account may be appropriated by the
2 General Assembly in a regular or special session.
3 (b) 1. If a lapse in federal appropriations occurs due to a shutdown of the
4 federal government or a temporary suspension in federal funding, the
5 Governor may authorize the limited use of the moneys in the budget
6 reserve trust fund to provide for the continuity of the Supplemental
7 Nutrition Assistance Program.
8 2. Any moneys used under this paragraph shall be considered a
9 temporary loan and be repaid to the budget reserve trust fund from the
10 affected federal funding source within ninety (90) days of the
11 restoration of federal appropriations.

13 moneys under this paragraph, notify the Legislative Research
14 Commission and the State Budget Director in a statement that
15 includes:
16 a. The amount of funds advanced;
17 b. The programs to which the funds were allocated; and
18 c. The anticipated source and timeline for repayment.
19 4. The Governor shall not authorize the use of more than five percent
20 (5%) of the total balance of the budget reserve trust fund without the
21 prior approval of the General Assembly.

23 secretary of the Finance and Administration Cabinet shall notify in writing the

25 the amount and purpose for the proposed allotment.
