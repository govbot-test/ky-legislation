1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION establishing the Cemeteries Across Kentucky
2 Task Force.
3 WHEREAS, Kentucky values all who abide within the Commonwealth and
4 respects their wishes in regard to their final resting places; and
5 WHEREAS, the task of caring for cemeteries and those interred within is one of
6 great responsibility, and the upkeep of their grounds should be shown great care and
7 respect; and
8 WHEREAS, there are cemeteries across Kentucky that have fallen into disrepair
9 with no knowledge of who is laid to rest, some of whom could be veterans; and
10 WHEREAS, these cemetery owners have not shown proper regard for the men and
11 women interred within and have abandoned those properties; and
12 WHEREAS, the final resting place of these men and women should be one of
13 dignity and reverence; and
14 WHEREAS, the General Assembly believes all people interred within the
15 cemeteries in Kentucky should be shown the respect they so richly deserve and have
16 earned through their lives;
17 NOW, THEREFORE,
18 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the General Assembly of the
19 Commonwealth of Kentucky, the Senate concurring therein:
20 Section 1. (1) The Legislative Research Commission is hereby directed to
21 establish the Cemeteries Across Kentucky Task Force. The task force shall review and
22 make recommendations regarding the problem of abandoned cemeteries within the
23 Commonwealth, including but not limited to:
24 (a) Conduct a survey of the total number of abandoned cemeteries in Kentucky;
25 (b) Review state and local laws, regulations, policies, and procedures that address
26 abandoned cemeteries;
27 (c) Examine efforts and policy recommendations in other states and

1 municipalities to address abandoned cemeteries; and
2 (d) Consider actions and policies the state should take to combat the problem of
3 abandoned cemeteries in Kentucky.
4 (2) In the development of policy recommendations and strategies, the task force
5 shall seek input from organizations and associations whose members address abandoned
6 cemeteries and the preservation of a person's final resting place and appropriate
7 stakeholders as determined by the task force.
8 Section 2. The task force shall be composed of the following thirteen (13)
9 members, with final membership of the task force being subject to the consideration and
10 approval of the Legislative Research Commission:
11 (1) Two members of the Senate to be appointed by the President of the Senate
12 and one member of the Senate to be appointed by the Minority Floor Leader of the
13 Senate, one of whom shall be designated by the President of the Senate as a co-chair of
14 the task force;
15 (2) Two members of the House of Representatives to be appointed by the Speaker
16 of the House of Representatives and one member of the House of Representatives to be
17 appointed by the Minority Floor Leader of the House of Representatives, one of whom
18 shall be designated by the Speaker of the House of Representatives as a co-chair of the
19 task force;
20 (3) One member from a county cemetery board;
21 (4) One representative of the State Veterans Cemeteries of the Kentucky
22 Department of Veterans' Affairs;
23 (5) One citizen and one cemetery owner to be appointed by the President of the
24 Senate; and
25 (6) One citizen and one cemetery owner to be appointed by the Speaker of the
26 House of Representatives; and
27 (7) One member from the National Association for Black Veterans.

1 Section 3. The Cemeteries Across Kentucky Task Force shall meet monthly
2 during the 2026 Interim of the General Assembly. The task force shall submit findings
3 and recommendations to the Legislative Research Commission for referral to the
4 appropriate committee or subcommittee thereof by December 1, 2026.
5 Section 4. Provisions of this resolution to the contrary notwithstanding, the
6 Legislative Research Commission shall have the authority to alternatively assign the
7 issues identified herein to an interim joint committee or subcommittee thereof, and to
8 designate a study completion date.
