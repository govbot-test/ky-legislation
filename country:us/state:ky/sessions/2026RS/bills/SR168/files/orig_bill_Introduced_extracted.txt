2 "Special Olympics – Deleting the 'Dis' in Disabled" community service initiative.
3 WHEREAS, according to the World Health Organization, approximately 1 in 6
4 people worldwide experience some form of physical or intellectual disability, and
5 individuals with different abilities deserve opportunities for inclusion, respect, and
6 community participation; and
7 WHEREAS, organizations such as the Special Olympics provide meaningful
8 opportunities for individuals with intellectual and physical disabilities to participate in
9 sports, build confidence, develop friendships, and demonstrate their ability; and
10 WHEREAS, the mission and values of the Special Olympics promote the idea that
11 disabilities should not define individuals, but rather that their abilities, courage, and
12 determination should be celebrated; and
13 WHEREAS, personal experiences with Special Olympic athletes demonstrate the
14 powerful impact of inclusion, perseverance, and kindness, and highlight the importance
15 of creating communities that recognize the value and potential of every individual; and
16 WHEREAS, community initiatives, such as Chloe Yates' "Special Olympics –
17 Deleting the 'Dis' in Disabled," have successfully recruited athletes and volunteers,
18 fostered meaningful one-on-one friendships, and shared athlete stories to inspire broader
19 support for individuals with different abilities; and
20 WHEREAS, the motto of the Special Olympics, "Let me win. But if I cannot win,
21 let me be brave in the attempt," reflects the courage, determination, and resilience that
22 these athletes demonstrate every day; and
23 WHEREAS, communities, organizations, and individuals should actively support
24 and promote the mission of the Special Olympics, advocating for inclusion, accessibility,
25 and opportunities for individuals with special needs to participate fully in sports and
26 community life; and
27 WHEREAS, individuals and organizations should encourage volunteerism,
