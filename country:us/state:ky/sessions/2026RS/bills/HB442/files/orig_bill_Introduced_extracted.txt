UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 1826
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 61.7991 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) As used in this section:
5 (a) "Covered person" means: [a]
6 1. A judicial officer or an immediate family member of a judicial officer;
7 2. A Commonwealth's attorney or assistant Commonwealth's attorney;
8 3. A county attorney or assistant county attorney;
9 4. An attorney employed by:
10 a. The Department of Public Advocacy;
11 b. The Office of the United States Attorneys; or
12 c. Western Kentucky Federal Community Defender, Inc.; or
13 5. An attorney appointed to a Criminal Justice Act panel to represent
14 indigent clients;
15 (b) "Disclose" means to post, display, publish, or otherwise make publicly
16 available;
17 (c) "Immediate family member" means:
18 1. A spouse, child, parent, or person under the familial custody or care of a
19 judicial officer; or
20 2. Any other familial relative who resides in the same household as the
21 judicial officer;
22 (d) "Judicial officer" means any active or senior judge and includes a:
23 1. Justice of the United States or a judge of the United States as those
24 terms are defined in 28 U.S.C. sec. 451;
25 2. Bankruptcy judge appointed under 28 U.S.C. sec. 152 or recalled
26 pursuant to 28 U.S.C. sec. 375;
27 3. United States magistrate judge appointed under 28 U.S.C. sec. 631 or

1 recalled pursuant to 28 U.S.C. sec. 375;
2 4. Judge confirmed by the United States Senate and empowered by statute
3 in any commonwealth, territory, or possession to perform the duties of a
4 federal judge;
5 5. Judge of the United States Court of Federal Claims appointed under 28

9 (e) "Personally identifiable information" means data that can identify a covered
10 person and includes:

14 4. Home or cellular telephone numbers;

17 schools and daycare facilities they attend;
18 7. Social Security number; and
19 8. The name of an immediate family member's employer; and
20 (f) "Written request" means a notice signed by a covered person requesting a
21 government agency refrain from posting or displaying publicly available
22 content that includes the personally identifiable information of the covered
23 person.
24 (2) (a) A government agency shall not disclose the personally identifiable
25 information of any covered person if the covered person has made a written
26 request to the government agency that this personally identifiable information
27 not be disclosed.

1 (b) Upon receipt of the written request, personally identifiable information shall
2 be removed from publicly available content within seventy-two (72) hours.
3 (c) A request under this subsection may be made on behalf of a minor who is a
4 covered person by a parent or guardian of the minor.
5 (3) (a) After the government agency has removed the personally identifiable
6 information from publicly available content, the government agency shall not
7 publicly post or otherwise release the information.
8 (b) Publication of personally identifiable information by the covered person
9 removes any prohibition under paragraph (a) of this subsection[unless the
10 covered person voluntarily publishes the information on the internet after June
11 27, 2025].
12 (4) A covered person may bring a civil action seeking injunctive or declaratory relief to
13 enforce this section in any court of competent jurisdiction.
14 (5) This section shall not apply to any financial disclosure required under this
15 chapter.
