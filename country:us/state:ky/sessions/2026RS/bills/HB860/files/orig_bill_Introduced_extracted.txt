1 AN ACT relating to motor vehicle license plates.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 186.240 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) It shall be the duty of the cabinet to carry out the provisions of KRS 186.005 to
5 186.260, and:
6 (a) Provide to the clerk in each county access to all forms provided for in KRS
7 186.005 to 186.260;
8 (b) Keep a numerical record of all registration numbers issued in the state and
9 also keep a record of motor or vehicle identification numbers required by
10 KRS 186.160;
11 (c) Furnish to each clerk, originally each year upon estimate, and thereafter upon
12 requisition at all times, a sufficient supply of standard, noncommercial plates
13 and the supplies necessary to provide evidence of registration for all classes of
14 vehicles required to be registered;[ and]
15 (d) Prescribe a standard plate of practical form and size for police identification
16 purposes that shall contain:
17 1. The registration identifier;
18 2. An indication that Kentucky is the issuing jurisdiction;
19 3. At the discretion of the cabinet, any combination of the following
20 phrases:
21 a. "Bluegrass State"; or
22 b. "United We Stand, Divided We Fall";
23 4. For standard plates for noncommercial vehicles:
24 a. The county in which the plate is issued; and
25 b. At the discretion of the person to whom the vehicle is registered,
26 the phrase "In God We Trust"; and
27 5. For plates for commercial vehicles, the year the license expires and

4 (e) Prescribe an alternative standard plate for noncommercial vehicles that is:
5 1. Available to individuals who are sixty-five (65) years of age or older to
6 choose at the time of initial or renewal registration of a motor vehicle;
7 and
8 2. Of a highly visible design and letter coloring scheme that is distinct
9 from the standard plates under paragraph (d) of this subsection.
10 (2) Except as provided in KRS 186A.127, license plates issued pursuant to this chapter
11 shall conform to the provisions of subsection (1)(c) and (d) of this section. The
12 Transportation Cabinet shall provide for the issuance of reflectorized plates for all
13 motor vehicles, and shall collect a fee, in addition to the fee set out in KRS Chapter
14 186 and KRS 281.631, of fifty cents ($0.50). The fifty cents ($0.50) fee to
15 reflectorize license plates shall be used by the cabinet as provided in subsection (3)
16 of this section.
17 (3) The reflectorized license plate program fund is established in the state road fund
18 and appropriated on a continual basis to the cabinet to administer the moneys as
19 provided in this subsection. The fifty cents ($0.50) fee collected by the cabinet to
20 reflectorize license plates shall be deposited into the program fund and used to issue
21 reflectorized license plates. If at the end of a fiscal year, money remains in the
22 program fund, it shall be retained in the fund and shall not revert to the state road
23 fund. The interest and income earned on money in the program fund shall also be
24 retained in the program fund to carry out the provisions of this subsection. The
25 Transportation Cabinet shall issue reflectorized license plates under the provisions
26 of this subsection on a schedule to be determined at the discretion of the cabinet.
