12 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 367 IS CREATED TO
13 READ AS FOLLOWS:
14 (1) A licensed professional:
15 (a) Shall not use artificial intelligence to assist in providing supplementary
16 support in therapy or psychotherapy services where the client's therapeutic
17 session is recorded or transcribed unless:
18 1. The patient or the patient's legally authorized representative is
19 informed in writing of the following:
20 a. That artificial intelligence will be used; and
21 b. The specific purpose of the artificial intelligence tool or system
22 that will be used; and
23 2. The patient or the patient's legally authorized representative provides
24 consent to the use of artificial intelligence;
25 (b) May use artificial intelligence only to the extent it is a permitted use of
26 artificial intelligence and the use meets the requirements of this section;
27 (c) Shall not allow artificial intelligence to:

1 1. Make independent therapeutic decisions;
2 2. Directly interact with clients in any form of therapeutic
3 communication;
4 3. Generate therapeutic recommendations or treatment plans without
5 review and approval by the licensed professional; or
6 4. Detect emotions or mental state; and
7 (2) An individual, corporation, or entity shall not provide, advertise, or otherwise
8 offer therapy or psychotherapy services, including through the use of internet-
9 based artificial intelligence, to the public in this Commonwealth unless the

11 licensed professional.
12 (3) All records kept by a licensed professional and all communications between an
13 individual seeking therapy or psychotherapy services and a licensed professional
14 shall be confidential and shall not be disclosed except as required by KRS
15 210.235.
16 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 367 IS CREATED TO
17 READ AS FOLLOWS:
18 (1) Any individual, corporation, or entity found to be in violation of Sections 1 to 3 of
19 this Act shall pay a civil penalty to the department in an amount not to exceed ten
20 thousand dollars ($10,000) per violation, as determined by the department, with
21 penalties assessed based on the degree of harm and the circumstances of the
22 violation.
23 (2) A civil penalty shall be:
24 (a) Assessed by the department after a hearing is held by the relevant board;
25 and
26 (b) Paid within sixty (60) days after the date of an order by the relevant board
27 imposing the civil penalty.
