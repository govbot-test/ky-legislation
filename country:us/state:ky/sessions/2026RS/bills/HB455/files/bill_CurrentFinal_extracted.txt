7 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 367 IS CREATED TO
8 READ AS FOLLOWS:
9 (1) A licensed professional:
10 (a) Shall not use artificial intelligence to assist in providing supplementary
11 support in therapy or psychotherapy services where the client's therapeutic
12 session is recorded or transcribed unless:
13 1. The patient or the patient's legally authorized representative is
14 informed in writing of the following:
15 a. That artificial intelligence will be used; and
16 b. The specific purpose of the artificial intelligence tool or system
17 that will be used; and
18 2. The patient or the patient's legally authorized representative provides
19 consent to the use of artificial intelligence;
20 (b) May use artificial intelligence only to the extent it is a permitted use of
21 artificial intelligence and the use meets the requirements of this section;
22 and
23 (c) Shall not allow artificial intelligence to:
24 1. Make independent therapeutic decisions; or
25 2. Generate therapeutic recommendations or treatment plans without
26 review and approval by the licensed professional.
27 (2) An individual, corporation, or entity shall not provide, advertise, or otherwise

9 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 367 IS CREATED TO
10 READ AS FOLLOWS:
11 (1) Any individual, corporation, or entity found to be in violation of Sections 1 to 3 of
12 this Act shall pay a civil penalty to the relevant board in an amount not to exceed
13 ten thousand dollars ($10,000) per violation, as determined by the relevant board,
14 with penalties assessed based on the degree of harm and the circumstances of the
15 violation.
16 (2) A civil penalty shall be:
17 (a) Assessed by the relevant board after a hearing is held by the relevant board
18 in accordance with KRS Chapter 13B; and
19 (b) Paid within sixty (60) days after the date of an order by the relevant board
20 imposing the civil penalty.
21 (3) The relevant board shall have authority to investigate any actual, alleged, or
22 suspected violation of Sections 1 to 3 of this Act.
23 (4) Sections 1 to 3 of this Act do not apply to religious counseling, peer support, or
24 self-help materials and educational resources that are available to the public and
25 do not purport to offer therapy or psychotherapy services.
