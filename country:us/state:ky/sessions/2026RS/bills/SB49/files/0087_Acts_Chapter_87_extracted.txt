CHAPTER 87 1
CHAPTER 87
( SB 49 )
AN ACT relating to battery stewardship.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF SUBCHAPTER 50 OF KRS CHAPTER 224 IS CREATED TO
READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) As used in this section and Section 2 of this Act:
(a) "Battery-containing product":
1. Means a product that contains or is packaged with a covered battery; and
2. Does not include computers, small-scale servers, computer monitors, electronic keyboards and
mice, printers, fax machines, scanners, televisions, digital video disc players and recorders,
video cassette recorders, digital converter boxes, cable receivers, satellite receivers, portable
digital music players, or video game consoles;
(b) "Covered battery":
1. Means a portable battery or a medium format battery; and
2. Does not include a battery:
a. That is contained in a medical device regulated under the federal Food, Drug, and
Cosmetic Act, 21 U.S.C. 301 et seq., and is not designed or marketed for sale or resale at
retail locations for personal use;
b. That contains an electrolyte as a free liquid or a product that contains such a battery;
c. Designed to power a motor vehicle, part of a motor vehicle, or a component part of a
motor vehicle assembled by, or for, a vehicle manufacturer or franchised dealer,
including replacement parts for use in a motor vehicle;
d. In a product that is not intended or designed to be easily removable from the product;
e. Or battery-containing product that is being recalled for safety reasons; or
f. Or battery-containing product offered for resale by a business that, as part of its
operations, offers products for resale to other businesses or to consumers;
(c) "Medium format battery" means:
1. For batteries that are not capable of being recharged, a battery that weighs more than four
and four-tenths (4.4) pounds but not more than twenty-five (25) pounds; or
2. For rechargeable batteries, a battery that weighs more than eleven (11) pounds or has a rating
of more than three hundred (300) watt-hours, or both, but that does not weigh more than
twenty-five (25) pounds or have a rating of more than two thousand (2,000) watt-hours;
(d) "Portable battery" means:
1. For batteries that are not capable of being recharged, a battery that weighs no more than four
and four-tenths (4.4) pounds; or
2. For rechargeable batteries, a battery that weighs no more than eleven (11) pounds and has a
rating of no more than three hundred (300) watt-hours;
(e) "Rechargeable battery" means a battery that contains one (1) or more voltaic or galvanic cells
electrically connected to produce electric energy and that is designed to be recharged;
(f) "Recovery" means the collection, accumulation, and transportation of covered batteries or battery-
containing products for the purpose of introducing them into a recycling process; and

(g) "Voluntary battery stewardship organization" means a voluntary organization designated by the
cabinet to directly implement a covered battery stewardship plan approved by the cabinet.
(2) Beginning on June 30, 2027, a person shall not place or cause to be placed a covered battery in a curbside,
commercial front load, or roll-off container that is used for either solid waste disposal or recycling.
SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF SUBCHAPTER 50 OF KRS CHAPTER 224 IS CREATED TO
READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) No later than March 31, 2027, the cabinet shall establish the Covered Battery Stewardship Program in
order to:
(a) Educate the public on the prohibition on covered battery disposal in Section 1 of this Act;
(b) Foster the creation of voluntary battery stewardship organizations in the Commonwealth;
(c) Oversee the implementation of a voluntary take-back program for recovery of rechargeable covered
batteries in an efficient and safe manner;
(d) Investigate the feasibility of establishing a voluntary take-back program for imbedded batteries;
(e) Promote a market for the recovery of covered batteries; and
(f) Prevent damage to solid waste collection equipment and disposal facilities due to the improper
disposal of covered batteries.
(2) To facilitate the establishment of the Covered Battery Stewardship Program, the cabinet shall collaborate
with, at a minimum, the following stakeholders or representatives thereof:
(a) Manufacturers, wholesalers, distributors, and retailers of covered batteries and covered battery-
containing products;
(b) Material recyclers, as defined in KRS 177.905;
(c) Solid waste collectors and solid waste disposal facilities;
(d) Municipalities;
(e) Counties;
(f) Voluntary battery stewardship organizations, if any; and
(g) Waste management districts established under KRS Chapter 109.
(3) Once the program is established, the cabinet shall publish, update, and maintain on its website:
(a) A listing of covered battery collection sites that will accept covered batteries for recovery; and
(b) Contact information for transporters that will accept covered batteries for transportation to a
collection site or recovery facility.
(4) (a) To be designated as a voluntary battery stewardship organization by the cabinet, the registrant shall
submit its registration in the form and manner as the cabinet may require. For approval, at a
minimum, the registration shall include the following:
1. The business structure of the voluntary battery stewardship organization;
2. The names and contact information for all officers of, and participants in, the voluntary
battery stewardship organization; and
3. Evidence that the voluntary battery stewardship organization is currently in good standing
with the Secretary of State.
(b) Within ten (10) business days of receiving a complete registration, the cabinet shall inform the
registrant that the registration is approved.
(5) After the cabinet has approved its registration under subsection (4) of this section, but before operation, a
voluntary battery stewardship organization shall submit to the cabinet a voluntary battery stewardship
organization plan for approval or disapproval, which shall contain, at a minimum:
(a) Requirements for participating retailers of covered batteries or battery-containing products to
conspicuously display on their products notice of the:

1. Prohibition on covered battery disposal in Section 1 of this Act; and
2. Website address maintained by the cabinet pursuant to subsection (3) of this section;
(b) Requirements for participating covered battery manufacturers or producers to ensure that the
covered battery:
1. Including a battery in a battery-containing product, is marked with an identification of the
manufacturer or producer of the battery; and
2. Is marked to ensure proper collection and recovery by identifying the chemistry of the battery
and including an indication that the battery should not be disposed of as household waste;
(c) Consumer awareness goals and a description of the education and outreach strategy that the
voluntary battery stewardship organization will implement;
(d) Collection site safety training procedures related to covered battery collection activities at collection
sites, including a description of operating protocols to reduce risks of spills or fires, response
protocols in the event of a spill or fire, and protocols for safe management of damaged batteries that
are returned to collection sites;
(e) Provisions for recordkeeping, tracking, and documenting the management and disposition of
collected covered batteries; and
(f) A method for fully funding the voluntary battery stewardship organization's plan in a manner that
equitably distributes the plan's costs among the members of the battery stewardship organization,
without adding a point-of-sale cost or fee to the consumer.
(6) Each voluntary battery stewardship organization shall submit to the cabinet on or before June 30, 2027,
and each June 30 thereafter, a report that includes:
(a) The weight, by chemistry, of covered batteries collected;
(b) The weight of materials recovered from covered batteries collected, in total, and by method of battery
recovery;
(c) A calculation of the recovery efficiency rate;
(d) Identification of all facilities utilized by the organization for recovery or other management of
covered batteries and battery components and a description of how the facility recovered or otherwise
managed covered batteries and battery components;
(e) The weight and chemistry, using approximations or actual amount, of covered batteries sent to each
facility that is used for the final disposition of batteries; and
(f) A detailed description of education and outreach efforts supporting plan implementation and an
evaluation of the effectiveness of education and outreach activities.
Signed by Governor April 10, 2026.
