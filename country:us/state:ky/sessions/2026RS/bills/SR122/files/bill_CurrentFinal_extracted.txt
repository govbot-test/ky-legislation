1 A RESOLUTION adjourning the Senate in honor and loving memory of John
2 Douglas "Doug" Hubbard.
3 WHEREAS, John Douglas "Doug" Hubbard was born October 18, 1941, to the late
4 EE and Marie Hubbard of Bardstown; and
5 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard was a lifetime member of Bardstown United Methodist
6 Church, and a proud Bardstown High School alum, class of 1959; and
7 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard was a graduate of the University of Kentucky, where
8 he served as president of Pi Kappa Alpha Fraternity; and
9 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard attended the University of Kentucky College of Law
10 and became a member of the bar in 1967; and
11 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard practiced law with his father at Fulton, Hubbard &
12 Hubbard Law Offices, where he spent more than 55 years passionately representing the
13 people of Nelson County and the surrounding area; and
14 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard also served as corporate attorney for Salt River Electric
15 Cooperative for more than 30 years, a role he especially enjoyed; and
16 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard was very involved in his community, having served as
17 past president of the Kiwanis Club and the Nelson County Jaycees; and
18 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard was named Outstanding Young Man in 1968 by the
19 Chamber of Commerce and was instrumental in growing his city, being inducted in the
20 Nelson County Hall of Fame in 2004; and
21 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard was a huge advocate for education, investing in the
22 Bardstown Independent School System all his life, and being later appointed to the
23 Kentucky Board of Education, as well as being inducted into the Bardstown High School
24 Athletic Hall of Fame in 2007; and
25 WHEREAS, Doug Hubbard is survived by his wife of 59 years, Judy Whitesides;
26 his three children, Susan (Mike) Carson, John (Julie) Hubbard, and Char (Chris) Rhoads;
27 his six grandchildren, Megan, Jack, and Ben Hubbard, and Clayton, Conner, and
