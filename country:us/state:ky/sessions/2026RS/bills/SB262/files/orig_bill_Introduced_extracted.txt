3 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
4 Section 1. Are you in favor of amending Section 256 of the Constitution of
5 Kentucky to permit constitutional amendments to be submitted to the voters by ballot
6 question or summary that clearly describes the substance and effect of the amendment,
7 rather than requiring the full text of the amendment to appear on the ballot, as stated
8 below?
9 Section 2. It is proposed that Section 256 of the Constitution of Kentucky be
10 amended to read as follows:
11 Amendments to this Constitution may be proposed in either House of the General
12 Assembly at a regular session, and if such amendment or amendments shall be agreed to
13 by three-fifths of all the members elected to each House, such proposed amendment or
14 amendments, with the yeas and nays of the members of each House taken thereon, shall
15 be entered in full in their respective journals. Then such proposed amendment or
16 amendments shall be submitted to the voters of the State for their ratification or rejection
17 at the next general election for members of the House of Representatives, the vote to be
18 taken thereon in such manner as the General Assembly may provide, and to be certified
19 by the officers of election to the Secretary of State in such manner as shall be provided by
20 law, which vote shall be compared and certified by the same board authorized by law to
21 compare the polls and give certificates of election to officers for the State at large. The
22 full text of the proposed amendment shall not be required to be printed on the ballot.
23 Instead, it shall be submitted to the voters of the State in the form of a ballot question
24 or summary that clearly and accurately states the substance and effect of the proposed
25 amendment. If it shall appear that a majority of the votes cast for and against an
26 amendment at said election was for the amendment, then the same shall become a part of
27 the Constitution of this Commonwealth, and shall be so proclaimed by the Governor, and

2 not be submitted at an election which occurs less than ninety days from the final passage
3 of such proposed amendment or amendments. Not more than four amendments shall be
4 voted upon at any one time. If two or more amendments shall be submitted at the same
5 time, they shall be submitted in such manner that the electors shall vote for or against
6 each of such amendments separately, but an amendment may relate to a single subject or
7 to related subject matters and may amend or modify as many articles and as many
8 sections of the Constitution as may be necessary and appropriate in order to accomplish
9 the objectives of the amendment. The approval of the Governor shall not be necessary to
10 any bill, order, resolution or vote of the General Assembly, proposing an amendment or
11 amendments to this Constitution.
12 Section 3. This amendment shall be submitted to the voters of the
13 Commonwealth for their ratification or rejection at the time and in the manner provided
14 for under Sections 256 and 257 of the Constitution, KRS 118.415, and Sections 4 and 5
15 of this Act.
16 Section 4. Notwithstanding any provision of KRS 118.415 to the contrary, the
17 Secretary of State shall cause the entirety of the question in Section 1 of this Act and the
18 entirety of the proposed amendment to the Constitution of Kentucky contained in Section
19 2 of this Act to be published at least one time in a newspaper of general circulation
20 published in this state, and shall also cause to be published at the same time and in the
21 same manner the fact that the amendment will be submitted to the voters for their
22 acceptance or rejection at the next regular election at which members of the General
23 Assembly are to be voted for. The publication required by this section and KRS 118.415
24 shall be made no later than the first Tuesday in August preceding the election at which
25 the amendment is to be voted on.
