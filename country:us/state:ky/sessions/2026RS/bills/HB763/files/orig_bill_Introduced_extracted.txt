18 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 336 IS CREATED TO
19 READ AS FOLLOWS:
20 (1) The Kentucky FLOW Program is hereby established to provide an orderly process
21 for eligible public water or wastewater systems to apply for and receive funds to:
22 (a) Support up to fifty (50) employees of public water or wastewater systems
23 each program year in obtaining a voluntary utility manager accreditation
24 from the Division of Enforcement within the Energy and Environment
25 Cabinet;
26 (b) Support up to fifty (50) new or continuing apprenticeship placements each
27 program year for apprentices in a registered apprenticeship program;

1 (c) Strengthen public water or wastewater system leadership capacity; and
2 (d) Improve public health, protection, and environmental compliance, while
3 safeguarding the Commonwealth's investments in capital water and
4 wastewater infrastructure projects.
5 (2) The cabinet shall administer a reimbursement program for public water or
6 wastewater systems that sponsor employees who enroll in and successfully
7 complete the Kentucky Division of Enforcement's voluntary utility manager
8 accreditation. Reimbursements shall be applied for and disbursed as follows:
9 (a) An eligible public water or wastewater system may receive reimbursement of
10 documented training, tuition, registration, materials, exam costs, and
11 reasonable travel expenses incurred on behalf of an employee completing
12 the accreditation, up to a maximum of two thousand five hundred dollars
13 ($2,500) per employee;
14 (b) A public water or wastewater system shall apply for reimbursement on
15 behalf of an employee and must demonstrate that the employee successfully
16 completed the accreditation requirements;
17 (c) The cabinet shall publish application procedures and may disburse
18 reimbursement upon proof of employee completion and submission of
19 supporting documentation; and
20 (d) The cabinet may reserve up to fifty (50) reimbursement awards per year.
21 (3) (a) The cabinet shall administer a reimbursement program for public water or
22 wastewater systems that sponsor employees who enroll in a registered
23 apprenticeship program for water systems operations specialists or
24 wastewater systems operations specialists.
25 (b) Subject to available funds, the cabinet may award to eligible public water or
26 wastewater systems sponsoring or employing apprentices:
27 1. Wage reimbursement up to fifty percent (50%) of the apprentice's

1 the Kentucky FLOW Program fund. The fund shall consist of moneys received

3 the cabinet, and any federal funds.
4 (2) The fund shall be administered by the cabinet consistent with this chapter and

15 this Act to provide funding for the purposes of the Kentucky FLOW Program.
