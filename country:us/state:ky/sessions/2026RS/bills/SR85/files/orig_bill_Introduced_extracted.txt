3 WHEREAS, Taiwan and the United States of America are long-standing allies that
4 deeply cherish the shared values of democracy, peace, and prosperity. Taiwan continues
5 to pursue integrated diplomacy and the "Three Chains" Strategy, which includes the
6 strategic framework of the "Global Democratic Value Chain", the "First Island Chain in

8 values, a firm alliance, and a flourishing economy serves the best interests of the Indo-
9 Pacific region by strengthening ties with like-minded nations, safeguarding regional
10 security, and leveraging its technological and economic achievements to promote
11 mutually beneficial partnerships; and
12 WHEREAS, 2026 marks both the 250th Anniversary of United States
13 Independence and the 30th Anniversary of Taiwan’s first direct presidential election--
14 milestones that honor liberty and the principle that sovereignty resides in the people. This
15 shared celebration reaffirms Taiwan-U.S. commitment to the rule of law, human rights,
16 and a rules-based international order. Through close cooperation, Taiwan and the United
17 States strengthen democratic values and contribute to Indo-Pacific security, economic
18 resilience, and global peace and stability for future generations; and
19 WHEREAS, the People’s Republic of China has repeatedly engaged in economic

21 United Nations General Assembly Resolution 2758 (XXVI) to obstruct Taiwan’s
22 contributions to the international community, thereby contributing to global challenges to
23 the rules-based international order and enabling aggressive authoritarian expansionism;
24 and
25 WHEREAS, Taiwan is the United States’ seventh-largest trading partner, while the
26 United States is Taiwan’s second-largest, with bilateral trade-in-goods value estimated at

18 interest in pursuing diversified and open investment opportunities in the United States.

20 further engage with their counterparts in Taiwan, to explore, enhance, and deepen
21 substantive relationships in issues that concern the Commonwealth.
