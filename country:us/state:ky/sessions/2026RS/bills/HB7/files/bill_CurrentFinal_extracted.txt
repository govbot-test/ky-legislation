3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 As used in Sections 1 to 6 of this Act:
6 (1) "Camera monitoring system" means a system with one (1) or more camera
7 sensors or computers installed and operated on a school bus that:
8 (a) Produce recorded images; and
9 (b) Record the activation status of the stop arm and the time, date, and location
10 of the school bus when the recorded images are captured;
11 (2) "Owner" has the same meaning as in KRS 186.010;
12 (3) "Recorded images" means two (2) or more photographic images or a segment of
13 any video medium recorded by a camera monitoring system which show on at
14 least one (1) image or portion of video the registration plate number of a motor
15 vehicle being operated in violation of Section 7 of this Act;
16 (4) "School district" means a county school district as described in KRS 160.010 or
17 an independent school district as described in KRS 160.020; and
18 (5) "Stop arm camera violation" means a violation of Section 7 of this Act that is
19 recorded by a camera monitoring system and enforced in accordance with
20 Sections 1 to 6 of this Act.

22 READ AS FOLLOWS:
23 (1) A school district may install and maintain a camera monitoring system on any
24 school bus operated by the school district for the enforcement of a civil penalty
25 against the owner of a motor vehicle for a stop arm camera violation.
26 (2) A school district may contract with a private vendor or manufacturer to install,
27 operate, and maintain camera monitoring systems on school buses operated by

11 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO
12 READ AS FOLLOWS:
13 (1) The amount of a civil penalty for a stop arm camera violation shall be:
14 (a) Three hundred dollars ($300) for the first civil penalty; and
15 (b) Five hundred dollars ($500) for each subsequent civil penalty issued to an
16 owner within a three (3) year period.
17 (2) All revenue generated from a civil penalty for a stop arm camera violation shall
18 be collected and retained by the school district operating the camera monitoring
19 system, unless the penalty is collected as a result of action taken in the Court of
20 Justice, in which case court costs may be deducted from the amount paid to the
21 school district.
22 (3) A law enforcement agency may charge and collect from the school district a fee
23 of twenty-five dollars ($25) from every civil penalty collected by the school district
24 under this section for a violation that was enforced by the law enforcement
25 agency.
26 (4) A stop arm camera violation shall not result in points being assessed against the
27 driving record of the owner or operator of the vehicle in violation.

13 SECTION 5. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO
14 READ AS FOLLOWS:
15 (1) A recorded image produced by a camera monitoring system shall be sufficient
16 evidence of a violation of Section 7 of this Act and shall be admitted without
17 further authentication for the purposes of enforcement. There shall be a
18 rebuttable presumption that when any school bus is stopped with its stop arm
19 activated it is for the purpose of receiving or discharging passengers, and that the
20 school bus signal lights are activated and operational when a recorded image
21 includes an electronic indicator signifying activation of signal lights.
22 (2) In a contest to a stop arm camera violation, liability shall be determined by a
23 preponderance of the evidence and it shall be a defense that:
24 (a) The motor vehicle or the motor vehicle registration plates were stolen before
25 the violation occurred and were not under the control or possession of the
26 owner at the time of the violation;
27 (b) The civil penalty is not enforceable because at the time and location of the
Page 4 of 13

UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS HB 7/EN
1 violation the stop arm was not activated;
2 (c) The owner was not operating the vehicle at the time of the violation. An
3 owner who raises this defense shall identify, under penalty of perjury, the
4 person who was operating the vehicle at the time of the violation, including
5 at a minimum the operator's name and address;
6 (d) The person operating the motor vehicle received a citation from a law
7 enforcement officer for a violation of Section 7 of this Act at the date and
8 approximate time listed on the stop arm camera violation form;
9 (e) The violation was necessary to allow the passage of an emergency vehicle;
10 or
11 (f) The violation occurred while the operator was participating in a funeral
12 procession.
13 (3) A contest to a stop arm camera violation shall be heard by the District Court, and
14 the school district and applicable law enforcement agency shall defend the stop
15 arm camera violation.
16 (4) A school district may contract for legal services to fulfill the obligations in
17 subsection (3) of this section.
