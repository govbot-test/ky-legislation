CHAPTER 62 1
CHAPTER 62
( HB 7 )
AN ACT relating to school bus safety.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
As used in Sections 1 to 6 of this Act:
(1) "Camera monitoring system" means a system with one (1) or more camera sensors or computers installed
and operated on a school bus that:
(a) Produce recorded images; and
(b) Record the activation status of the stop arm and the time, date, and location of the school bus when
the recorded images are captured;
(2) "Owner" has the same meaning as in KRS 186.010;
(3) "Recorded images" means two (2) or more photographic images or a segment of any video medium
recorded by a camera monitoring system which show on at least one (1) image or portion of video the
registration plate number of a motor vehicle being operated in violation of Section 7 of this Act;
(4) "School district" means a county school district as described in KRS 160.010 or an independent school
district as described in KRS 160.020; and
(5) "Stop arm camera violation" means a violation of Section 7 of this Act that is recorded by a camera
monitoring system and enforced in accordance with Sections 1 to 6 of this Act.
SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) A school district may install and maintain a camera monitoring system on any school bus operated by the
school district for the enforcement of a civil penalty against the owner of a motor vehicle for a stop arm
camera violation.
(2) A school district may contract with a private vendor or manufacturer to install, operate, and maintain
camera monitoring systems on school buses operated by the school district.
(3) A stop arm camera violation may be enforced by a law enforcement agency, a certified peace officer, or a
school resource officer as defined in KRS 158.441, upon review of the recorded images produced by a
camera monitoring system.
(4) A school bus with a camera monitoring system installed and maintained in accordance with this section
shall display a warning sign notifying the public of the camera monitoring system.
(5) Recorded images and other records related to a specific stop arm camera violation are private information
and shall only be accessible to authorized personnel for the purpose of enforcing stop arm camera
violations.
SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) The amount of a civil penalty for a stop arm camera violation shall be:
(a) Three hundred dollars ($300) for the first civil penalty; and
(b) Five hundred dollars ($500) for each subsequent civil penalty issued to an owner within a three (3)
year period.
(2) All revenue generated from a civil penalty for a stop arm camera violation shall be collected and retained by
the school district operating the camera monitoring system, unless the penalty is collected as a result of
action taken in the Court of Justice, in which case court costs may be deducted from the amount paid to the
school district.

(3) A law enforcement agency may charge and collect from the school district a fee of twenty-five dollars ($25)
from every civil penalty collected by the school district under this section for a violation that was enforced
by the law enforcement agency.
(4) A stop arm camera violation shall not result in points being assessed against the driving record of the owner

SECTION 4. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) Upon a determination to issue a stop arm camera violation notice, the following documents shall be sent,
either by the law enforcement agency or the school district's agent, by first class mail to the owner of a
motor vehicle within thirty (30) days of a stop arm camera violation:
(a) The stop arm camera violation notice form as described in subsection (2) of this section;
(b) A copy of the recorded images for the stop arm camera violation; and
(c) A signed, sworn statement from a law enforcement officer that, based on inspection of the recorded
images, the motor vehicle was being operated in violation of Section 7 of this Act. This statement may
be signed electronically and is admissible in any proceeding challenging a stop arm camera violation.
(2) The stop arm camera violation notice form shall contain in substance the following information:
(a) The name and address of the registered owner of the vehicle;
(b) A statement that the notice represents a determination that a stop arm camera violation has been
committed by the owner of the vehicle and that the determination shall be final unless contested in
accordance with Section 5 of this Act;
(c) The date and time of the violation;
(d) The location of the violation;
(e) The amount of the civil penalty imposed and the date by which the civil penalty shall be paid;
(f) Instructions on how to pay the civil penalty to the school district or its designated agent;
(g) Information advising the owner as to the manner and time in which the civil penalty may be
contested; and
(h) A warning that failure to pay the civil penalty imposed or to contest the matter in a timely manner is
an admission of liability and shall result in the suspension of the motor vehicle's registration.
(3) A recorded image produced by a camera monitoring system shall be destroyed:
(a) No later than thirty-one (31) days from the date the recorded image is captured if the recorded image
does not result in a stop arm camera violation notice form issued under this section; or
(b) Upon final disposition of the stop arm camera violation if the recorded image results in a stop arm
camera violation notice form issued under this section.
SECTION 5. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) A recorded image produced by a camera monitoring system shall be sufficient evidence of a violation of
Section 7 of this Act and shall be admitted without further authentication for the purposes of enforcement.
There shall be a rebuttable presumption that when any school bus is stopped with its stop arm activated it is
for the purpose of receiving or discharging passengers, and that the school bus signal lights are activated
and operational when a recorded image includes an electronic indicator signifying activation of signal
lights.
(2) In a contest to a stop arm camera violation, liability shall be determined by a preponderance of the evidence
and it shall be a defense that:
(a) The motor vehicle or the motor vehicle registration plates were stolen before the violation occurred
and were not under the control or possession of the owner at the time of the violation;
(b) The civil penalty is not enforceable because at the time and location of the violation the stop arm was
not activated;

(c) The owner was not operating the vehicle at the time of the violation. An owner who raises this
defense shall identify, under penalty of perjury, the person who was operating the vehicle at the time
of the violation, including at a minimum the operator's name and address;
(d) The person operating the motor vehicle received a citation from a law enforcement officer for a
violation of Section 7 of this Act at the date and approximate time listed on the stop arm camera
violation form;
(e) The violation was necessary to allow the passage of an emergency vehicle; or
(f) The violation occurred while the operator was participating in a funeral procession.
(3) A contest to a stop arm camera violation shall be heard by the District Court, and the school district and
applicable law enforcement agency shall defend the stop arm camera violation.
(4) A school district may contract for legal services to fulfill the obligations in subsection (3) of this section.
SECTION 6. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 158 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
(1) If a stop arm camera violation civil penalty is not paid or contested in accordance with Section 5 of this Act
within sixty (60) days, the applicable law enforcement agency or officer may notify the Transportation
Cabinet of the nonpayment.
(2) Upon notice of nonpayment, the cabinet shall:
(a) Send a notice of suspension by certified mail with restricted delivery and return receipt; and
(b) Suspend the registration of the motor vehicle until the civil penalty is paid.
(3) The applicable law enforcement agency or officer shall notify the cabinet of the need to release a
suspension levied under this section within one (1) business day of collecting the funds to satisfy the civil
penalty.
