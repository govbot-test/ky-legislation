UNOFFICIAL COPY 26 RS BR 189
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 164.2951 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) [Beginning with the 2012-2013 academic year and each academic year thereafter
5 ]For first-time students enrolling in a public college or university, postsecondary
6 education institutions are encouraged to limit the credit-hour requirements to sixty
7 (60) credit hours for each associate of science or associate of arts degree program
8 and to one hundred twenty (120) credit hours for each bachelor of arts or bachelor
9 of science degree program, except in situations in which:
10 (a) Quality and content of a program would be negatively impacted; or
11 (b) A program must comply with specific program standards established by
12 external accreditation bodies.
13 (2) The council[ on Postsecondary Education], in collaboration with the public
14 universities and the Kentucky Community and Technical College
15 System[community and technical colleges], shall:
16 (a) Facilitate the development and implementation of a statewide agreement for
17 alignment of Kentucky Community and Technical College System lower-
18 division associate of arts and associate of science coursework that shall be
19 accepted and fully credited to related bachelors degree programs by all public
20 universities. The agreement shall specify the general education learning
21 outcomes and program-specific prerequisite learning outcomes of the
22 coursework. Where applicable, curricula shall be reviewed to determine
23 comparability of core content standards required under KRS 164.302. The
24 agreement shall direct that the associate of arts and associate of science
25 coursework meeting the learning outcomes specified shall be accepted for
26 transfer and degree credit, whether earned as individual courses or within
27 block programs;

18 hours that shall be available for transfer and application toward
