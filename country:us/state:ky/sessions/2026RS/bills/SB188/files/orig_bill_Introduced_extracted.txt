1 AN ACT relating to railroad safety.
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 174 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) The Transportation Cabinet shall establish a state rail safety inspection program.
6 The cabinet may appoint and hire state rail safety inspectors and other personnel
7 as necessary to carry out the program.
8 (2) The cabinet shall apply to and enter into agreements with the Federal Railroad
9 Administration to participate in the federal State Safety Participation Program to
10 train and certify inspectors under 49 U.S.C. secs. 20103, 20105, 20106, and
11 20113, and 49 C.F.R. pt. 212.
12 (3) A state rail safety inspector may:
13 (a)_ Inspect mainline track, secondary track, and yard and industry track;
14 (b) Inspect railroad right-of-way, including adjacent or intersecting drainage,
15 culverts, bridges, overhead structures, and traffic and other public
16 crossings;
17 (c) Inspect yards and physical plants;
18 (d) Inspect train equipment;
19 (e) Inspect railroad operations;
20 (f) Inspect railroad-highway grade crossings;
21 (g) Inspect railroad signal and train control systems;
22 (h) Review and enforce safety requirements;
23 (i) Review maintenance and repair records; and
24 (j) Review railroad security measures.
25 (4) A state rail safety inspector may perform but is not limited to the duties described
26 in the federal State Safety Participation Program. An inspector may train, be
27 certified, and participate in any of the federal State Safety Participation Program

22 three (3) nominees each supplied by:
23 1. The Kentucky Railroad Association;
24 2. A major railroad operating in Kentucky; and
25 3. An organized rail labor organization.
26 In appointing members under subparagraph 1. of this paragraph, the
27 Governor shall give consideration to including two (2) representatives from
